## Key Dynamics
- **APP's record financials masked by sentiment-driven selloff.** FY2025 revenue $5.5B (+70%), Q4 EBITDA margin 84%, $4B FCF — yet stock fell 41% YTD by Feb 2026. CloudX launch and SEC probe triggered sentiment collapse on best-ever quarter. >60% gaming mediation share + Axon 2.0 expansion into e-commerce/CTV signal TAM broadening. *(Source: [[Research/2026-02-26 - APP - AppLovin AI Ad Platform Deep Dive]])*
- **DUOL's 83% drawdown is mispricing deliberate deceleration as permanent impairment.** Stock at ~$93 (from ATH $540) after management guided 10-12% bookings growth for 2026 (vs 33% in FY2025). FY2025 delivered $1.04B revenue (+39%), 52.7M DAU, 73% gross margins, 37% FCF margins. Core thesis: Duolingo is a behavioral architecture company (not a language app) whose habit-formation moat transfers across subjects — chess already fastest-growing subject ever. 10.5x trailing P/E on a 44% revenue CAGR-since-IPO business. Key catalyst: Q1 2026 earnings (May). *(Source: [[Theses/DUOL - Duolingo]])*

- **KAMBI's modular pivot challenges the vertical integration death thesis.** FY2025 revenue €162M (-8.2% YoY, -1.2% ex-transition fees), adj. EBITA €15.5M (-39%) — headline deterioration masks strategic progress. FDJ UNITED (which acquired Kindred for €2.45B) returned to Kambi for Odds Feed+ after building in-house — the "boomerang" validating B2B sportsbook resilience. AI division Tzeract now prices ~50% of all bets; 2026 World Cup to be first fully AI-traded tournament. Customer concentration halved (top-3 from 60%+ to 35%), 15 new partnerships since Q4 2025, Hard Rock Digital signed Odds Feed+ for 8 US states. Nevada license secured, OLG Ontario launched. 2026 guidance: €20-25M adj. EBITA. Key risk: Sportradar ORAKO competitive encirclement. *(Source: [[Theses/KAMBI - Kambi Group]])*

- **NFLX is the only structurally profitable pure-play streamer, and the profitability trajectory — not subscriber growth — is the real story.** FY2025: $45.2B revenue (+16%), 29.5% operating margin, $11B net income, ~$8B FCF. FY2026 guide: $50.7-51.7B revenue, 31.5% operating margin, ~$11B FCF. Operating margin expanded ~1,000bps in three years while content spend grew from $13B to $18B. Three-engine monetization: subscriptions (pricing power proven with 30-35% cumulative increases, sub-3% churn impact, Premium now $26.99), advertising ($1.5B FY2025, targeting $3B FY2026, $9B by 2030; 190M MAVs on ad tier, only 45% fill rate; in-house ad tech launched April 2025), and live events (WWE Raw $5B/10yr, NFL, boxing). International content moat across 50+ countries with 14x cost efficiency on non-English hits (Squid Game vs Stranger Things); 70% of 325M subs outside North America. WBD acquisition walked away Feb 2026 ($2.8B breakup fee), resumed $8B buyback authorization — capital discipline over empire-building. Key competitive threats: YouTube (9.7% vs Netflix 7.6% US TV viewership), Disney bundle (26% combined US share). Stock at ~$106 post 10-for-1 split (~$447B market cap, ~32x forward P/E). Q1 2026 earnings April 17. Conviction medium — promote if ad business inflects toward $5B+ or margins continue toward 35%. *(Source: [[Theses/NFLX - Netflix]], updated Apr 15, 2026)*

- **META is about to dethrone Google as #1 in global digital advertising while trading at the cheapest Mag 7 multiple.** FY2025 revenue $201B (+22.2%), operating margin 41.4%, FCF $46.1B — projected to reach $243.5B in 2026 ad revenue (26.8% market share) vs Google's $239.5B (26.4%), the first leadership change in digital advertising history. Advantage+/Andromeda AI ad platform delivering 30% higher conversion rates; Llama 5 released April 8, 2026 with 700M+ downloads commoditizing the AI model layer ("commoditize the complement" strategy). At ~$635 (~21x forward P/E), the market prices capex risk ($115-135B in 2026, ~2x FY2025) rather than the structural advertising leadership shift. Reality Labs $17B+/yr losses ($73B+ cumulative) remain the capital allocation drag. Key catalyst: Q1 2026 earnings April 29 + capex plateau signal. *(Source: [[Theses/META - Meta]], updated Apr 15, 2026)*

- **OPEN is a venture-capital-style option bet on the rarest business model transition in real estate: asset-heavy trader to asset-light software platform.** Last scaled iBuyer standing (~67% share) after Zillow/Redfin exits. Under Nejatian (ex-Shopify COO) and Rabois (co-founder/Chairman, $40M personal investment), "Opendoor 2.0" is deploying AI pricing ("Mainframe"), computer vision assessments, and agent partnerships (eXp 87K agents, Zillow, Key Connections) to transform from house-flipper to real estate operating system. FY2025 rev $4,371M (-15%), adj. net loss $62M, Q4 FCF +$67M. Targeting adj. net income breakeven by end 2026 — would be first iBuyer ever to prove sustainable profitability. Stock ~$4.93 trades 3x above analyst median $1.60 (consensus Sell). Housing market hostile: March 2026 existing home sales 9-month low, mortgage rates ~6.5% (Iran conflict), NAR forecast revised from +14% to +4%. The "Exclusives" marketplace (targeting 40% of volume by end 2026) is the linchpin — if capital-light revenue scales, valuation framework shifts from trading company (0.5-1.5x rev) to platform (3-5x+). Low conviction: qualitative transformation genuine, fundamentals unproven. *(Source: [[Theses/OPEN - Opendoor]], updated Apr 15, 2026)*

- **Reliance's conglomerate-to-platform transformation is the most consequential business model transition in Indian equity markets.** The market still prices RELIANCE as a refining conglomerate with a telco attached — energy-desk analysts apply conglomerate-discount frameworks while Jio Platforms (515.3M subs, 51.8% EBITDA margins, $130-180B IPO valuation, Meta JV, 485 5G/6G patents) would attract platform-premium multiples as a standalone. JioMart's contribution-margin-positive unit economics at 1.6M daily orders (+200% YoY) represent a structural advantage in India's $11.5B quick-commerce market — the only profitable player at scale, enabled by 20,000 existing stores functioning as dual-use fulfillment centers. The Jio-Starlink distribution deal (March 2025) reveals the platform strategy: rather than building satellite infrastructure, Jio becomes the distribution layer capturing margin from upstream technology providers. At ₹1,220 (~$210B market cap), the SOTP implies near-zero value for Retail, O2C, New Energy, and Financial Services after subtracting Jio's standalone valuation — mathematically implausible given Retail's ₹3.3L cr annual revenue and Samsung C&T's $3B green ammonia offtake. IPO (Q1 FY2027, 17 banks) is the catalyst for value crystallization. 33/36 analyst Buy ratings with +23-41% implied upside. Conviction medium. *(Source: [[Theses/RELIANCE - Reliance Industries]], updated Apr 15, 2026)*

- **SPOT's business model transition from music distribution pipe to audio platform with supply-side marketplace economics is the most underappreciated structural shift in consumer digital.** FY2025: ~€17.7B revenue (+13%), ~€2.5B operating income (+50%+), €2.9B FCF (17% cash margin), 33.1% Q4 gross margin (+640bps in two years). 751M MAU / 290M Premium subscribers (Q4 2025). The market prices Spotify as a subscription streaming service paying ~70% to labels — the non-consensus view is that Spotify has built a supply-side platform tax (Discovery Mode 30% royalty commission at <5% artist participation generating $150-225M annually, Campaign Kit marketplace tools, 1,000-stream threshold redistributing $47M) that is structurally analogous to Google extracting search advertising from the supply side. The audiobook bundling reclassification saved €205-256M annually in mechanical royalties; 2025 direct deals with all three majors partially reversed savings (40-60%) but Premium gross margin held flat at 34.8% — proving margin durability. Co-CEO structure (Norström commercial / Soderström product-tech, Ek as Executive Chairman) effective January 2026; Norström targeting ~1B subscribers. Audio commands 20% of media time but only 2.9% of digital ad spend — SAX launched April 2025, 44% programmatic penetration, 222% advertiser growth, but Q4 ad revenue declined 4%. AI DJ at 90M users / 4B+ hours; label AI partnerships with all three majors + Merlin + Believe (October 2025). Music Pro tier at +$5.99/month (6% adoption) provides ARPU expansion lever. Stock ~$510 (~$105B market cap, ~37x forward P/E, compressed from ~50x). Key risks: MLC lawsuit (€256M+ exposure, amended "price manipulation" theory), Phonorecords V CRB (2028-2032), YouTube Music competitive threat (fastest-growing, 13% YoY US). Q1 2026 earnings April 29 — guided at ~15% revenue growth (acceleration). Conviction medium — promote if SAX ad revenue inflects and MLC resolves favourably. *(Source: [[Theses/SPOT - Spotify]], updated Apr 15, 2026)*

- **TTWO is a binary-outcome bet on whether GTA VI transforms Take-Two from a cyclical game publisher into a platform operator — and EA's $55B take-private makes it the last independent large-cap gaming play.** At ~$206 (~$36.5B market cap), the stock embeds moderate GTA VI success (FY2027E EPS $7.50+ vs $1.40 FY2026) but does not price the full platform optionality: the UGC creator marketplace (Project ROME, Cfx.re acquisition, Roblox/Fortnite-style creator payouts) could shift Rockstar from sole content provider to platform tax collector. Q3 FY2026 net bookings $1.76B (+28% YoY), FY2026 guidance raised to $6.65-6.7B. Zynga mobile (~45% of bookings) provides counter-cyclical cash flow; Toon Blast $3B+ lifetime (+43% YoY). The RDR2 lesson is definitive: identical Metacritic (97) to GTA V but ~half the revenue because online monetization failed — proving unit sales alone cannot justify current valuation. Risks: organizational turmoil (34 firings, UK union tribunal, morale "rock bottom"), Dan Houser departure, 30fps-locked console, P2P networking. EA going private (~June 2026) creates scarcity premium for gaming exposure, and the $55B EA deal anchors sector takeout expectations. GTA VI launches Nov 19, 2026. Conviction medium — promote if UGC marketplace details confirm platform economics. *(Source: [[Theses/TTWO - Take-Two Interactive]], updated Apr 15, 2026)*

- **UBER is a capital-light platform monopoly disguised as a rideshare company, completing a business model transition the market hasn't re-underwritten.** At ~$72 (~$150B market cap, ~21x forward P/E), the stock is down ~30% from October 2025 highs despite record operations: $10B annual FCF, 20%+ gross bookings growth, expanding margins in every segment. The market prices Uber as a mature utility (PEG <1.0) rather than an accelerating multi-sided platform with compounding network effects. Five non-consensus insights: (1) the $2B+ advertising business (growing 50%+ YoY) is near-pure margin that raises blended take rates — the Amazon ad-transformation playbook; (2) the AV strategy (25+ partnerships, 10+ cities, Rivian $1.25B deal for 50K exclusive robotaxis, WeRide Dubai commercial launch, VW/MOIA LA testing) positions Uber as the "Android of autonomy" — the platform wins regardless of which AV technology succeeds; (3) 46M Uber One members represent behavioral lock-in (4x spend, approaching 50% of gross bookings) that is structurally different from a discount program; (4) Khosrowshahi's cultural turnaround from "growth at all costs" to disciplined capital allocation is a durable competitive advantage invisible in financial screens (84% developer AI adoption rate compounds operating leverage); (5) investor anchoring bias from the cash-burning IPO era creates a persistent discount that the buy side has not yet re-underwritten ($20B buyback, >50% FCF committed to repurchases). Key risks: Waymo expanding to 20+ cities independently (including London), FTC Uber One billing complaint (21 states), CA AB 1340 gig worker unionisation, Tesla Cybercab production April 2026, DoorDash+Deliveroo integration "ahead of expectations." Q1 2026 earnings May 6. Conviction high. *(Source: [[Theses/UBER - Uber]], updated Apr 15, 2026)*

- **MTN's governance architecture problem is the purest case study in public-vs-private competitive asymmetry.** Vail Resorts' ~$130 stock ($4.6B market cap, 6.9% dividend yield) reflects a company structurally unable to match Alterra Mountain Company's $400M+ annual guest-facing capex — a 4x per-resort spending gap that compounds every season. FY2026 catastrophic: Rockies snowfall at 30-year lows, skier visits down 20% through Jan 4, EBITDA guidance slashed from $842-898M to $745-775M. Epic Pass units declining for 2nd consecutive year (~2.3M holders). Rob Katz returned as CEO (May 2025) with incremental tactics (20% under-30 discount, lift ticket aggression) applied to a structural supply-side problem. The impossible trinity: Vail cannot simultaneously maintain $320M annual dividends, close the capex gap, and lower prices to attract younger demographics. Climate change accelerating: US ski seasons shortened 5.5-7.1 days, projections 27-62 days shorter by 2050s. Low conviction (avoid). *(Source: [[Theses/MTN - Vail Resorts]], updated Apr 15, 2026)*
