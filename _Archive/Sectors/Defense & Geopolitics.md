## Key Dynamics
- **PLTR is the uncontested software platform for Western defense AI** — Palantir holds DoD IL6 provisional authorization (one of six cloud providers), FedRAMP High, CMMC Level 2, and 20+ years of classified operational embedding that no competitor can replicate. The $10B Army Enterprise Agreement (July 2025) consolidated 75 legacy contracts into a single integration — this is structural, not contractual. Maven Smart System ($1.275B ceiling, 20K+ users across 35+ military entities) is now a core military system with multi-year Pentagon funding. NATO adopted Maven in "one of the most expeditious" acquisitions in its history. ShipOS ($448M) demonstrated 160 hrs → 10 min for submarine scheduling at General Dynamics Electric Boat. TITAN made Palantir the first software company to serve as primary contractor on a major hardware program. UK MoD awarded £240.6M without competitive tender. The NVIDIA Sovereign AI OS Reference Architecture (Blackwell Ultra + Rubix + Apollo + AIP) addresses the sovereign/air-gapped AI market that hyperscalers structurally cannot serve. DOGE's 8% Pentagon cuts paradoxically benefit Palantir — budget scrutiny eliminates legacy contractors while a record $1.5T 2027 defense budget request funds AI-native platforms. Re-industrialization narrative is durable and bipartisan: Warp Speed (L3Harris, GE Aerospace, Boeing), ShipOS (U.S. Navy), NOS (nuclear), creating revenue decoupled from commercial SaaS cycles. *(Source: [[Theses/PLTR - Palantir]], updated Apr 15, 2026)*
- **June 2025 Israel-Iran conflict (12 days) ended in fragile ceasefire, which collapsed into the 2026 Hormuz crisis now entering a dual-blockade phase.** The June 2025 ceasefire was always fragile — Iran's nuclear program was set back months, not destroyed, and the shadow war (cyber, assassinations) continued. The February 28, 2026 US-Israeli strikes (Operation Epic Fury) killed Supreme Leader Khamenei and triggered the IRGC's closure of Hormuz. After an April 8 ceasefire that Iran agreed to but complicated with $1M+ transit tolls, peace talks collapsed on April 12 over Iran's enriched uranium. Trump then imposed a US naval counter-blockade on Iranian ports. As of April 14, no ships have passed the US blockade; 600-800+ vessels are stranded in the Gulf. The ceasefire expires April 21. The key investment implication: the insurance mechanism that controls modern maritime flows means even post-resolution normalization takes months — systematically underpriced by equity markets anchoring to crude oil headlines. *(Sources: [[Research/2025-07-03 - Macro - Iran-Israel Ceasefire Tensions and Geopolitical Risk]], [[Iran War Macroeconomic Scenario]], web research April 2026)*
- **India-Pakistan defense investment is asymmetric.** India has 14+ publicly listed defense contractors (BEL, HAL, BDL, etc.); Pakistan has zero listed defense companies — entire sector is state-owned. Aatmanirbhar Bharat policy structurally favors Indian defense equities. *(Source: [[Research/2025-05-10 - Macro - Indian vs Pakistani Defence Stocks]])*
