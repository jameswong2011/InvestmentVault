# Australian Healthcare Non-Traditional Models

## Original Query
> Provide a list of Australian consumer / healthcare listed companies that have a non traditional business model.

---

Note: You should verify current listing status and business models, as company situations can change.

Here's a list of Australian listed companies in consumer/healthcare with non-traditional business models:

1. **Healthia (HLA)** - Platform/aggregator model for allied health services (podiatry, physiotherapy, etc.) rather than traditional single-clinic ownership

2. **MedAdvisor (MDR)** - Digital medication management and pharmacy engagement platform connecting patients, pharmacies, and pharmaceutical companies

3. **Monash IVF (MVF)** - Consolidated IVF services model with network of clinics, moving away from individual practitioner model

4. **Clinuvel Pharmaceuticals (CUV)** - Ultra-rare disease specialist with unique "ultra-orphan" drug development model focusing on extremely small patient populations

5. **Skin Elements (SKN)** - Direct-to-consumer skincare with focus on natural/toxin-free products bypassing traditional retail

6. **Kogan.com (KGN)** - Pure-play online retailer with private label focus, no physical stores

7. **Temple & Webster (TPW)** - Online-only furniture and homewares marketplace connecting suppliers directly to consumers

8. **Life360 (360)** - Freemium family safety app with location sharing and subscription tiers

9. **Silk Laser Australia (SLA)** - Vertically integrated cosmetic and laser treatment clinics with membership model

10. **Adore Beauty (ABY)** - Online-only beauty retailer with content-driven commerce model

These companies distinguish themselves through digital-first approaches, platform/aggregator models, direct-to-consumer channels, or subscription-based services that differ from traditional brick-and-mortar or fee-for-service models.
