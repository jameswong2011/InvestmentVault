## 1. Moats Are Wider Than Consensus Credits

The market systematically underestimates the durability of moats that are invisible on a financial statement.

- **[[Theses/LITE - Lumentum]]**: The butt-joint regrowth step in EML laser manufacturing is the most underappreciated barrier to entry in the semiconductor industry — growing two atomically different crystal structures with sub-100nm alignment requires decades of empirical learning that no capital expenditure can shortcut. Coherent buys EMLs FROM Lumentum despite operating the world's first 6-inch InP fab with yields exceeding legacy 3-inch lines — wafer diameter confers a cost advantage, but epitaxial process mastery confers the performance advantage that matters at 200G speeds. Failure rates scale with the 4th power of bandwidth, meaning 200G is ~16x harder than 100G in yield terms and the gap widens at each speed generation. Chinese transceivers (Innolight, $5.3B revenue, 40% global share, 70%+ collective Chinese market share) are simultaneously Lumentum's largest customers AND competitors, structurally dependent on its EML components while competing at the module level — component-level pricing power persists (double-digit price increases in 2026) even as module ASPs collapse 40-50%. The SiPh foundry buildout (GFS/AMF, Tower 3x, TSMC COUPE) is paradoxically bullish: every SiPh chip needs an external InP laser, increasing demand for the Lumentum/Coherent/Broadcom oligopoly. NVIDIA's $2B investment includes capacity lock-out rights, not just a supplier relationship. OCS (MEMS, millisecond switching, data-rate agnostic) market revised from $500M to $1.5B for 2026. *(Updated Apr 15, 2026)*

- **[[Theses/CSGP - CoStar Group]]**: The CRE data business is a "hostage monopoly" — customers stay because no alternative exists, not because they're satisfied. The defining customer quote: *"If there was a viable alternative we would have gladly pursued it and never looked back, but CoStar knew this."* Homes.com traffic overstated by bundling with Apartments.com (standalone likely 50-70M vs reported 108M network); Goldman confirmed 8% YoY traffic moderation in Feb 2026. At ~$37 (~4.7x P/S vs 5yr avg ~14x), the commercial franchise alone (43-47% EBITDA margins, record $308M bookings) may justify half the market cap. Third Point exited entirely (Apr 10, 2026) after governance changes were implemented — proxy overhang removed. Legal aggression (Xceligent bankruptcy, Crexi lawsuit, Zillow photo suit) is itself a moat layer. Matterport's forced Zillow exclusivity creates differentiated content but risks market fragmentation. Investment phase ending: net Homes.com spend drops $300M+ in 2026; 2026 EBITDA guided +77% to $740-800M. *(Sources: [[Research/2026-03-26 - CSGP - CoStar Group Investment Evaluation]], web research Apr 2026)*

Layer 1: **International content production across 50+ countries** creates a 14x cost-per-viewer advantage (Squid Game $0.01/viewer/episode vs Stranger Things $0.14) that no competitor has attempted to replicate — Amazon Prime Video's catalog is 85% English vs Netflix's 62%, and Netflix is responsible for 8 of the top 20 most in-demand non-English originals in the US. Layer 2: **1.8% monthly churn** (vs industry avg 5.5%) in an era where 23% of US streaming subscribers are "serial churners" driving 42% of all cancellations — Netflix is structurally immune because it has achieved "Evergreen" status through daily habit formation, not event-driven viewing. Layer 3: **Empirically proven pricing power** — two price increases in two years (cumulative 30–35%, Premium now $26.99) with sub-3% churn impact, validated by JPMorgan/Citi estimates of $1.7B incremental annualized revenue. The advertising engine ($1.5B in FY2025, targeting $3B FY2026, $9B by 2030) with only 45% fill rate adds a margin-accretive second curve that didn't exist 30 months ago. The WBD walkaway ($2.8B breakup fee, resumed $8B buyback authorization) is the strongest capital discipline signal this management team has sent. At ~$106 post-split (~$447B market cap, ~32x forward P/E), the stock prices execution, but the three-engine model (subscriptions + ads + live events), 2030 targets ($80B revenue, ~38.5% op margin), and $11B FY2026 FCF guidance suggest the profitability trajectory — not subscriber growth — is the real underappreciated story. Conviction medium. *(Updated Apr 15, 2026)*

## 2. Markets Mispricing Duration of Physical Supply Shocks

Futures markets and equity valuations systematically underestimate how long physical infrastructure damage takes to repair.

- **[[Theses/STNG - Scorpio Tankers]]**: The market treats STNG as an "oil trade" — a bet on crude staying above $100 — when it is actually a "diesel logistics infrastructure trade" where product spreads can widen even if crude stabilises. Diesel cracks at $44-50/bbl (5-year avg ~$28/bbl) and jet fuel cracks peaking near $100/bbl demonstrate that refined products are moving more than crude because no pipeline bypass exists for products at Hormuz (unlike crude's 9-10M bbl/day bypass capacity). The insurance mechanism — not navies — controls modern maritime flows: war risk surged from 0.125% to 5%+ of hull value, and even post-ceasefire, normalisation takes months (Iran charging $1M+ tolls, 600-800+ vessels stranded, July earliest normalisation). STNG's TCE underperformance paradox (3-10% lower rates than TORM/Hafnia) creates a structural P/Book discount (1.05x vs peers 1.3-1.5x) that inverts during rate spikes — when LR2s earn $80-120k/day, the 5% gap is noise. Q1 2026 TCEs already at $32-51k/day with Q2 LR2 bookings at $101k/day. The April 12 peace talk collapse and subsequent US counter-blockade have escalated the situation to a dual-blockade regime, with the ceasefire expiring April 21. Hafnia-TORM consolidation (13.97% stake, Dec 2025) signals industry repricing that benefits STNG as either consolidator or premium target. *(Updated Apr 15, 2026)*

- **[[Macro/Iran War Macroeconomic Scenario]]**: Dubai physical crude at $138–140/bbl vs Brent December 2026 futures at ~$80 — the physical-futures disconnect is the clearest signal of consensus error. - **[[Theses/LNG - Cheniere Energy]]**: The toll-road contract model is the most misunderstood business model in energy — ~95% take-or-pay SPAs with $120B+ contracted revenue through 2050. Brownfield cost advantage widening ($6.50-7.00/MMBtu vs $8.50-10.80 greenfield) while competitors face cascading execution risk (Venture Global arbitrations, Golden Pass contractor bankruptcy, Tellurian sold at 90% loss). The April 2026 ceasefire creates a "normalization lag" that equity markets systematically underestimate: insurance repricing takes months, Iran charging $1M+ tolls per Hormuz transit, and LNG has no pipeline bypass unlike crude's 3.5-5.5 mb/d alternatives. ## 3. AI Capex: The Timing Mismatch No One Wants to Price

The AI investment cycle differs from past manias in generating real revenue, but the return-on-capital timeline is longer than investors are pricing.

- **[[Macro/AI Bubble Risk and Semiconductor Valuations]]**: J.P. Morgan calculates that delivering 10% returns on AI investments through 2030 requires $650 billion in annual AI revenue "into perpetuity." Only ~3% of AI users pay for premium services — a significant monetization gap. Open source is at just 13% of enterprise workloads despite capability parity, because deployment complexity and compliance concerns dominate procurement decisions.

- **[[Theses/META - Meta]]**: The market frames Meta's AI capex as "spending without visible monetization" — but the data contradicts this narrative. Advantage+/Andromeda delivered $60B+ run rate by Q3 2025 (3x faster monetization than OpenAI) and AI-enhanced video ads produce 30% higher conversion rates. FY2025: $201B revenue (+22.2%), $46.1B FCF, 41.4% operating margin. 2026 capex guidance of $115-135B (~2x FY2025) is the real controversy — but management guided 2026 operating income above 2025, and Meta is projected to overtake Google as #1 in digital advertising ($243.5B vs $239.5B). The open-source Llama strategy (700M+ downloads, Llama 5 matching GPT-5) commoditizes the AI model layer to Meta's benefit — if AI models are free, value accretes to distribution (3.54B DAP) and data (ad targeting), which Meta owns. Ray-Ban smart glasses (7M+ units sold in 2025, targeting 10M in 2026) and Meta AI (1B MAU) represent embedded option values priced at ~zero in a stock trading at 21x forward. The capex narrative is a multiple problem, not an earnings problem — a capex plateau signal in 2027 guidance could trigger sharp re-rating. *(Updated Apr 15, 2026)*

- **[[Theses/285A - Kioxia]]**: NAND inventor with strongest BiCS/CBA technology but Bain Capital ~51% overhang. Forward P/E ~7.3x if earnings estimates hold, but trailing ~70x. ## 11. Surgical / MedTech: Digital Ecosystems > Hardware Monopolies

The era of hardware exclusivity in surgical robotics is ending, but the winners are building data moats that matter more. **ISRG thesis now active at medium conviction.**

- **[[Theses/ISRG - Intuitive Surgical]]**: ISRG has completed the rarest transition in medtech — from hardware monopolist to surgical intelligence platform. The market still categorizes it as a "premium medical device company," but 83-85% recurring revenue, OTA software updates, usage-based instrument pricing ($1,400/procedure), and the emerging Case Insights AI platform make the business structurally closer to enterprise SaaS embedded in the operating room. Competitors (Hugo urology-only, Ottava not yet cleared anywhere) are replicating yesterday's moat while ISRG builds tomorrow's: the cross-procedure compounding effect (six specialties, 85 instruments, 90,000 trained surgeons, 25-year regulatory predicate chain) is self-reinforcing and requires 5-10+ years of sequential FDA expansions for any competitor to approach. The data flywheel (20M+ patients, 3.15M procedures in FY2025) is two orders of magnitude ahead of any competitor. At ~44x forward (vs 70x+ historical peaks), the stock is at its cheapest valuation in years while the business accelerates through the dV5 cycle. *(Updated Apr 15, 2026)*

- **China bifurcation is a pricing power signal, not an existential threat.** Domestic Chinese robots (MicroPort, Edge Medical, KangDuo) are winning on unit volume in Tier 2/3 hospitals at 30-60% price discounts, but ISRG retains 72.7% of China market by procurement value. No Chinese endoscopic robot has FDA clearance; US market (67% of revenue) is insulated by 145% tariffs. The risk is to the international growth narrative in price-sensitive emerging markets, where CE MDR certifications are opening doors for Chinese players. VBP expansion to surgical robot consumables is the highest-impact policy risk. *(Source: [[Research/2026-03-28 - AI Threats to Intuitive Surgical]])*

- **The Ion diagnostic-to-therapeutic funnel is the most undervalued strategic asset in surgical robotics.** Ion's 84.2% diagnostic yield (vs Monarch's 71.0%) is establishing monopoly on robotic lung biopsy. The integrated pathway — diagnose with Ion, resect with da Vinci in same anesthetic event — creates a "one-stop-shop" that competitors offering disjointed platforms cannot match. This expands ISRG's TAM into thoracic surgery and represents a template for future natural orifice procedures.

- **Cross-portfolio pattern: ISRG's "planned obsolescence as moat reset" parallels [[Theses/EINK - E Ink Holdings|EINK]]'s competitive graveyard.** Both companies have competitors that fail not from lack of capital but from inability to replicate decades of accumulated process knowledge. In EINK's case, Qualcomm spent $975M and failed. In ISRG's case, Hugo launched targeting Xi capabilities only to find ISRG had already moved the goalposts to dV5. The common thread: moats built on empirical learning curves (epitaxial process mastery for EINK, 25-year clinical predicate chains for ISRG) are structurally wider than moats built on IP or capital alone.
