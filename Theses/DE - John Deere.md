# DE — John Deere

## Summary

P/E re-rated from ~16x to 30-36x during declining earnings — unprecedented for a farm equipment company, signaling a structural shift toward platform valuation. Deere's Operations Center (500M engaged acres, 1M+ connected machines) is a de facto "farm ERP" targeting 10% of revenue (~$5-6B) from recurring software by 2030 at ~85% gross margins vs ~25% for equipment. The thesis faces a genuine pincer: AGCO's open-platform counter-strategy narrows the technology gap from below, while FTC right-to-repair litigation and a $99M class action settlement (10-year software access) threaten the closed-ecosystem moat from above. At ~$602 with a $1.2B tariff overhang, risk/reward has improved from the $660 February peak but remains contingent on actual order acceleration — not yet visible in dealer data.

## Key Non-consensus Insights

- **The market still prices Deere as a cyclical equipment OEM; the real option is a "farm ERP" / vertical SaaS platform.** Operations Center: 1M+ connected machines, ~500M engaged acres (up 52% from 329M in 2022), 200+ third-party apps via Developer Portal. Ag Leader and Kinze data-integration agreements allow competitive equipment to stream into the Deere dashboard — co-option over exclusion. 10% recurring revenue target implies ~$5-6B at ~85% gross margins vs ~25% for equipment. 80% automation attach rate on new combines and See & Spray's 76% herbicide reduction across 5M+ acres prove measurable ROI drives farmer adoption.

- **The P/E expansion from ~16x to 30-36x at cycle trough is the strongest quantitative evidence of structural re-rating — unprecedented for a farm equipment company.** An estimated 60-70% of the stock's move reflects a technology/platform premium; 30-40% is the cyclical recovery bet. A purely cyclical trade would compress toward 18-20x, not expand. The premium is permanent only if execution on the platform thesis continues.

- **Deere's closed-ecosystem strategy is a double-edged sword — and the regulatory threat materialized faster than consensus expected.** $99M class action settlement (April 7, 2026) requires 10-year software access parity with offline reprogramming by December 31, 2026 — separate from the ongoing FTC antitrust lawsuit in discovery. Iowa right-to-repair passed committee 18-5, joining Colorado. Forced broad platform access could erode subscription pricing power underpinning the recurring revenue target.

- **Deere's tech lead in large-scale agriculture is genuine but narrowing, and in construction Deere is clearly behind.** AGCO's OutRun won the Davidson Prize two consecutive years, targeting Deere's 8R tractor base with retrofit autonomy; PTx Trimble at ~$860M revenue targets $2B by 2029. In construction: Caterpillar has 500+ autonomous trucks (9.3B+ tonnes hauled), Komatsu 700+ (10B+ metric tons) vs Deere's early-stage CES 2025 "Dusty." The market may be over-extrapolating the ag tech premium into construction.

- **Near-term signals are contradictory: $44.3B in government payments may shorten the upcycle, and insider selling at the top contradicts the trough narrative.** $23.9B disaster assistance, $15.2B commodity payments, $23B enhanced crop insurance inflate near-term demand but create a 2027-2028 cliff. CEO May sold $20.8M weeks before ATH; CIO Kalathur sold ~$12.3M; Fidelity reduced 19%. Crop economics are genuinely weak: corn -3.7%, soybeans -7.2%, wheat -9.8% YoY.

## Business Model & Product Description

John Deere's business model is undergoing a transformation from "Caterpillar for farms" (cyclical equipment OEM) to "Apple of agriculture" (closed hardware ecosystem with an emerging SaaS platform layer). The traditional model — manufacture equipment, sell through 1,900+ dealer locations, finance through captive Financial Services segment — remains the revenue base, but the strategic evolution is the Operations Center platform, which functions as a "farm ERP" analogous to Apple's iOS: Deere controls the core operating system layer while 200+ third-party applications integrate through its Developer Portal. The critical economic insight is the margin differential: equipment carries ~25% gross margins with heavy cyclical exposure, while the targeted 10% software/subscription revenue layer (~$5-6B by 2030) would carry ~85% gross margins with recurring, cycle-resistant characteristics. Four reportable segments generate equipment and financing revenue, while the cross-cutting technology platform is the hidden fifth segment that the market has begun to price but hasn't fully valued:

### Production & Precision Agriculture (PPA)

Large-scale farming equipment and integrated precision agriculture technology for commercial row-crop operations:

- **Equipment**: Row crop tractors (7R, 8R, 9R series), four-wheel-drive tractors (9RX), combine harvesters (X9, S7, S8 series), cotton pickers, self-propelled sprayers (400/600 series), and seeding equipment (ExactEmerge planters with individual row control).
- **Autonomous Tillage**: Fully autonomous tractor operation for tillage tasks (chisel plowing, field cultivating, discing). Commercial launch in 2026 with retrofit kits available for 2016+ model-year machines. Only ~20 US farmers outfitted for initial deployment.
- **See & Spray**: AI-powered computer vision system that identifies weeds in real time and applies herbicide only to targeted plants, leaving crops untreated. Scaled to 5M+ acres in 2025, delivering 43.9–90.6% herbicide reduction (averaging 76%). Saved 31M gallons of herbicide mix. Unlimited annual license option introduced for 2026.
- **Precision Upgrades**: Retrofit technology packages for the installed base of 2016+ model-year machines, enabling guidance, connectivity, and automation capabilities on existing equipment. Opens the entire installed base to recurring technology monetization.

Q1 FY2026 segment margins collapsed to 4.4% (from 11.0%) due to absorption of $250M in tariff costs (out of $1.2B FY2026 total).

### Small Agriculture & Turf (SAT)

Equipment for smaller-scale farming, landscaping, and residential use:
- Utility tractors (1–6 series), compact utility tractors, riding lawn equipment, zero-turn mowers, commercial mowing equipment (QuikTrak, ZTrak), Gator utility vehicles, and golf course maintenance equipment.
- Q1 FY2026 operating profit +24% YoY — the first segment to show cyclical recovery.

### Construction & Forestry (C&F)

Heavy equipment for construction, infrastructure, forestry, and roadbuilding:
- Excavators, wheel loaders, crawler dozers, articulated dump trucks, backhoe loaders, skid steers, motor graders, forestry harvesters, and feller bunchers.
- **Autonomous construction**: "Dusty" autonomous dump truck prototyped at CES 2025, but early-stage compared to Caterpillar (500+ autonomous haul trucks, 9.3B+ tonnes hauled) and Komatsu (700+ autonomous trucks, 10B+ metric tons).
- Q1 FY2026 revenue +34% YoY, operating profit more than doubled, driven by Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act tailwinds. Full-year guide: flat to +5%.

### Financial Services (FS)

Equipment financing, operating leases, revolving credit, and crop insurance for Deere customers. Provides the credit backbone enabling equipment purchases — particularly critical during cycle troughs when farmer cash flow is constrained.

### Technology Platform (cross-segment)

**Operations Center** — Cloud-based farm management platform with 1M+ connected machines and ~500M engaged acres (up 52% from 329M in 2022; target 600M by 2030). Functions as a "farm ERP" with hub-and-spoke architecture analogous to Apple's App Store:
- Deere controls the core data and operations layer.
- 200+ third-party applications integrate via the Developer Portal (REST APIs, OAuth, farmer-controlled data permissions) including Samsara, DeLaval, Yara.
- Recent data-integration agreements with Ag Leader and Kinze allow *competitive* equipment to stream metrics into the Deere dashboard — signaling a strategy to co-opt third-party equipment into the Deere ecosystem rather than exclude it.
- 80%+ automation package attach rate on new combines demonstrates demand for the technology tier.
- Management targets 10% of revenue (~$5–6B) from recurring software/subscription revenue by 2030 at ~85% gross margins (vs. ~25% for equipment).

## Industry Context

**Agricultural Cycle Dynamics.** Deere management and AGCO CEO both declared 2025/2026 as the cycle bottom, with equipment fleets at peak age across major markets. FY2026 earnings remain in decline: Deere guided net income of $4.5-5.0B (raised from $4.0-4.75B post-Q1 beat), down from $5.03B in FY2025 and $7.1B in FY2024. US/Canada large ag equipment demand expected down 15-20% in FY2026. March 2026 AEM data showed US tractor sales down 9.1% and combines down 25.3% (Canada worse: combines -60.6%). The trough-bottom call requires these numbers to inflect — they have not yet. USDA forecasts 2026 net farm income at $153.4B (down 0.7% from 2025), with crop cash receipts declining $14.2B (-2.7%). The decline is concentrated in corn (-3.7%), soybeans (-7.2%), and wheat (-9.8%) — the exact crops that drive large equipment purchases.

**The iOS vs. Android War for the Farm.** The agricultural technology market is bifurcating into two competing philosophies. Deere runs a closed, vertically integrated ecosystem (hardware + software + data + dealer network) that delivers a seamless user experience but locks farmers into a single brand. AGCO/PTx Trimble runs an open, mixed-fleet platform that works across equipment brands, targeting the reality that most farms operate 3-5 brands. AGCO's FarmENGAGE, launched August 2025, is positioned as "the ag industry's only digital platform that can manage farmers' entire operations of multiple brands." The philosophical divide mirrors Apple vs. Android: Deere extracts higher margins (32.6% gross vs CNH ~21.8% and AGCO ~23-24%) through integration, while AGCO targets the larger addressable market of non-Deere farms. The most probable outcome (40-45% probability) is a 2-3 player oligopoly where each builds semi-closed ecosystems around its machinery base, with ISOBUS/AgIN standards ensuring baseline interoperability.

**Right-to-Repair as a Structural Market Force.** The regulatory landscape has intensified beyond what consensus priced 12 months ago. The FTC lawsuit (joined by Illinois, Minnesota, Michigan, Wisconsin, Arizona) is in discovery. The $99M class action settlement (April 7, 2026) commits Deere to 10-year software access parity between farmers and dealers. Iowa's right-to-repair bill passed committee 18-5. Colorado already enacted farm right-to-repair law. 11+ states have legislation advancing. The Bayer Climate FieldView data layer (250M+ subscribed acres across 23 countries) and AEF's AgIN dataspace framework are pushing interoperability from the standards side. Right-to-repair is no longer a tail risk — it is an active, multi-front force reshaping the competitive landscape.

**Precision Agriculture Market Growth.** The global precision ag market was valued at $9.5B in 2025, projected to reach $17.3B by 2031 (10.5% CAGR). The broader agricultural equipment market ($302B in 2025) is projected to reach $690B by 2035. Autonomous farm equipment sales projected to surpass 60,000 units annually by 2026. The replacement cycle is increasingly driven by obsolescence of non-connected machinery, not just equipment age — farmers retrofitting with AI-enabled components creates recurring revenue streams that support a software-like multiple.

**Tariff and Trade Overhang.** Deere faces $1.2B in pre-tax tariff costs for FY2026 ($250M absorbed in Q1 alone), including 50% tariffs on imported steel/aluminum and up to 60% on Chinese components. Management chose to absorb rather than pass through immediately, crushing PPA margins to 4.4% (from 11.0% prior year). A universal 10% tariff announced in April 2026 adds further pressure. On the demand side, tariff uncertainty has triggered a farm equipment spending freeze — AEM data shows US tractor sales down 9.1% and combines down 25.3%, with farmers prioritizing repairs over new purchases.

**Construction & Forestry as Cyclical Ballast.** Deere's C&F segment surged 34% in Q1 FY2026 (operating profit more than doubled) on Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act tailwinds. However, Deere guides C&F "flat to up 5%" for the full year, suggesting the initial pulse has been absorbed. In construction autonomy, Deere is technologically competitive but structurally behind CAT and Komatsu, who have deployed autonomous mining/hauling at commercial scale for years.

## Bull Case

- **Cycle bottom is confirmed and earnings inflect:** If management's trough call proves correct and FY2027 delivers the consensus ~30% earnings recovery, the stock re-rates further from an already elevated base. Small Ag & Turf (+24%) and Construction & Forestry (+34%) already showed genuine recovery in Q1 FY2026.
- **Precision ag platform achieves escape velocity:** 80% automation attach rate on new combines, 5M+ See & Spray acres, and the retrofit Precision Upgrades strategy for 2016+ machines demonstrate the installed base monetization path. If recurring software revenue reaches 5-7% of sales by 2028, the earnings quality transformation becomes undeniable.
- **Farm ERP becomes the consensus framing:** As generalist investors adopt the "farm ERP" / vertical SaaS lens, the valuation paradigm shifts permanently from cyclical (15-18x mid-cycle) to platform (25-30x), justified by the ~85% software gross margin differential.
- **Government payments + fleet age drive replacement surge:** $44.3B in government payments + peak fleet age + falling interest rates could produce an equipment replacement wave in H2 2026 and into 2027. The $11B FBA program specifically targets row crop producers — Deere's core customer base.
- **Data flywheel compounds:** Each of Deere's 1M+ connected machines acts as a mobile sensor suite generating continuous agronomic data. See & Spray alone has collected tens of millions of high-resolution weed images. This data flywheel — better models, better products, more sales, more data — is a classic increasing-returns dynamic that competitors cannot replicate without equivalent field-scale deployment.
- **Autonomous tillage commercializes at scale:** Full commercial launch in 2026, with retrofit kits available for order by summer 2026. Global autonomous farm equipment sales projected to surpass 60,000 units annually.

## Bear Case

- **Trough is deeper or longer than consensus:** March AEM data (tractors -9.1%, combines -25.3% US; combines -60.6% Canada) shows no sign of inflection. If corn prices remain below breakeven ($4.50-4.80/bu), equipment demand could contract through 2027, meaning the stock is paying a growth premium on a shrinking earnings base.
- **Tariffs permanently compress PPA margins:** The $1.2B FY2026 tariff headwind may not be fully offset by pricing. PPA margins at 4.4% (Q1) vs the guided range of 11-13% (full year) suggest the tariff absorption is front-loaded — but if trade policy escalates, Deere cannot simultaneously absorb tariffs AND maintain the premium valuation.
- **Right-to-repair erodes the platform moat:** The $99M settlement's 10-year software access commitment plus the ongoing FTC case could force genuine platform openness. If farmers and independents get dealer-equivalent repair tools by Dec 31, 2026, Deere's high-margin aftermarket revenue (typically 15-20% of revenue but a much higher profit share) faces structural compression.
- **AGCO's open platform gains traction:** OutRun winning the Davidson Prize two consecutive years, targeting Deere's own 8R tractor base with retrofit autonomy, and AGCO FarmENGAGE offering multi-brand management demonstrates the competitive gap is closing. PTx Trimble at $860M revenue with $2B target by 2029 is a credible challenger.
- **Insider selling at the top:** CEO May sold $20.8M and CIO Kalathur sold $12.3M ahead of the stock's largest rally. Fidelity reduced 19%. If executives with best visibility are reducing exposure, the market's enthusiasm may exceed internal confidence.
- **Government payments pull demand forward:** If FBA and ECAP payments inflate 2026 equipment purchases, they shorten the subsequent upcycle and create a 2027-2028 demand cliff. The farm economy is sustained by $44.3B in government payments, not organic crop receipts (which are declining 2.7% YoY).
- **Construction autonomy gap ignored:** Stock trades at a platform premium partly based on the autonomy narrative, but in construction — where CAT (500+ autonomous trucks) and Komatsu (700+ autonomous trucks) dominate — Deere has no structural edge. Investors may be over-extrapolating the ag tech premium across all segments.

## Catalysts

- **Q2 FY2026 earnings (May 2026):** Will PPA margins recover from 4.4%? Has management achieved "price-cost neutral" on tariffs? Any guidance upgrade would validate the trough-bottom call.
- **Autonomous tillage kit commercial launch (Summer 2026):** Retrofit kits for 2016+ models available for order. Take-rate data will be the first real test of farmer willingness to pay for autonomy at scale beyond early adopters.
- **FTC right-to-repair case developments:** Discovery phase ongoing; any ruling, settlement signal, or acceleration of timeline would move the stock. The $99M class action settlement may pressure the FTC case toward resolution.
- **FBA payment disbursement (Feb-Apr 2026):** $11B flowing to row crop producers. Channel checks on dealer sentiment and order books post-payment will indicate whether the trough is real.
- **Commodity price movements:** Corn season-average forecast $4.20/bu. A weather-driven supply shock (La Nina/El Nino in South America) or Chinese soybean buying surge could rapidly improve farmer economics and accelerate equipment purchasing.
- **Precision ag subscription revenue disclosure:** Deere has been opaque about actual software revenue dollars. Any breakout reporting or quantified subscription revenue would be a significant re-rating catalyst (positive or negative depending on the number).
- **Trade policy shifts:** Tariff escalation (additional universal tariffs) or de-escalation (China Phase 2 deal) would materially impact both Deere's cost structure and farmer purchasing power.
- **Analyst consensus convergence:** Current $665 average target with 13 Buy / 0 Sell. The stock at ~$602 is below consensus for the first time since early January — a shift from the February peak when it traded above all targets.

## Risks

1. **Cycle timing:** Market pricing 2027 recovery; any delay extends the trough and compresses the multiple. March AEM data shows no inflection yet. A $1 earnings miss in FY2026 guidance could trigger a sharp de-rating from 30x+ to historical 15-18x (~40-50% downside).
2. **Right-to-repair / regulatory:** FTC lawsuit in discovery; $99M settlement forces 10-year software access by Dec 31, 2026. Forced open access could erode platform control, subscription pricing power, and the high-margin aftermarket — the foundation of the premium multiple vs CNH/AGCO.
3. **Tariff margin destruction:** $1.2B pre-tax tariff cost in FY2026; PPA margins at 4.4% in Q1 vs 11.0% prior year. If tariffs are structural (not transitory), the earnings recovery is delayed regardless of demand inflection.
4. **Competitive catch-up:** AGCO/PTx Trimble's open-platform autonomy (OutRun) directly targeting Deere's installed base. CNH's SenseApply spot-spraying at 25 mph exceeds See & Spray's 15 mph. The technology lead that justifies the valuation premium may compress from 3-5 years to 1-2 years faster than consensus expects.
5. **Demand pull-forward:** $44.3B government payments may inflate 2026 demand and shorten the 2027-2028 upcycle. Farm economy is sustained by fiscal policy, not organic crop economics. Corn receipts -3.7%, soybeans -7.2%, wheat -9.8% YoY.
6. **Insider selling signal:** CEO May ($20.8M), CIO Kalathur ($12.3M), Fidelity (-19%) — the people with best visibility are reducing exposure. 32 insider sells vs 25 buys over trailing 12 months.
7. **Valuation fragility:** At 30-36x trough earnings, there is no margin for error. The stock trades above Morningstar's fair value estimate. A de-rating to 15x on $16 EPS implies $240 — massive downside from current levels. The "tech premium" must be continuously earned through execution.
