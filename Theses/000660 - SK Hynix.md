# SK Hynix — 000660.KS

## Summary

The monopoly narrative is ending, and the market has not re-priced the thesis. SK Hynix just printed Q1 2026 revenue of $37.9B at a 72% operating margin — the highest quarterly result in memory-industry history — on the back of a 57% HBM market share, a $30B 2026 HBM revenue book, and a $587.6B market cap that implies sustained dominance through Nvidia's Rubin cycle. The non-consensus read: Samsung HBM4 passed Nvidia qualification with "best scores" for Vera Rubin, Micron ramped to ~25% HBM share, and Gemini's consensus-skeptic model projects SK Hynix share tracking 62% → 57% → 52% → 48% → 45% (2024 → 2030) — an "Incumbent Erosion" path that the sell-side's $1.43M 12-month price target and 13x P/E do not reflect. The thesis is medium-conviction long because the HBM4 Rubin cycle (H2 2026 through 2028) anchors a $70-120B 2026 operating-profit base that compounds even while share erodes from 62% toward 45%, and because two asymmetric call options — the SanDisk-partnered HBF (High-Bandwidth Flash) standardization at OCP and the Solidigm 51% QLC market-share position inside a bifurcating AI-storage market — are unpriced in the current valuation. Thesis kill trigger is specific: Samsung HBM4 capturing >35% of Rubin SKU allocation in the first two shipping quarters would validate the 45% terminal-share path ahead of schedule and make current multiples indefensible.

## Key Non-consensus Insights

**1. Share erosion from 62% → 45% by 2030 is priced as a cyclical dip, not the structural regression it is.** Sell-side 12-month targets averaging KRW 1.43M (Mirae raised to 1.37M, Samsung Securities and IBK to 1.8M) extrapolate the current 57% HBM share forward on the thesis that MR-MUF packaging, 1c DRAM yields (~80%, nearing 90%), and Nvidia cycle familiarity constitute a durable moat. Gemini's HBM4 Market Canvas projects the opposite: Samsung's "U-Shape Recovery" (24% → 33% by 2030) and Micron's "Steady Climb" (15% → 21% by 2030) erode SK Hynix from 58% to 45% within five years. The consensus error is treating Samsung's HBM3E qualification miss (2024) as permanent rather than self-correcting — Samsung passed Nvidia's HBM4 qualification with "best scores" for Vera Rubin-specific SKUs in Q1 2026. Once Samsung's 1c DRAM yield crosses 70% (pilot at 50%, target 70% by mid-2026), Nvidia's procurement posture shifts from "SK Hynix preferred" to "dual-source at parity pricing" — which compresses SK Hynix's blended HBM gross margin from the current 60%+ toward 40-45% as Samsung and Micron take unit share and reset pricing at the marginal supplier.

**2. MR-MUF is a process moat, not an architectural one — and architecture wins long-cycle memory battles.** Consensus treats MR-MUF (Mass Reflow Molded Underfill) as SK Hynix's structural advantage for HBM4 12-Hi and HBM4E 16-Hi because it delivers superior thermal performance and lower warpage than Samsung's TC-NCF at equivalent stack heights. The non-consensus read: Samsung is betting on hybrid bonding (via BESI Kinex qualification, 16-Hi and 20-Hi enablement by 2027) as a leap-frog technology, and SK Hynix's March 2026 Kinex order from BESI signals that even SK Hynix sees MR-MUF as a temporary cushion. Once hybrid bonding crosses the yield threshold (currently 50% at 16-Hi, target >70% by H2 2027), it collapses SK Hynix's differentiation — because the 20-Hi stack becomes the architectural standard for HBM5 (2028-2029), and hybrid bonding is the only packaging path that delivers 20-Hi at acceptable thermals. SK Hynix is a fast follower on hybrid bonding, not a leader; this concession is what the MR-MUF narrative obscures.

**3. HBF (High-Bandwidth Flash) co-invented with SanDisk is an unpriced $5-10B TAM option inside the thesis.** SK Hynix architecture simulations (H3 architecture) show a 2.69x perf/watt improvement, 18.8x batch size expansion, and GPU-count reduction from 32 → 2 when HBF replaces a portion of HBM in inference workloads. OCP's HBF standardization workstream (SK Hynix + SanDisk + Samsung) creates a new memory category by 2027. The sell-side models SK Hynix as a pure DRAM/HBM/NAND business and assigns zero value to HBF because it is pre-revenue. If HBF captures even 10% of the HBM TAM by 2030 (Gemini projects HBM at $98-101B by 2030), that is a $10B+ revenue line at 50%+ gross margin where SK Hynix shares the architectural IP with only one partner. This call option has a strike price embedded in the March 2026 HBF OCP spec release and an exercise window that opens when Nvidia's post-Rubin architecture (likely 2028+) selects HBF as a memory tier.

**4. CXMT is the right bear case, but the wrong timing bear case.** The consensus bear on Chinese memory — CXMT ramping from 70K → 200K → 300K WSPM and capturing 15% of global DRAM share — is correct on the end state but wrong on the timing. CXMT is still three nodes behind on leading-edge DRAM (1gamma vs SK Hynix 1c) and has no HBM product at all. The genuine CXMT threat is legacy-DRAM commoditization by 2028-2029, which compresses SK Hynix's commodity-DRAM segment (~40% of DRAM revenue) from 30-35% gross margin to 15-20%. But HBM, which carries 60%+ gross margin and represents the earnings engine, is insulated from CXMT through at least 2028 by the 1c node gap and the absence of a Chinese hybrid-bonding packaging ecosystem. The bear case that matters is Samsung capturing Nvidia Rubin share in 2026, not CXMT capturing share in 2029. The market conflates these and mis-prices the nearer-term threat.

**5. Solidigm is a hidden $8-12B asset trapped inside SK Hynix's consolidated reporting.** SK Hynix paid $9B for Solidigm in 2021 at what looked like a top-of-cycle multiple. At Q3 2025 NAND share of 21.1% (SK Group combined, up from 16.6% in Q1 2025) with a record $3.34B quarterly revenue run-rate and a 51% QLC market share, Solidigm alone would command a standalone valuation of $15-25B at Kioxia-comparable 8-10x EBITDA — inside a $587.6B consolidated market cap where NAND contributes ~30% of revenue but receives zero separate multiple recognition. A Solidigm IPO (rumored for 2027, post-Kioxia public float re-pricing) would crystallize $6-15B of hidden value and reset the parent-company multiple by separating a 15% EBITDA-margin NAND business from an 80% EBITDA-margin DRAM/HBM business. The sell-side assigns SK Hynix a blended 8x EV/EBITDA; a sum-of-parts (DRAM/HBM at 12x + Solidigm at 8x) argues for 10-11x — a 20-30% re-rating that is independent of HBM share trajectory.

## Outstanding Questions

**Q1: What percentage of Nvidia Rubin HBM4 allocation does Samsung actually ship in the first two quarters (Q3 2026–Q4 2026)?** Samsung passed qualification with "best scores," but qualification is necessary, not sufficient — yield on 1c DRAM remains at 50% pilot vs 70% target, and Nvidia's dual-source posture for Rubin-specific SKUs could range from 20% (SK Hynix preferred, Samsung backup) to 40% (genuine dual-source). The answer determines whether 2026 HBM4 market share is SK Hynix 62%/Samsung 25%/Micron 13% (favorable) or SK Hynix 52%/Samsung 35%/Micron 13% (adverse). Confirmed via Nvidia quarterly disclosures and Samsung's own Q3 2026 earnings HBM revenue line item.

**Q2: Does MR-MUF scale to 20-Hi at acceptable thermals, or does SK Hynix need hybrid bonding for HBM5?** SK Hynix's March 2026 BESI Kinex order is the market's tell — if MR-MUF scaled to 20-Hi natively, there would be no need for hybrid bonding qualification. The H2 2026 Kinex qualification result (pass/fail at 16-Hi, with 20-Hi roadmap visibility) reveals whether SK Hynix's packaging lead on HBM4/HBM4E translates into HBM5 leadership or whether Samsung's hybrid-bonding lead inverts the packaging hierarchy in 2028.

**Q3: Does CXMT produce a credible HBM product (HBM2e or HBM3) by 2028, and at what yield?** This is the single question that determines whether Chinese memory is a 2028-2029 commodity-DRAM threat (contained) or a 2028+ HBM threat (thesis-breaking). CXMT is three DRAM nodes behind and has no public HBM roadmap, but the PRC's $142B semiconductor-subsidy fund and the Huawei-CXMT integration path could compress the timeline. Confirmed via CXMT equipment orders (EUV would be the giveaway), Huawei Ascend roadmap specifications, and export-control enforcement effectiveness on TEL/AMAT advanced-node tools.

**Q4: Does HBF achieve hyperscaler production deployment by 2027, or does it slip to 2028+?** The H3 architecture simulation results are compelling, but architecture simulations consistently under-estimate real-world integration cost (memory coherency, power delivery, software stack). OCP standardization (target H2 2026) is the leading indicator; hyperscaler qualification at Meta or Microsoft is the confirming indicator. If HBF slips to 2028, the option value discounts by ~30% (one generation pushed out); if it slips to 2029, the HBF thesis dilutes to zero value in the base case.

**Q5: Does the 2026 HBM market enter oversupply as Goldman Sachs projects (25% oversupply, 10-15% price drop), and if so, does SK Hynix hold pricing better than Samsung/Micron?** SK Hynix 2026 HBM revenue of $30B is modeled at current-price assumptions. A Goldman-scenario 15% price decline on a full-year basis trims $4.5B from revenue and ~$3B from operating profit (100% decremental margin on HBM downside). The question is whether 57% market share + MR-MUF technical advantage translates into 10-20% price premium retention under oversupply, or whether HBM follows commodity DRAM's historical full pass-through of price declines to all suppliers equally.

**Q6: When does SK Hynix IPO or carve out Solidigm, and at what valuation?** Rumored 2027 IPO window. At $15-20B standalone valuation, Solidigm monetization crystallizes $6-11B of hidden value (net of $9B 2021 purchase price and intervening capex). Timing matters — Kioxia's public float in late 2024 at ~$5B market cap and subsequent re-rating to $17B+ by mid-2026 established the NAND public-market valuation template. A 2027 Solidigm IPO could be priced against Kioxia's then-multiple (Kioxia 285A currently at ~8x forward EBITDA).

**Q7: What is the Taiwan-tail HBM exposure, and what is the playbook if CoWoS capacity disappears?** SK Hynix ships HBM to Taiwan for TSMC CoWoS integration with Nvidia/AMD silicon. A China-Taiwan kinetic scenario (binary, low-probability, high-impact) invalidates 60-80% of HBM end-market demand for 12-24 months until CoWoS-class packaging capacity can be rebuilt outside Taiwan. The vault's `[[Macro/AI Bubble Risk and Semiconductor Valuations]]` macro note flags this as a $650B-annual-revenue-requirement threshold risk; SK Hynix has no public disclosed hedge.

## Business Model & Product Description

SK Hynix is the world's second-largest memory-semiconductor manufacturer by revenue and the dominant producer of HBM — the high-bandwidth memory stack that attaches to GPU and ASIC accelerators in AI compute systems. The company's revenue mix is **~70% DRAM/HBM, ~30% NAND flash**, with NAND housed primarily inside the Solidigm subsidiary (acquired from Intel for $9B in 2021) plus organic 3D NAND fabs in South Korea.

**DRAM segment (~55% of revenue):** SK Hynix manufactures DDR5 commodity DRAM, LPDDR5X mobile DRAM, GDDR7 graphics DRAM, and — the profit center — HBM3E, HBM4, and HBM4E stacks. HBM currently represents approximately **40% of DRAM revenue but 54% of DRAM operating profit**, reflecting HBM's 60%+ gross margin vs commodity DRAM's 30-35%. HBM is sold almost exclusively to Nvidia (primary customer, ~70% of HBM revenue), AMD (second), and Broadcom/AWS/Google TPU custom-silicon programs (combined third). The HBM stack is manufactured by bonding 8-16 layers of DRAM die with through-silicon vias (TSV) and a logic base die, using SK Hynix's proprietary **MR-MUF (Mass Reflow Molded Underfill)** packaging process that outperforms Samsung's TC-NCF on thermal performance and warpage — the key reason SK Hynix captured HBM3E sole-source Nvidia qualification in 2024.

**HBM segment revenue trajectory:** $12B (2024) → $30B (2026E, per Gemini Market Canvas) → $45B (2028E, on HBM4E + HBM5 ramp). Capacity is supply-constrained through 2026 and partially through 2027 — SK Hynix's M15X fab is ramping from 10K → 50K WSPM by Q4 2026, and the company's total DRAM capacity target is ~620K WSPM by end-2026 (nearly doubling to match Samsung's scale). CapEx guidance for 2026 is $20.5B (+17% YoY), with CapEx/revenue in the mid-30% range. 1c DRAM is expanding 800% to 160-190K WSPM by end-2026, enabled by a $7.9B ASML EUV order placed in 2025.

**NAND segment (~30% of revenue):** Solidigm subsidiary handles enterprise QLC SSDs (PS1012 244TB QLC on track for 2026) and captures **51% combined QLC market share**. Organic SK Hynix NAND serves mobile (UFS), client SSD, and datacenter TLC markets. Q3 2025 NAND revenue hit a record $3.34B (+52.5% QoQ), with SK Group NAND share reaching 21.1% (up from 16.6% in Q1 2025) — driven by AI-storage demand for high-capacity QLC arrays used as cold-tier storage in inference clusters. NAND gross margin runs 20-30% depending on cycle phase; currently cycle-peak at ~30%. SK Hynix announced a NAND CapEx pullback for 2026 to redirect fab space to DRAM/HBM conversion.

**HBF (High-Bandwidth Flash) — emerging category:** Jointly developed with SanDisk, HBF is a 3D NAND stack engineered for GPU-attached memory-tier service. H3 architecture simulations show 2.69x perf/watt and 18.8x batch-size expansion vs HBM-only configurations on large-batch inference workloads. OCP standardization workstream is in flight (SK Hynix + SanDisk + Samsung); first-production deployment targeted for 2027. Not yet in revenue.

**Revenue segmentation heuristic (margin-weighted view):** Approximately **$30B HBM (60%+ GM, ~$18B gross profit) + $40B commodity DRAM/LPDDR/GDDR (30% GM, ~$12B) + $25B NAND via Solidigm + organic (25% GM, ~$6B) = $95B revenue, $36B gross profit**. The HBM segment delivers 50% of gross profit on 30% of revenue — the concentration that drives SK Hynix's valuation.

## Industry Context

**HBM is a three-supplier triopoly with demand-binding structural scarcity through 2027.** SK Hynix (57% 2026 share), Samsung (30%+ recovering), and Micron (~25% climbing) exhaust the global HBM supply. HBM demand is tied directly to AI-accelerator unit shipments (Nvidia H200/B200/B300/Rubin, AMD MI300X/MI350/MI450, Broadcom TPU v6/v7, Google TPU v5p/v6p); HBM content per GPU is increasing 20-30% per generation, with Rubin Ultra targeting ~1TB HBM per GPU. TrendForce projects HBM demand growth of >130% in 2025 and 77% in 2026. HBM capacity from all three suppliers was **sold out through CY2026** as of mid-2025; current supply-demand balance is tightening again after brief fear-driven softness around TurboQuant selloff (March 24, 2026). **DRAM prices surged 171.8% YoY** in late 2025 and are projected up another 40-50% through H1 2026.

**The packaging-technology arms race is the competitive axis.** Three vendor philosophies:

| Vendor | Packaging | Current Stack | 2027 Roadmap | Strategic Bet |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| SK Hynix | MR-MUF | HBM4 12-Hi | HBM4E 16-Hi | Process lead preserves margin through HBM4/4E cycle; BESI Kinex qual for HBM5 hybrid bonding |
| Samsung | TC-NCF → Hybrid Bonding | HBM3E 8-Hi | HBM4 12-Hi + HBM5 hybrid 20-Hi | Leap-frog via hybrid bonding; regain leadership at HBM5 |
| Micron | TC-NCF pragmatic | HBM3E 8-Hi | HBM4 12-Hi redesign | Re-enter with mid-tier Rubin CPX variant; steady share gain |

Samsung's hybrid-bonding bet (enabled by BESI Kinex tool, 67% D2W hybrid-bonding market share — see [[Theses/BESI - BE Semiconductor Industries]]) is the existential competitive threat beginning 2027-2028. If hybrid bonding yields cross 70% at 16-Hi, Samsung leap-frogs SK Hynix's MR-MUF lead at HBM5 and reverses the 2024-2026 share trajectory.

**Value chain leverage analysis:** SK Hynix sits downstream of ASML (EUV scanner monopoly — $7.9B order in 2025) and upstream of TSMC CoWoS (sole-source packaging integrator). The value chain leverage is asymmetric — ASML captures ~8% of SK Hynix CapEx as pure equipment margin, and TSMC captures 30-40% gross margin on CoWoS integration that locks in SK Hynix's HBM customer delivery. SK Hynix's own leverage comes from (1) HBM scarcity vs Nvidia's GPU-unit growth curve and (2) Samsung's yield catch-up lag. Both are time-bounded advantages.

**Macro demand anchor:** J.P. Morgan calculates **$650B annual AI revenue by 2030** is required to justify current AI capex through that date. HBM capex is a ~15% slice of total AI-capex flow. If AI revenue tracks below $400B by 2030 (bear case — T. Rowe 95% zero-return enterprise-adoption signal), HBM enters oversupply one year sooner than Goldman's 2026 warning, and SK Hynix's 57% share dominance converts from asset to liability (highest absolute unit exposure to a price-decline cycle). See [[Macro/AI Bubble Risk and Semiconductor Valuations]].

## Bull Case

The bull case rests on four pillars compounding through 2028:

1. **Rate-cut-driven multiple expansion on proven earnings.** 2026 operating profit of $70-120B on a $587.6B market cap puts forward P/E in the 5-8x range on realized earnings. Korean equity multiples compress historically at 8-10x for cyclical peaks; a Fed-cycle-aligned KRW appreciation + Korean equity re-rating toward 12-15x P/E on 2026 earnings delivers 50-80% upside to KRW 2.0-2.4M by mid-2027. This is the sell-side's implicit path (Samsung Securities 1.8M, high-end forecasts 2.5M).

2. **HBM supercycle extension through 2028.** If Rubin ship-through runs ahead of Nvidia's current guidance (supported by TSM CoWoS capacity expansion to 80K wafers/month by Q4 2026) and HBM4E captures the 16-Hi stack premium at 1.5-2x ASP vs HBM4 12-Hi, 2027 HBM revenue reaches $40-45B at 65%+ gross margin. Combined with HBM5 ramp beginning late 2027, SK Hynix runs a 2024-2028 HBM earnings super-cycle — not a peak in 2026.

3. **HBF monetization at OCP-standard deployment (2027+).** If OCP HBF spec finalization in Q3 2026 triggers Meta/Microsoft qualification in Q1 2027 and production-scale deployment in H2 2027, SK Hynix books $2-5B incremental 2028 revenue at 50%+ gross margin. The option value is un-modeled in current sell-side estimates.

4. **Solidigm IPO re-rating.** A 2027 Solidigm spinoff at $15-20B valuation crystallizes $6-11B net value transfer to SK Hynix shareholders and unlocks a 20-30% sum-of-parts re-rating on the remaining DRAM/HBM-pure business.

**Bull valuation target:** KRW 2.4M per share, ~$1.67T market cap (FX-adjusted), by end-2027. Path: 2026 EPS of $60 × 14x multiple + $80B Solidigm/HBF option value.

## Bear Case

The bear case is Samsung execution combined with a 2026 oversupply:

1. **Samsung HBM4 captures 35%+ of Rubin allocation in H2 2026.** Samsung's Vera Rubin qualification with "best scores" converts from qualification to unit-ship allocation. SK Hynix 2026 HBM share drops to 50-52% from 57% — a -5pp share loss, compounded by Micron climbing to 25%. SK Hynix 2026 HBM revenue tracks $25B vs consensus $30B (-17% miss).

2. **Goldman Sachs 25% oversupply materializes.** HBM pricing declines 10-15% on a full-year 2026 basis as Samsung capacity adds come online. $30B revenue × -12% blended = -$3.6B revenue, -$2.4B operating profit (67% decremental). Q4 2026 realized Op profit misses consensus by 20%.

3. **MR-MUF lead erodes at HBM5 transition.** By Q4 2027, Samsung's hybrid-bonding yield crosses 70% at 16-Hi and 60% at 20-Hi. SK Hynix HBM5 roadmap requires hybrid-bonding adoption (via BESI Kinex) that it cannot ramp as fast as Samsung's native investment. 2028 HBM share steps to 48%; 2029 to 45% — tracking Gemini's Incumbent Erosion path.

4. **CXMT accelerates legacy DRAM commoditization.** CXMT reaches 200K WSPM by end-2027 (vs current 70K WSPM). Commodity DRAM prices drop 20-25% per year 2027-2029. SK Hynix's $40B commodity-DRAM segment loses $8-10B revenue on price. Blended 2028 SK Hynix operating profit drops to $35-45B (vs peak $70-120B in 2026).

5. **Taiwan tail.** Probability-weighted 5-10% through 2028. A single kinetic event zeros 18-24 months of HBM revenue, permanently re-rates KRX memory multiples, and invalidates the sum-of-parts thesis. No disclosed hedge.

**Bear valuation target:** KRW 900K per share, ~$390B market cap. Path: 2028 EPS of $30 × 10x multiple, -30% from current.

## Risks

**Thesis risks (investment case is wrong):**

1. **Samsung Rubin allocation exceeds 30% in 2026.** Directly invalidates the 57% share base that anchors the current earnings profile. Trigger: Nvidia Q3 2026 Rubin ship disclosure showing Samsung supply allocation. Response: conviction → low, re-evaluate kill trigger at 35%.

2. **HBM4E MR-MUF process issue delays sole-source opportunity.** SK Hynix's 12-Hi MR-MUF stack has not been ramp-proven at 16-Hi scale; any yield issue in H2 2026 hands Samsung a sole-source window. Trigger: SK Hynix Q3 2026 HBM revenue miss >10% vs guidance.

3. **HBM5 hybrid-bonding dependency.** SK Hynix 20-Hi HBM5 requires hybrid bonding; BESI Kinex qualification is not guaranteed. Trigger: Q4 2026-Q1 2027 Kinex qualification fail.

**Position risks (thesis is right but stock drops):**

4. **Taiwan kinetic event.** Binary, low-probability, high-severity. Probability-weighted 5-10% over 3 years per vault macro analysis. Unhedged in the thesis.

5. **Korean macro — KRW depreciation shock.** SK Hynix is KRX-listed but earns in USD. A KRW-strengthening shock (BOK rate divergence from Fed) compresses KRW-denominated earnings optics; historical pattern shows -10 to -15% stock decline on 5%+ KRW appreciation shocks.

6. **Memory cycle downturn despite HBM strength.** If commodity DRAM enters down-cycle while HBM stays strong, blended margin compresses 300-500bps and multiples de-rate faster than HBM revenue growth compensates. Trigger: TrendForce commodity DRAM contract pricing Q-Q decline for 2 consecutive quarters.

7. **Solidigm IPO delay or cancellation.** If Solidigm IPO slips to 2028+ or is cancelled, the sum-of-parts re-rating thesis pillar disappears. Removes ~15-20% of bull-case upside.

8. **TurboQuant-style algorithmic-compression event re-rates memory demand.** March 24, 2026 TurboQuant shock caused SK Hynix -6.23% in one session. A follow-on breakthrough (e.g., 2-bit weight quantization at <1% accuracy loss) could trigger a 15-25% single-day drop even if Jevons absorbs the demand impact over 6-12 months. See [[Research/2026-03-27 - TurboQuant Impact on Memory Demand]].
