## Summary

The market values SK Telecom as a damaged Korean wireless incumbent at ~5.2x core EV/EBITDA — a discount to Western telecoms (7-9x) and only a slight premium to depressed Korean peers (KT, LG U+ at ~4x) — and grudgingly awards a ~$1B option value to its 0.3% Anthropic stake. The non-consensus mark: at Yuanta's mark of $2.4B (₩3.5T using Anthropic IV $800B-$1T from secondary markets and rejected VC offers), the Anthropic stake is **16-22% of SKM's $14.5B market cap**. With Anthropic targeting an Oct 2026-Q1 2027 IPO at consensus $643B-$1.04T, IFRS will force a mark-to-market step-up that adds ₩2-3T (~$1.5-2B) to SKT equity book value — a ~10-15% NAV step. Layer in: (a) FY26 operating recovery (Yuanta forecasts +71% YoY OP after the USIM breach + restructuring drag laps), (b) the May 29 2026 SK Broadband full absorption (cash squeeze-out, no SKT dilution) that dismantles the parent-sub holdco discount, (c) AIDC revenue growing 35% YoY with 1GW Ulsan capacity contracted into Stargate Korea (OpenAI MOU signed Oct 2025) and the Anthropic relationship, and (d) a ≥50% adjusted profit shareholder return policy that could support 7-8% total yield post-recovery. The skeptical IC view that this is "Korea discount + holdco trap + AI bubble option" misses three structural facts: SKT executed a clean chaebol unwind in 2021 (SK Square absorbed all non-telecom holdings), the breach impact is fading with H2 2025 churn already stabilized, and the Anthropic stake's mark-to-market sensitivity means **every $100B Anthropic re-rate moves SKT NAV by ~3% of market cap** — asymmetry that compresses sharply at IPO.

## Key Non-consensus Insights

**1. The "Anthropic optionality" framing understates how much of SKM's market cap the stake mathematically represents at IPO valuations consistent with current secondary markets and rejected VC offers.**

The market treats SKT's 0.3% Anthropic stake as cheap optionality, valuing it near book (~$1B). Yuanta marks it at ₩3.5T ($2.4B) using $800B-$1T Anthropic IV — and Anthropic currently trades at $1T on secondary markets with VCs offering $800B (rejected per Bloomberg April 14 2026). At Yuanta's mark, the stake is 16.6% of SKM's $14.5B market cap; at the secondary-market price, ~21%. Critically, Anthropic isn't a typical illiquid private holding — it has investment bank mandates with Goldman/JPM/Morgan Stanley and a stated target window of October 2026 with Q1 2027 a more realistic landing. Once Anthropic IPOs, IFRS forces SKT to mark the stake to market (currently held at cost or fair-value through OCI depending on classification). The non-consensus claim isn't that Anthropic will be worth $1T — it's that **even at base-case IPO of $643B (Bloomberg consensus from $30B ARR data point), the stake is worth ~$1.9B = 13% of SKM market cap, which exceeds what current valuation models implicitly award to non-core assets at Asian telecom holdcos (typically 5-8% pre-monetization).** The IPO event itself transforms the stake from "trust-me asset" to GAAP-recognized equity, eliminating the holdco discount applied to private-stake mark.

**2. The Korean "Korea discount" thesis is being applied to SKT despite its de facto separation from chaebol cross-shareholding via the 2021 SK Square spinoff and the imminent SK Broadband absorption.**

The Korea Discount narrative (chaebol cross-holdings, opaque governance, capital allocation incentives misaligned with minorities) has historically depressed Korean equities by 30-50%. SKT executed the cleanest chaebol unwind in modern Korean corporate history when it spun off SK Square in November 2021 — every non-telecom asset (SK Hynix 20% stake, T1 Esports 55%, T-Map Mobility, 11Street) moved to SK Square. SKT's only material non-operating asset today is the Anthropic stake. Yet the market continues pricing SKT at a chaebol-style holdco discount: 5x EV/EBITDA vs. global telecoms at 7-9x. This implies the market either (a) doesn't believe the spinoff was structurally real, or (b) prices in legacy chaebol risks that no longer apply post-2021. The next governance simplification — May 29 2026 SK Broadband full absorption via cash squeeze-out (₩15,032/share, no new SKT equity issued, ratio 1 SKT to 0.1916882 SKB) — eliminates the parent-sub structure that creates an implicit second-layer discount on fixed-line cash flows. Three structural simplification events (SK Square spinoff → Broadband absorption → likely future Anthropic monetization) progressively dismantle the holdco discount premise; the discount has not yet narrowed correspondingly.

**3. The USIM breach is being mis-read as permanent customer-loss damage when it was a transient regulatory event with capped fine exposure and an inflated near-term comparable base.**

The April 2025 USIM breach (25M customers exposed = entire base) triggered: a ₩134.8B PIPC fine (capped — case is closed), ~250K customer churn to KT/LG U+ (≈1% of base, far below catastrophic-loss models), and ~₩400-500B drag on FY25 net income from breach response (free SIM replacements + remediation + restructuring). Net income fell 73% YoY largely because of these one-time charges plus AI investment ramp. The market has extrapolated as multi-year structural impairment. Three counter-signals: (a) 5G subscriber base actually grew to 17.26M by Sep 2025 (79% of total) — share recovery already underway; (b) Korean wireless market structure (3-player oligopoly, MVNOs at <10% subscriber share with limited national-brand pull) doesn't support sustained multi-percentage-point share loss; (c) Yuanta consensus FY26 OP = ₩1.8T (+71% YoY), implying recovery is the modal forecast. The non-consensus view: the breach created a 6-9 month earnings dip the market is still pricing as a 24+ month decay. The May 12 2026 Q1 earnings print is the first fully-clean post-breach quarter and likely catalyzes the re-rating.

**4. SKT's AI Data Center business is being mis-priced as "small and unprofitable" when capacity, hyperscaler-anchor characteristics, and Korean sovereign AI tailwinds suggest it's structurally advantaged in a market the rest of Asia hasn't yet contested at scale.**

AIDC revenue grew 34.9% YoY in FY25 to ₩519.9B (~$400M) — small absolutely vs. wireless's ₩12.55T but: (a) **growing 7x faster** than consolidated revenue; (b) anchored by both Anthropic (compute partner via TelClaude joint-development relationship) AND OpenAI (Stargate Korea MOU signed October 2025 with Sam Altman/Chey Tae-won); (c) sitting on 1GW Ulsan AIDC scaling announced + Pangyo testbed + existing Gasan (80MW) and Yangju facilities; (d) one of three finalists in the Korean government Sovereign AI Foundation Model project (LG, SKT, Upstage — Naver eliminated; ₩530B total funding, June-December 2026 final selection). Compare to global hyperscaler-adjacent IDC operators trading at mid-teens to 20x EBITDA (Equinix, Digital Realty). At a conservative 12x EBITDA on SKT's AIDC at ~$150M EBITDA (35% segment margins implied by industry comps), AIDC alone is worth ~$1.8B = ~12% of SKM's market cap. The non-consensus framing: SKT is morphing from "Korean telecom incumbent + Anthropic option" into "**Korean AI infrastructure incumbent that happens to also operate the largest wireless network**" — a positioning the market hasn't priced because it requires believing both OpenAI Stargate and Anthropic relationships scale with Korea's $735B sovereign AI push.

**5. The Anthropic stake's mark-to-market behavior makes SKM functionally a leveraged bet on the Anthropic IPO outcome, with payoff profile fundamentally unlike a typical telco holdco — and Korean-listed exposure trades at a deeper discount to NAV than equivalent US/Japanese AI minority-stake structures.**

Compare SKM's setup to SoftBank Group's Arm stake: SoftBank's 90% Arm holding is worth ~$158B vs. SoftBank's $101B market cap — SoftBank trades at a 58% discount to NAV. SKM is a milder version: ~17% of market cap is Anthropic at Yuanta mark, with the rest representing core operating telecom + AI services. The Korean-listed structure trades at a deeper discount-to-NAV than equivalent US-listed AI minority-stake plays (e.g., Microsoft's OpenAI exposure is fully embedded in MSFT's premium multiple, not discounted). The non-consensus view: **post-Anthropic-IPO, SKM's discount-to-NAV gap should narrow toward 20-30% holdco discount norms** (vs. current ~50% if you accept Yuanta's stake mark + global-telco multiple on core), because Korean institutional investors lack vehicles to express direct Anthropic exposure and SKM becomes the singular Korean-listed Anthropic proxy. This dynamic doesn't exist for SoftBank because (a) Arm is liquid US-listed, (b) Japanese investors have direct alternatives. SKM has no Korean listed substitute for Anthropic exposure.

The Stage X 4th-carrier failure removed near-term existential risk but the de facto MVNO-driven price warfare persists.

6. **Is the 1GW Ulsan AIDC actually contracted with disclosed take-or-pay agreements, or is the capacity announcement aspirational?** Press releases describe capacity targets without disclosed off-take. Korean class-action regime is weaker than US but has expanded in recent years. Tail risk could be ₩200-500B over 2026-2028.

8. **What is SKT's actual capital return capacity under the FY24-26 ≥50% adjusted profit policy if FY26 OP recovers to Yuanta's ₩1.8T forecast?** At that OP level + ₩353B 2025 dividend run-rate + Anthropic stake mark-to-market step-up, SKT could fund a 7-8% dividend yield + ₩500B+ buyback. Question: will the new CEO actually execute on capital return, or does the AI capex cycle absorb available cash through 2028? Track: 2026 dividend declaration vs. Yuanta forecast.

## Business Model & Product Description

SK Telecom is structurally three businesses bundled under a single Korean-listed entity, plus one venture stake that has begun to dominate the equity story:

**Segment 1 — MNO Wireless (≈73% of consolidated revenue, ₩12.55T FY25)**

The dominant cash engine. SKT operates Korea's largest mobile network with 17.26M 5G subscribers (Sep 2025) representing 79% of its total subscriber base. ARPU of ₩30,554 (Q2 2025) is the highest of any major Asia-Pacific carrier, reflecting (a) Korea's premium-tier 5G plan adoption (highest 5G penetration of any G20 economy), (b) bundled enterprise/family plan stickiness, and (c) limited price competition from MVNOs (which hold <10% subscriber share despite recent regulatory tailwinds). The wireless business is mature: 5G build-out is essentially complete (capex declining), spectrum auctions are infrequent, and the 3-player oligopoly (SKT ~37-45% depending on metric, KT ~31%, LG U+ ~26%) prevents sustained pricing pressure. The analogy: this looks like AT&T's wireline business circa 2005 — slow-growth annuity with high cash conversion, vulnerable mainly to regulatory price intervention.

**Segment 2 — Fixed-line / IPTV via SK Broadband (≈25% of consolidated revenue, ₩4.19T FY25)**

SKT operates Korea's #2 fixed broadband + IPTV provider via SK Broadband (currently 74.4% owned, becoming 100% wholly-owned May 29 2026 via cash squeeze-out at ₩15,032/share). SKB grew 5.0% in 9M 2025 — the only consolidated segment showing organic growth aside from AI. SKB also runs SKT's traditional IDC capacity (Gasan 80MW, Ilsan, Pangyo). The fixed-line business is the unsung infrastructure layer: it provides the physical fiber + power-and-cooling foundation that makes SKT's AI Data Center pivot credible (you can't lease 1GW of GPU capacity if you don't already own the cooling water rights, the substations, and the metro fiber rings). Post-absorption, the SKB margins flow 100% to SKT shareholders (currently ~26% minority leakage). The analogy: SKB is to SKT what Verizon's wireline business is to Verizon — a structural moat masquerading as a low-growth segment.

**Segment 3 — AI Services (≈3% of consolidated revenue, growing 30%+, ₩519.9B AIDC + ₩181B AIX = ~₩700B FY25)**

Three sub-pillars from the SKT "AI Pyramid 2.0" strategy:

- **AI Data Center (AIDC)**: ₩519.9B FY25 (+34.9% YoY). Capacity: Gasan (80MW), Pangyo testbed, Ulsan scaling to 1GW. Anchor customers: Anthropic (TelClaude development infrastructure), OpenAI (Stargate Korea MOU October 2025), Korean enterprises via GPUaaS launched Jan 2025 (1,000+ Nvidia B200 GPUs).
- **AI Transformation (AIX)**: ₩181B FY25 (+27% YoY). Enterprise AI cloud, AICC, Vision AI, the "Adot Biz" B2B agent product. This is the SaaS-y layer monetizing AIDC capacity for Korean enterprises.
- **AI Service ('A.')**: SKT's consumer personal AI assistant, leveraging Anthropic Claude + telco data. Pre-revenue / nominal monetization but strategic for B2C AI native positioning.

Target: ≥₩5T AI revenue by 2030 (vs. ~₩700B FY25 = ~7x growth in 5 years implied = ~50% CAGR).

**Segment 4 — Anthropic Equity Stake (~0.3% of Anthropic, fair value ₩2.1-3.5T)**

The investment that has become the equity story. Initial $100M August 2023 at $5B Anthropic Series C extension valuation = ~2% stake. Diluted to ~0.3% through Series D ($61.5B), Series E, Series F ($183B), Series G ($380B Feb 2026). Held on SKT books at cost-basis or fair-value-through-OCI (need confirmation from FY25 annual report). Strategic complement: TelClaude joint-development LLM (telco-specialized, +58% accuracy on telecom queries), Anthropic Seoul office expansion 2026, multilingual model collaboration. The non-strategic value: at Anthropic IPO ($643B-$1T+ consensus range), the stake mark-to-market is $1.9B-$3B = 13-21% of SKT's $14.5B market cap.

## Industry Context

**Korean wireless oligopoly — structurally protected, regulatorily threatened**

Korea's wireless market is a classic 3-player oligopoly: SKT ~37-45%, KT ~31%, LG U+ ~26% (subscriber share ranges depend on whether IoT/MVNO are included). Combined the three control >90% of MNO revenue. The 4th-carrier threat (Stage X) failed in 2025 — the Korean government revoked the spectrum award after Stage X failed to demonstrate financing. With facility-based competition off the table, the regulatory pressure shifted to MVNO promotion: wholesale rate cuts of up to 52% (KRW 1.29 → 0.62 per MB), permanent MVNO Wholesale Provision Obligation (Dec 2023 amendment), and lifted device subsidy caps from July 2025 (which actually helps incumbents because they can re-enter the device-discount game vs. MVNOs). Net effect: budget-tier price competition intensifies but core ARPU at ~₩30,554 holds because (a) 5G plans aren't price-sensitive for high-tier users, (b) MVNOs lack national brand pull, (c) family/enterprise plan bundles create switching friction.

**The pricing power test for SKT is enterprise/B2B + AI plans**, not consumer wireless. Consumer wireless is a ~stable annuity. The growth optionality lives in (a) enterprise AI sales (AIX segment), (b) AIDC colocation pricing power vs. global IDC peers, (c) Sovereign AI Foundation Model awards.

**Korean sovereign AI initiative — government-anchored growth lever**

In August 2025 the Korean Ministry of Science and ICT selected 5 consortia (Naver, SK Telecom, LG Group, NCSoft, Upstage) for the Sovereign AI Foundation Model project. Total funding ₩530B (~$390M). By December 2025 first-stage evaluation eliminated NCSoft + Naver (failed originality test). By June 2026 second-phase ends, by December 2026 only 2 consortia survive as designated "Korea AI champions." SKT's selection (and survival of Phase 1) is meaningful: the government is effectively underwriting AI capex against tax revenue exposure — a structural positive for SKT's AIDC depreciation profile. Combined with the OpenAI Stargate Korea partnership (LOI October 2025, joint AI Data Center build in southwest Korea) and the existing Anthropic strategic relationship, SKT is positioning as the Korean-listed proxy for Western AI infrastructure penetration — a positioning competitor KT/LG U+ have not credibly contested.

**Anthropic competitive context — primary partner is the new battleground**

Anthropic's competitive position vs. OpenAI is the underlying determinant of SKM's stake value. Anthropic hit $30B annualized revenue March 2026 (+1,400% YoY from $9B end-2025) — surpassing OpenAI in some product segments per recent disclosures. Series G funding ($30B at $380B post-money Feb 2026) was led by GIC + Coatue with strong over-subscription. The IPO timeline is the key catalyst: Bloomberg consensus at $643B IPO valuation; Polymarket trading higher; secondary trading at $1T already reflects market anticipation. Anthropic engaged Wilson Sonsini (legal) + Goldman/JPM/Morgan Stanley (banking) in late 2025; typical 6-9 month timeline from bank engagement to IPO suggests Q1-Q2 2027 most realistic. Each $100B Anthropic IV change moves SKT NAV by ~$300M (at 0.3% stake) = ~2% of SKT market cap.

## Bull Case

**Anthropic IPO clears $700B at Q1 2027 + operating recovery to Yuanta forecast + AIDC re-rating + dividend resumption.**

Specific drivers:

1. **Anthropic IPO at $700B-$1T**: stake mark-to-market = $2.1-$3B = 14-21% of current SKM market cap. IFRS step-up flows through OCI directly, lifting SKT book value by ₩2-3T. Korean institutional investors lacking direct Anthropic exposure rotate into SKM as the singular Korean-listed proxy; valuation re-rates from holdco discount toward NAV. **Estimated NAV uplift: +15-20%**.

2. **Operating recovery to Yuanta FY26 forecast (₩1.8T OP, +71% YoY)**: USIM breach laps in Q2 2026, restructuring charges decline, AI capex stabilizes. EBITDA recovers from ₩4.66T to ~₩5.5T. Multiple expansion as earnings normalize: 5x → 6x EV/EBITDA on core telecom adds **~$3-3.5B EV (+15-17% market cap)**.

3. **AIDC re-rating as standalone asset**: 1GW Ulsan capacity contracted into Stargate Korea + Anthropic + Korean sovereign AI = $250M+ EBITDA by 2027. At 15x IDC multiple = $3.75B AIDC asset value. Currently embedded in SKT at consolidated 6.5x → ~$1.6B implied. **Re-rating delta: +$2-2.5B EV (+10-12%)**.

4. **Dividend resumption above 5% yield**: Post-recovery, ≥50% payout policy on Yuanta's ₩1.8T OP supports ~₩900B distribution = ~5.5-6% yield + buyback room for 1-2% additional. Korean income-fund inflows.

5. **SK Broadband absorption (May 29 2026)**: removes minority leakage, fully consolidates fixed-line economics, and serves as governance simplification signal.

**Bull-case price target: $48-52 ADR (+25-37% from $38)** = ₩125,000-130,000 common = ₩28-30T market cap. This roughly aligns with Yuanta's ₩118,000 target plus realized Anthropic IPO bump.

**Aggressive bull case (Anthropic at $1.04T, base of secondary trading)**: stake = $3.1B = 21% of current market cap. Add full operating recovery + IDC multiple unlock = **$60-65 ADR (+58-71%)**. This requires Anthropic IPO clearing high-end of 2026 secondary trading + sovereign AI award + no execution stumbles — low probability individually but the disjunction is plausible.

## Bear Case

**Anthropic dilution / IPO disappoints + Korean wireless margin compression intensifies + USIM tail liabilities expand + Korean Won weakens.**

Specific drivers:

1. **Anthropic IPO at $300-400B (or no IPO through 2028)**: stake mark = $900M-$1.2B = 6-8% of market cap. Still positive but no re-rating catalyst. If Anthropic instead raises another private round at $400B with SKT diluted further (no follow-on participation), stake value compresses to ~$700M = 5% of market cap. **Anthropic option value impairs**.

2. **Korean wireless margin erosion 200-300bps**: MVNO wholesale rate cuts (52%) + lifted device subsidy caps catalyze price competition. Combined with AIDC over-build (1GW Ulsan operating below 60% utilization through 2027), EBITDA stays flat at ₩4.5T rather than recovering to ₩5.5T. Multiple stays at 5x → no expansion.

3. **USIM breach tail liabilities expand**: class action settlements, EU GDPR exposure (some affected users were EU residents), consent-decree compliance costs. Additional ₩300-500B charges through 2027. Net income recovery slips to FY27.

4. **Sovereign AI consortium loss**: SKT eliminated in Phase 2 (June 2026) or Phase 3 (December 2026). LG or Upstage wins. SKT's AIDC narrative loses government anchor. Customer pipeline weakens.

5. **KRW weakens to ₩1,600/USD**: ADR investors absorb FX loss even on flat KRW share price. -10% USD return baseline before any operating performance.
