# AIXA - Aixtron SE

## Summary

Aixtron is being repriced from a cyclical SiC/GaN power-equipment supplier into the dominant MOCVD toolmaker for the InP photonics buildout, but the market has not yet underwritten how complete the application mix has flipped: optoelectronics moved from 12% of equipment revenue in 2024 to 23% in 2025, and Q1 2026 order intake hit 65% optoelectronics — a structural transition compressed into four quarters. The thesis is that AIXA captures a near-monopolistic share of a multi-year InP capacity wave (Lumentum Greensboro, Coherent, Nokia G10-AsP, SMART Photonics, plus three undisclosed leading laser makers) at the same time GaN-on-Si data-center power demand layers on as an independent second leg via NVIDIA's 800V transition expected in 2027. Despite a +138% YTD rally and Q1 2026's -38% EBIT operating deleverage from light-seasonality revenue, AIXA still trades at semis-cycle equipment multiples (~30-40x forward earnings) against customers like LITE/COHR pricing photonics growth at 48-60x — implying the market sees AIXA as a SiC equipment cycle name with a photonics call option rather than a photonics enabler with a SiC tail. Initial conviction medium pending Apr 30 Q1 2026 results validating mix-shift durability, FY guide reaffirmation, and free cash flow trajectory.

## Key Non-consensus Insights

**The mix shift IS the thesis, and it is already happening — not a 2027+ option**: Sell-side consensus models AIXA as a cyclical SiC/GaN power-equipment supplier with a photonics adjacency. The 2024→2025 application-split data ruptures that framing: power equipment fell from 55% to 50% of mix while optoelectronics doubled from 12% to 23%, and Q1 2026 prelim orders printed 65% optoelectronics share. The market is treating the FY2026 €560M revenue guide raise (from €520M) as a power-cycle stabilization story — it is mechanically driven by InP order book expansion that AIXA disclosed but consensus has not yet fully digested. The implication is that 2026 numbers will look like a typical "second leg of a SiC recovery" while the underlying revenue composition will be ~45-55% optoelectronics by year-end, and 2027 will land entirely under a different economic narrative the sell-side is not modeling. This is a business-mix transition pricing event, not a cycle-timing call.

**G10-AsP is the ASML of InP for the 6-inch wafer transition**: The InP industry is migrating from 3-inch and 4-inch wafers to 6-inch ([[Theses/LITE - Lumentum]] Greensboro fab is the canonical reference deployment), and 6-inch InP/AsP epitaxy requires reactor architectures the legacy 3/4-inch installed base cannot retrofit. Aixtron's G10-AsP platform is the only commercially shipping 6-inch InP MOCVD reactor at scale — Nokia's confirmed G10-AsP order, SMART Photonics' fab buildout, and three additional "leading laser maker" wins disclosed in the FY2025 results call effectively give Aixtron sole-source position for the 6-inch transition window. Veeco's Compound Semiconductor exposure (9% of 2025 revenue, guided to 12% in 2026E) is concentrated in mature LED and laser MOCVD on smaller wafer sizes; Veeco does not have a comparable 6-inch InP product on the market today. This is a 2-3 year window of vendor monoculture in the equipment that gates an entire industry transition — economically equivalent to ASML's EUV monopoly during the 7nm-to-5nm transition, scaled to a fraction of the absolute dollar TAM but with similar share dynamics.

**Headline MOCVD market share (VECO 28%, AIXA 24%) understates AIXA's dominance in the InP-specific segment by ~3x**: Public market-share data aggregates all MOCVD applications — LED, laser, power, photonics — into a single "MOCVD equipment" denominator, in which Veeco's mature LED-MOCVD installed base inflates its top-line share. Within InP/photonics-specific MOCVD, customer disclosures and equipment supplier audits put Aixtron at >70% share, and within the 6-inch InP subset specifically (G10-AsP class) the share is approximately 90%+. Kerrisdale Capital's published long thesis triangulates this from the customer side (Lumentum, Coherent, Nokia, SMART Photonics public capacity guidance + IQE epi-foundry buildout). The investor-bias error is that a sell-side analyst pulling "MOCVD market share" from Yole or Mordor Intelligence will see a balanced 24/28/48 (Aixtron / Veeco / others) split and conclude AIXA has commoditized competition risk, when the actual exposed segment AIXA is winning has near-monopoly economics. This understates both pricing power on incoming orders and gross margin defensibility through the cycle.

**AIXA is implicitly long LITE/COHR/NVDA at semis-cycle equipment multiples**: Aixtron sells the picks-and-shovels into the InP capacity wave that Lumentum, Coherent, IQE, and II-VI epi customers are building to feed NVIDIA's CPO transition and the broader 1.6T/3.2T optical transceiver upgrade. [[Theses/LITE - Lumentum]] currently trades at ~50x forward earnings on the high-conviction CPO/EML thesis; [[Theses/NVDA - NVIDIA]] silicon photonics roadmap is priced at premium multiples. Aixtron, as the equipment supplier capturing first-dollar capacity capex (typically 18-24 months ahead of customer revenue ramp), trades at ~30-40x forward earnings on Bloomberg consensus EPS — the SiC/GaN equipment-cycle multiple range. The valuation gap implies the market is pricing AIXA's photonics exposure at ~0% probability of materializing into the equipment book at scale, when the application-mix data already shows it has materialized. Re-rating to a photonics-equipment multiple (40-55x) on consensus 2027 EPS would imply 30-60% upside before any fundamental beat. This is not a derivative valuation argument — it is direct multiple arbitrage between a customer that pays AIXA's invoices and AIXA itself.

**800V data center power is a second independent demand leg, not photonics 2.0**: NVIDIA's 800V architecture transition (publicly targeted for 2027 GPU racks) requires GaN-on-Si and SiC power conversion stages with ~3x the device count of current 48V/54V systems, and current 200mm/300mm GaN-on-Si epi capacity is sized for consumer/auto applications rather than the AI data-center vertical at scale. AIXA's G10-SiC and AIX G5+C platforms are the dominant tools for the GaN-on-Si epi capacity that this requires. The non-consensus point is timing: photonics MOCVD orders are landing in 2026-2027 (capacity for 2028-2029 transceiver shipments), and 800V power MOCVD orders should start landing in late 2026 / 1H 2027 (capacity for 2027-2028 NVIDIA GPU rack shipments). These are uncorrelated demand cycles drawing on the same equipment base, meaning AIXA's revenue growth path through 2027-2028 has two independent S-curves stacking rather than the single SiC-recovery curve sell-side currently models. The bear case requires both cycles to disappoint simultaneously, which has historically been rare.

## Business Model & Product Description

Aixtron designs, manufactures, and supports MOCVD (Metal-Organic Chemical Vapor Deposition) systems — the deposition equipment used to grow compound semiconductor epitaxial layers (InP, GaAs, GaN, SiC, AlN) on wafer substrates. The business is structurally analogous to [[Sectors/Semiconductor Capital Equipment]] within compound semiconductors: Aixtron sells one-time capital equipment ($3-15M per tool) plus recurring revenue from spare parts, consumables, software upgrades, and service contracts that compound across the installed base. The SaaS analog is closer than typical equipment makers because each MOCVD tool ties the customer into Aixtron's recipe library, precursor qualification, and proprietary process recipes — switching cost is high enough that customer churn at the equipment level is rare even when competitor tools are nominally cheaper.

Three product platforms drive ~85% of revenue:

**G10-AsP (InP/GaAs/AsP photonics, 6-inch)**: The flagship 6-inch InP MOCVD reactor, the only commercially shipping platform at scale for the InP/AsP photonics 6-inch transition. Each tool is approximately €4-7M ASP, qualified for laser diodes (EML, DFB), photodetectors, semiconductor optical amplifiers (SOAs), and integrated photonic ICs. Customers include Nokia (announced), SMART Photonics (announced), three undisclosed leading laser/photonics customers (likely subset of Lumentum, Coherent, II-VI/Coherent, Marvell, IQE foundry layer). This platform is the InP/photonics revenue driver and the highest-margin product in the portfolio (estimated 45-55% gross margin).

**G10-SiC (Silicon Carbide power, 200mm)**: The 200mm SiC MOCVD platform serving EV power module manufacturers (BorgWarner, ON Semi, Wolfspeed, ROHM, Infineon partial). Each tool ~$3-5M ASP. This is the cyclical leg — strong 2022-2024 with the EV inflection, then normalized in 2025 with EV demand softness in China and Europe; recovery driven by Wolfspeed restructuring + ROHM India expansion + ON Semi Czech buildout in 2026-2027. SiC is more competitive than InP (Veeco Propel 200mm is direct competitor, plus Chinese entrants 3SBio, Naura at smaller scale). Estimated 30-40% gross margin.

**AIX G5+C and G4+C (GaN-on-Si and GaN-on-Sapphire, 200mm)**: Multi-wafer GaN platforms targeted at GaN-on-Si power electronics (data-center 800V, EV onboard chargers, fast-charging AC adapters) and the legacy GaN-on-Sapphire LED business. The data-center 800V demand layer is the new optionality — historically these tools served LED and consumer fast-charging; the 800V transition reframes them into AI infrastructure equipment. Each tool ~$2-4M ASP. Estimated 35-45% gross margin.

Revenue segmentation (FY2025 actuals):
- **Equipment**: ~€430M (~77% of total) — new tool sales to compound semi fab customers.
- **Aftermarket/Service**: ~€127M (~23% of total) — spare parts, service contracts, recipe library upgrades. Recurring, ~50% gross margin, growing in lockstep with installed base.

Application split of equipment revenue (FY2025): 50% Power (SiC/GaN-on-Si combined), 22% LED, 23% Optoelectronics (InP photonics + lasers), 5% Other. The Q1 2026 65% optoelectronics order intake share is the leading indicator that revenue mix flips materially in 2026-2027.

Geographic revenue mix (FY2025): ~45% Asia (Korea, China, Japan, Taiwan), ~30% Europe (Nokia, SMART Photonics, II-VI/Coherent Saxony fab), ~25% Americas (Lumentum Greensboro, Coherent Sherman, Wolfspeed). Asia weighting reflects China LED legacy + Korea/Taiwan compound semi foundry buildout.

Operating model: ~€135M annual fixed cost (R&D + SG&A + manufacturing overhead), capex €60-80M/year (R&D-intensive, including next-generation reactor platforms), free cash flow margin 12-18% in steady state, swinging materially negative in low-revenue quarters due to the high fixed-cost base. Cash position FY2025 end ~€330M, no debt — balance sheet provides 4-6 quarters of runway through any cyclical trough without external financing.

## Industry Context

The MOCVD equipment industry is structurally a duopoly between Aixtron (Germany) and Veeco (US), with a long tail of smaller players (CCS Equipment, Tokuyama, Atomate, NIPPON SANSO) addressing specific subsegments. Total MOCVD equipment TAM is approximately $1.0-1.2B globally in 2025, and is projected to reach $1.8-2.2B by 2028 driven by three converging cycles:

1. **InP photonics 6-inch transition** (~$300-400M cumulative equipment spend 2026-2028): The InP industry is shifting from 3-4 inch wafers to 6-inch. [[Theses/LITE - Lumentum]] Greensboro is the canonical reference fab; Coherent (Sherman, Allen TX), Nokia (Espoo), SMART Photonics (Eindhoven), II-VI Saxony, and IQE epi-foundry are all building or expanding 6-inch InP capacity. This wave is concentrated 2026-2028 with capacity feeding 2028-2030 photonics revenue.

2. **Data-center 800V power** (~$200-300M cumulative 2026-2028): NVIDIA's 800V transition is the dominant demand pull, but the broader migration of AI rack power architectures from 48V/54V to 400V/800V layers ~2-3x GaN-on-Si epi requirement on top of consumer/auto demand. Tier-1 power management chip makers (Vicor, Delta Electronics, Innoscience, Navitas) are the indirect customer base; their fab partners (TSMC, GlobalFoundries 200mm, Innoscience captive) are the direct equipment buyers.

3. **SiC power normalization + India/Eastern Europe buildout** (~$300-400M cumulative 2026-2028): SiC is past peak hype but normalized at meaningful baseline — Wolfspeed restructuring, ROHM's India $700M fab, ON Semi Czech expansion, plus Chinese internal SiC capacity all drive 200mm SiC MOCVD tool demand at lower growth but durable absolute levels.

Competitive dynamics:

- **Aixtron** (~24% headline MOCVD share, ~70%+ InP-specific share, ~90%+ 6-inch InP G10-AsP share): differentiated on the high-margin InP/photonics segment, exposed in SiC/GaN power (more commodity-like). Strategic emphasis since 2022 has been doubling down on photonics — G10-AsP development, 6-inch reactor architecture, recipe library expansion to laser-specific structures.

- **Veeco** (~28% headline MOCVD share, ~9-12% Compound Semi revenue share within Veeco itself): weighted toward mature LED MOCVD (legacy installed base) and 200mm SiC competing with Aixtron G10-SiC. Less exposed to the 6-inch InP transition — Propel 6-inch InP is on Veeco's roadmap but not shipping at competitive scale as of FY2025. Veeco's CEO commentary on the FY2025 call framed compound semi as "9% to 12% growth into 2026" — directionally consistent with secular but not transformational for Veeco's overall mix.

- **Long tail (CCS, Tokuyama, Atomate, NIPPON SANSO)**: regional / niche players, not credible threats in 6-inch InP or 200mm SiC at scale. Some lab-scale and R&D positions but not high-volume fab-scale equipment.

The value chain from precursors to end products positions Aixtron as the bottleneck:

```
Wafer substrate (SUMCO, Shin-Etsu, Coherent InP wafers)
  → Aixtron MOCVD epi tool (epitaxial growth)
    → Compound semi fab (Lumentum, Coherent, IQE epi-foundry)
      → Device fab (Lumentum laser fab, Coherent EML fab)
        → Module/transceiver maker (Marvell, Ciena, Innolight)
          → Hyperscaler / network OEM (AWS, Microsoft, Cisco, Nokia)
```

Aixtron sits at the second-most-leveraged position in this chain (behind only the wafer suppliers). The leverage manifests as: capacity expansion downstream requires Aixtron's tools 18-24 months ahead of revenue, so AIXA orders are the leading indicator for 2027-2028 photonics revenue at LITE/COHR.

### MOCVD physics, InP specialization, and AIXA's moat against new entrants

MOCVD (Metal-Organic Chemical Vapor Deposition) is epitaxy: ultrapure metal-organic precursor gases (TMGa, TMIn) and hydrides (PH₃, AsH₃, NH₃) are introduced into a 500-1,600°C reactor, undergo pyrolysis, and deposit one atomic layer per pass onto a wafer substrate. Growth is deliberately slow (2-5 hours per run) because deposited atoms must align to the substrate's lattice within sub-Angstrom tolerance — sub-nanometer mismatch propagates as crystal defects that destroy device yield. A single laser or HEMT device stacks 30-200+ layers, each <10 nm to several microns, with different thermal expansion coefficients; the operator must compensate for thermal stresses that bow and crack the wafer at scale. This inverts CMOS plain-silicon manufacturing — for digital logic, lithography is the constraint and the wafer is easy; for compound semis, deposition itself is the bottleneck.

**InP/AsP epitaxy is the hardest MOCVD variant**. Phosphorus is volatile (PH₃ cracks easily, contaminates chambers, requires higher overpressure than AsH₃ or NH₃); lattice and thermal mismatches with practical substrates capped historical wafer sizes at 3-4 inches; 6-inch is happening now and is non-trivial; 8-inch remains research-stage. The G10-AsP is purpose-built for As/P chemistry — distinct precursor injection geometry, chamber materials, and process-control loops from G10-GaN or G10-SiC. A photonics customer cannot run InP recipes on a GaN tool; conversion is not economically attractive. This is the structural reason the InP installed base is a separate market from GaN/SiC despite all three being MOCVD.

**Competitive comparison — what's actually shipping in the InP segment**:

|---|---|---|---|
| **AIXA** | G10-AsP | 6-inch (150mm) InP/AsP | Tool of record; multi-customer qualification |
| Veeco | Propel (InP variant) | 4-inch primarily | Single-wafer architecture; not at 6-inch competitive scale; Q4-2024 €28M Epiluvac/NuFlare impairment after 2-year delay |

| AMEC (China) | Prismo | Mixed | Commodity LED only — declined from 55% of AMEC revenue (2017) to 7% (2023); pivoted to etch |

PVD (sputtering) is non-epitaxial and lattice-incoherent; ALD deposits sub-nanometer films but cannot do micron-scale III-V stacks; plain CVD without metal-organic precursors lacks precursor purity for compound device-grade epitaxy. For photonics-grade InP devices, MOCVD is the only economically viable process — the across-deposition-type comparison is moot.

**AIXA's moat against deposition-savvy new entrants — six layers**:

1. **Multi-wafer planetary reactor IP** (25+ years of refinement). AIXA's planetary tool processes 5×200mm or 9×150mm wafers per batch with single-wafer uniformity. Veeco TurboDisc relies on high-rotation single-wafer architecture; the Veeco VP of Technology himself acknowledged in industry interview that *"the high speed of rotation can actually introduce some mechanical stress on the wafer and lead to wafer defects, nonuniformity, thermal issues and so forth that makes it much more challenging to deliver the same level of epi performance that the Aixtron tool has."* Veeco's 300mm GaN tool is forced to single-wafer showerhead, sacrificing throughput. The planetary moat is process know-how, not patentable — competitors cannot copy by inspection.
2. **In-situ cleaning** (G10 family). Integrated reactor self-cleaning between runs. Competitor tools require manual offline cleaning, a yield-loss risk and TCO disadvantage. Asian SiC fabs achieving 99% yield on AIXA tools where comparable Chinese fabs run 50% historic yields on alternative tools.
3. **Process control depth**. Spectroscopic ellipsometry (layer thickness), reflectometry (growth rate), and deflectometry (wafer stress) integrated as in-situ feedback loops. The recipe library accumulated since 1983 — thousands of customer-tuned process windows for specific device types — is the highest-value IP. A new entrant copying an AIXA tool would still need 5-10 years of customer co-development to match production-grade recipes.
4. **Customer switching cost**. Per the Veeco VP of Technology interview itself: *"You find that a company invests in a platform; it's everything from spares, layout of the fab, training of the teams. The operators, the scientists that run the tool, they get to understand their equipment very well. Shifting to a different platform really changes all of those factors... how you specifically achieve the optimal results on the system is quite different between the [Veeco and Aixtron] platforms. That does represent a barrier to entry."* Switching costs to displace an installed AIXA tool exceed the marginal cost of adding capacity on a new AIXA tool.
5. **Wafer-size leadership lock-in**. AIXA was first to 200mm GaN (2024 Infineon ramp), first to 6-inch InP (G10-AsP). Each migration to a larger wafer renders smaller-wafer competitors uncompetitive on TCO; customers scrap 150mm in favor of 200mm rather than retrofit. Pushing the wafer-size frontier is the structural mechanism that prevents competitor catch-up.
6. **China localization risk is bounded for InP specifically**. Domestic Chinese MOCVD attempts (Naura, AMEC, 3SBio) have succeeded in commodity LED but failed in advanced GaN-on-Si, 200mm SiC, and InP. Aixtron has never had a tool reverse-engineered in China — the planetary multi-wafer design requires 20+ years of process IP plus access to high-quality Western gas-delivery and process-control modules. For 6-inch InP specifically, the chemistry is harder than SiC (which Chinese players have struggled with), and the customer base is largely ex-China (Lumentum, Coherent, Nokia, SMART Photonics).

**Net moat assessment**: AIXA's monopoly economics in 6-inch InP MOCVD persist for ~2-3 years (2026-2028) on process know-how + planetary reactor IP + customer integration depth. This is not equivalent to ASML's EUV monopoly (physically irreproducible) — AIXA's moat is a 5-10 year window before a determined, well-capitalized competitor (Veeco at 300mm GaN, possibly a future Chinese entrant) could ship a competitive 6-inch InP product. Outside that window, AIXA retains share leadership through scale economics + recipe library lock-in but with shared-vendor pricing dynamics. Thesis monopoly economics are 2-3 years; structural share leadership extends through 2030+.

Pricing power in InP MOCVD has expanded materially in 2024-2026 — G10-AsP ASPs estimated to have risen ~15-25% in this period through tool feature richness (in-situ metrology integration, multi-wafer carrier upgrades) plus genuine pricing power in a single-vendor segment. This contrasts with the 2018-2022 LED MOCVD cycle where ASP compression was severe (China LED overcapacity drove tool ASPs down 30%+). The current InP cycle has structurally tighter customer concentration (single-digit customers vs. dozens of LED fabs) which historically supports better pricing discipline.
