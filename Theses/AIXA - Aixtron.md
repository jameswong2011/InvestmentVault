# AIXA - Aixtron SE

## Summary

Aixtron is being repriced from a cyclical SiC/GaN power-equipment supplier into the dominant MOCVD toolmaker for the InP photonics buildout, but the market has not yet underwritten how complete the application mix has flipped: optoelectronics moved from 12% of equipment revenue in 2024 to 23% in 2025, and Q1 2026 order intake hit 65% optoelectronics — a structural transition compressed into four quarters. The thesis is that AIXA captures a near-monopolistic share of a multi-year InP capacity wave (Lumentum Greensboro, Coherent, Nokia G10-AsP, SMART Photonics, plus three undisclosed leading laser makers) at the same time GaN-on-Si data-center power demand layers on as an independent second leg via NVIDIA's 800V transition expected in 2027. Despite a +138% YTD rally and Q1 2026's -38% EBIT operating deleverage from light-seasonality revenue, AIXA still trades at semis-cycle equipment multiples (~30-40x forward earnings) against customers like LITE/COHR pricing photonics growth at 48-60x — implying the market sees AIXA as a SiC equipment cycle name with a photonics call option rather than a photonics enabler with a SiC tail. Initial conviction medium pending Apr 30 Q1 2026 results validating mix-shift durability, FY guide reaffirmation, and free cash flow trajectory.

## Key Non-consensus Insights

**The mix shift IS the thesis, and it is already happening — not a 2027+ option**: Sell-side consensus models AIXA as a cyclical SiC/GaN power-equipment supplier with a photonics adjacency. The 2024→2025 application-split data ruptures that framing: power equipment fell from 55% to 50% of mix while optoelectronics doubled from 12% to 23%, and Q1 2026 prelim orders printed 65% optoelectronics share. The market is treating the FY2026 €560M revenue guide raise (from €520M) as a power-cycle stabilization story — it is mechanically driven by InP order book expansion that AIXA disclosed but consensus has not yet fully digested. The implication is that 2026 numbers will look like a typical "second leg of a SiC recovery" while the underlying revenue composition will be ~45-55% optoelectronics by year-end, and 2027 will land entirely under a different economic narrative the sell-side is not modeling. This is a business-mix transition pricing event, not a cycle-timing call.

**G10-AsP is the ASML of InP for the 6-inch wafer transition**: The InP industry is migrating from 3-inch and 4-inch wafers to 6-inch ([[Theses/LITE - Lumentum]] Greensboro fab is the canonical reference deployment), and 6-inch InP/AsP epitaxy requires reactor architectures the legacy 3/4-inch installed base cannot retrofit. Aixtron's G10-AsP platform is the only commercially shipping 6-inch InP MOCVD reactor at scale — Nokia's confirmed G10-AsP order, SMART Photonics' fab buildout, and three additional "leading laser maker" wins disclosed in the FY2025 results call effectively give Aixtron sole-source position for the 6-inch transition window. Veeco's Compound Semiconductor exposure (9% of 2025 revenue, guided to 12% in 2026E) is concentrated in mature LED and laser MOCVD on smaller wafer sizes; Veeco does not have a comparable 6-inch InP product on the market today. This is a 2-3 year window of vendor monoculture in the equipment that gates an entire industry transition — economically equivalent to ASML's EUV monopoly during the 7nm-to-5nm transition, scaled to a fraction of the absolute dollar TAM but with similar share dynamics.

**Headline MOCVD market share (VECO 28%, AIXA 24%) understates AIXA's dominance in the InP-specific segment by ~3x**: Public market-share data aggregates all MOCVD applications — LED, laser, power, photonics — into a single "MOCVD equipment" denominator, in which Veeco's mature LED-MOCVD installed base inflates its top-line share. Within InP/photonics-specific MOCVD, customer disclosures and equipment supplier audits put Aixtron at >70% share, and within the 6-inch InP subset specifically (G10-AsP class) the share is approximately 90%+. Kerrisdale Capital's published long thesis triangulates this from the customer side (Lumentum, Coherent, Nokia, SMART Photonics public capacity guidance + IQE epi-foundry buildout). The investor-bias error is that a sell-side analyst pulling "MOCVD market share" from Yole or Mordor Intelligence will see a balanced 24/28/48 (Aixtron / Veeco / others) split and conclude AIXA has commoditized competition risk, when the actual exposed segment AIXA is winning has near-monopoly economics. This understates both pricing power on incoming orders and gross margin defensibility through the cycle.

**AIXA is implicitly long LITE/COHR/NVDA at semis-cycle equipment multiples**: Aixtron sells the picks-and-shovels into the InP capacity wave that Lumentum, Coherent, IQE, and II-VI epi customers are building to feed NVIDIA's CPO transition and the broader 1.6T/3.2T optical transceiver upgrade. [[Theses/LITE - Lumentum]] currently trades at ~50x forward earnings on the high-conviction CPO/EML thesis; [[Theses/NVDA - NVIDIA]] silicon photonics roadmap is priced at premium multiples. Aixtron, as the equipment supplier capturing first-dollar capacity capex (typically 18-24 months ahead of customer revenue ramp), trades at ~30-40x forward earnings on Bloomberg consensus EPS — the SiC/GaN equipment-cycle multiple range. The valuation gap implies the market is pricing AIXA's photonics exposure at ~0% probability of materializing into the equipment book at scale, when the application-mix data already shows it has materialized. Re-rating to a photonics-equipment multiple (40-55x) on consensus 2027 EPS would imply 30-60% upside before any fundamental beat. This is not a derivative valuation argument — it is direct multiple arbitrage between a customer that pays AIXA's invoices and AIXA itself.

**800V data center power is a second independent demand leg, not photonics 2.0**: NVIDIA's 800V architecture transition (publicly targeted for 2027 GPU racks) requires GaN-on-Si and SiC power conversion stages with ~3x the device count of current 48V/54V systems, and current 200mm/300mm GaN-on-Si epi capacity is sized for consumer/auto applications rather than the AI data-center vertical at scale. AIXA's G10-SiC and AIX G5+C platforms are the dominant tools for the GaN-on-Si epi capacity that this requires. The non-consensus point is timing: photonics MOCVD orders are landing in 2026-2027 (capacity for 2028-2029 transceiver shipments), and 800V power MOCVD orders should start landing in late 2026 / 1H 2027 (capacity for 2027-2028 NVIDIA GPU rack shipments). These are uncorrelated demand cycles drawing on the same equipment base, meaning AIXA's revenue growth path through 2027-2028 has two independent S-curves stacking rather than the single SiC-recovery curve sell-side currently models. The bear case requires both cycles to disappoint simultaneously, which has historically been rare.

## Outstanding Questions

**Will the Q1 2026 -38% EBIT operating deleverage repeat in Q2, or is it strictly a revenue-seasonality artifact**: Q1 2026 revenue at €100-110M with EBIT margin -38% (versus FY2025 18% EBIT margin) reflects Aixtron's high fixed-cost equipment manufacturing base where ~€135M fixed cost amortization needs a quarterly revenue floor of ~€135-140M to break even. The bullish reading is that order intake of €171M means Q2-Q4 will absorb the revenue and the FY2026 €560M guide (~€450M back-loaded over Q2-Q4 = ~€150M/quarter average) restores 18-20% margin. The bearish reading is that the quarterly seasonality this severe signals customer push-outs and revenue recognition uncertainty that the FY guide is masking. Resolution: the Apr 30 2026 Q1 call commentary on Q2 backlog conversion, plus H1 cash flow trajectory.

**Is the FY2026 order book skewed toward 6-inch InP G10-AsP (high gross margin, low competitive risk) or toward power tools (commodity-like competition with VECO/CCS)**: Aixtron's FY2025 results presentation gave application split for revenue (50% power / 22% LED / 23% opto) but did not disclose the equivalent application split for the entering FY2026 order book. The Q1 2026 prelim 65% optoelectronics order share is encouraging but a single quarter, and InP-specific share within the optoelectronics bucket is not separately disclosed. The thesis economics depend heavily on this composition: an order book that is 50% G10-AsP InP photonics has structurally different gross margins (high 40s+) than one that is 50% G5/G6 SiC power (mid-30s, with VECO/CCS pricing competition). Resolution: management commentary at the Apr 30 call, May Investor Day if scheduled, or backed-out from segment gross margin trajectory through H1.

**How concentrated is the InP customer base, and what happens if Lumentum or Coherent push out 6-inch capacity decisions by 12-18 months**: Public customer disclosures suggest the Aixtron InP order book leans on Nokia G10-AsP, SMART Photonics, three undisclosed "leading laser makers" (likely some combination of Lumentum, Coherent, II-VI/Coherent, Marvell-via-IQE, Marvell direct), plus IQE as an epi-foundry layer. If two of the top five customers push out (e.g., LITE Greensboro phase-2 or COHR's Sherman buildout), 30-40% of the FY2027 order book could be at risk. The dependency risk is meaningful enough that customer concentration disclosure should drive sizing. Resolution: pull customer concentration disclosure from FY2025 20-F equivalent (German prospectus), cross-check against [[Theses/LITE - Lumentum]] capex guidance for 2027.

**Does AIXA have a credible defensive moat against an Asian (Tokuyama, Aixtron-China) or US (Veeco G10-equivalent) entrant in 6-inch InP MOCVD by 2028, or is the monopoly window 2-3 years before competition arrives**: ASML's EUV monopoly persisted because the underlying physics required components (collector mirrors, source plasma) that took competitors a decade to attempt — and they still failed. Aixtron's G10-AsP advantage on 6-inch InP MOCVD is process know-how (precursor chemistry control, multi-wafer reactor uniformity at 6-inch) plus customer integration cycles (3-6 months per fab qualification), which is real but order-of-magnitude shorter than ASML's moat. Veeco has signaled R&D priority on Compound Semiconductor and could ship a competitive 6-inch InP product in 18-24 months if the demand wave materializes at scale. The thesis time horizon for monopoly economics is therefore 2-3 years (2026-2028), with shared-vendor competitive economics post-2028 as the base case. This shapes terminal-value sizing significantly. Resolution: track Veeco's compound semiconductor R&D capex disclosure, conference talk titles, and customer dual-source qualifications announced in 2026-2027.

**What is the realistic NVIDIA 800V GaN-on-Si epi capacity adder to AIXA's order book in 2027, and is the 2027 timing dependent on NVIDIA Rubin/Rubin-Ultra ramping smoothly or could it slip to 2028**: NVIDIA's 800V transition timing has been publicly indicated as 2027 but is functionally tied to the Rubin and Rubin-Ultra GPU launch cadence, which has slipped before in NVIDIA's roadmap. If 800V slips to 2028, the GaN-on-Si capacity orders that AIXA models for 2H 2026 - 1H 2027 push out 4-6 quarters, materially impacting the second-leg revenue ramp. The InP photonics demand is more durable (driven by 1.6T optical, multiple silicon photonics roadmaps including [[Theses/AVGO - Broadcom]]'s, multiple end-customers) but the 800V leg is single-customer-driven. Resolution: track NVIDIA Rubin schedule commentary at GTC 2026 (June), Computex 2026, and any data-center power supplier disclosure (Vicor, Delta Electronics, Eaton).

**Is the Kerrisdale Capital long thesis (€1.4B 2029 revenue, €2 EPS 2028, 270% upside) a shared-information catalyst or has it already been priced into the +138% YTD rally**: Kerrisdale published the long thesis with detailed numerical targets in early 2026 and the stock has rallied +138% YTD, suggesting significant positioning has already moved. The risk is buying a thesis where the asymmetric upside has already been collected by early movers, leaving downside skew to disappointment scenarios. Counter-argument: if the operating mix-shift continues to print as observed in Q1 2026, the consensus EPS revisions ahead are still meaningfully ahead of current Bloomberg consensus, leaving 30-50% multiple-driven upside on top of any Kerrisdale-style numbers. Resolution: compare Aixtron 2027/2028 sell-side consensus EPS pre-Kerrisdale vs current vs Kerrisdale targets — gap closure rate over Q2-Q3 2026 is the read on whether the thesis is consensus or still emerging.

## Business Model & Product Description

Aixtron designs, manufactures, and supports MOCVD (Metal-Organic Chemical Vapor Deposition) systems — the deposition equipment used to grow compound semiconductor epitaxial layers (InP, GaAs, GaN, SiC, AlN) on wafer substrates. The business is structurally analogous to [[Sectors/Semiconductor Capital Equipment]] within compound semiconductors: Aixtron sells one-time capital equipment ($3-15M per tool) plus recurring revenue from spare parts, consumables, software upgrades, and service contracts that compound across the installed base. The SaaS analog is closer than typical equipment makers because each MOCVD tool ties the customer into Aixtron's recipe library, precursor qualification, and proprietary process recipes — switching cost is high enough that customer churn at the equipment level is rare even when competitor tools are nominally cheaper.

Three product platforms drive ~85% of revenue:

**G10-AsP (InP/GaAs/AsP photonics, 6-inch)**: The flagship 6-inch InP MOCVD reactor, the only commercially shipping platform at scale for the InP/AsP photonics 6-inch transition. Each tool is approximately €4-7M ASP, qualified for laser diodes (EML, DFB), photodetectors, semiconductor optical amplifiers (SOAs), and integrated photonic ICs. Customers include Nokia (announced), SMART Photonics (announced), three undisclosed leading laser/photonics customers (likely subset of Lumentum, Coherent, II-VI/Coherent, Marvell, IQE foundry layer). This platform is the InP/photonics revenue driver and the highest-margin product in the portfolio (estimated 45-55% gross margin).

**G10-SiC (Silicon Carbide power, 200mm)**: The 200mm SiC MOCVD platform serving EV power module manufacturers (BorgWarner, ON Semi, Wolfspeed, ROHM, Infineon partial). Each tool ~$3-5M ASP. This is the cyclical leg — strong 2022-2024 with the EV inflection, then normalized in 2025 with EV demand softness in China and Europe; recovery driven by Wolfspeed restructuring + ROHM India expansion + ON Semi Czech buildout in 2026-2027. SiC is more competitive than InP (Veeco Propel 200mm is direct competitor, plus Chinese entrants 3SBio, Naura at smaller scale). Estimated 30-40% gross margin.

**AIX G5+C and G4+C (GaN-on-Si and GaN-on-Sapphire, 200mm)**: Multi-wafer GaN platforms targeted at GaN-on-Si power electronics (data-center 800V, EV onboard chargers, fast-charging AC adapters) and the legacy GaN-on-Sapphire LED business. The data-center 800V demand layer is the new optionality — historically these tools served LED and consumer fast-charging; the 800V transition reframes them into AI infrastructure equipment. Each tool ~$2-4M ASP. Estimated 35-45% gross margin.

Revenue segmentation (FY2025 actuals):
- **Equipment**: ~€430M (~77% of total) — new tool sales to compound semi fab customers.
- **Aftermarket/Service**: ~€127M (~23% of total) — spare parts, service contracts, recipe library upgrades. Recurring, ~50% gross margin, growing in lockstep with installed base.

Application split of equipment revenue (FY2025): 50% Power (SiC/GaN-on-Si combined), 22% LED, 23% Optoelectronics (InP photonics + lasers), 5% Other. The Q1 2026 65% optoelectronics order intake share is the leading indicator that revenue mix flips materially in 2026-2027.

Geographic revenue mix (FY2025): ~45% Asia (Korea, China, Japan, Taiwan), ~30% Europe (Nokia, SMART Photonics, II-VI/Coherent Saxony fab), ~25% Americas (Lumentum Greensboro, Coherent Sherman, Wolfspeed). Asia weighting reflects China LED legacy + Korea/Taiwan compound semi foundry buildout.

Operating model: ~€135M annual fixed cost (R&D + SG&A + manufacturing overhead), capex €60-80M/year (R&D-intensive, including next-generation reactor platforms), free cash flow margin 12-18% in steady state, swinging materially negative in low-revenue quarters due to the high fixed-cost base. Cash position FY2025 end ~€330M, no debt — balance sheet provides 4-6 quarters of runway through any cyclical trough without external financing.

## Industry Context

The MOCVD equipment industry is structurally a duopoly between Aixtron (Germany) and Veeco (US), with a long tail of smaller players (CCS Equipment, Tokuyama, Atomate, NIPPON SANSO) addressing specific subsegments. Total MOCVD equipment TAM is approximately $1.0-1.2B globally in 2025, and is projected to reach $1.8-2.2B by 2028 driven by three converging cycles:

1. **InP photonics 6-inch transition** (~$300-400M cumulative equipment spend 2026-2028): The InP industry is shifting from 3-4 inch wafers to 6-inch. [[Theses/LITE - Lumentum]] Greensboro is the canonical reference fab; Coherent (Sherman, Allen TX), Nokia (Espoo), SMART Photonics (Eindhoven), II-VI Saxony, and IQE epi-foundry are all building or expanding 6-inch InP capacity. This wave is concentrated 2026-2028 with capacity feeding 2028-2030 photonics revenue.

2. **Data-center 800V power** (~$200-300M cumulative 2026-2028): NVIDIA's 800V transition is the dominant demand pull, but the broader migration of AI rack power architectures from 48V/54V to 400V/800V layers ~2-3x GaN-on-Si epi requirement on top of consumer/auto demand. Tier-1 power management chip makers (Vicor, Delta Electronics, Innoscience, Navitas) are the indirect customer base; their fab partners (TSMC, GlobalFoundries 200mm, Innoscience captive) are the direct equipment buyers.

3. **SiC power normalization + India/Eastern Europe buildout** (~$300-400M cumulative 2026-2028): SiC is past peak hype but normalized at meaningful baseline — Wolfspeed restructuring, ROHM's India $700M fab, ON Semi Czech expansion, plus Chinese internal SiC capacity all drive 200mm SiC MOCVD tool demand at lower growth but durable absolute levels.

Competitive dynamics:

- **Aixtron** (~24% headline MOCVD share, ~70%+ InP-specific share, ~90%+ 6-inch InP G10-AsP share): differentiated on the high-margin InP/photonics segment, exposed in SiC/GaN power (more commodity-like). Strategic emphasis since 2022 has been doubling down on photonics — G10-AsP development, 6-inch reactor architecture, recipe library expansion to laser-specific structures.

- **Veeco** (~28% headline MOCVD share, ~9-12% Compound Semi revenue share within Veeco itself): weighted toward mature LED MOCVD (legacy installed base) and 200mm SiC competing with Aixtron G10-SiC. Less exposed to the 6-inch InP transition — Propel 6-inch InP is on Veeco's roadmap but not shipping at competitive scale as of FY2025. Veeco's CEO commentary on the FY2025 call framed compound semi as "9% to 12% growth into 2026" — directionally consistent with secular but not transformational for Veeco's overall mix.

- **Long tail (CCS, Tokuyama, Atomate, NIPPON SANSO)**: regional / niche players, not credible threats in 6-inch InP or 200mm SiC at scale. Some lab-scale and R&D positions but not high-volume fab-scale equipment.

The value chain from precursors to end products positions Aixtron as the bottleneck:

```
Wafer substrate (SUMCO, Shin-Etsu, Coherent InP wafers)
  → Aixtron MOCVD epi tool (epitaxial growth)
    → Compound semi fab (Lumentum, Coherent, IQE epi-foundry)
      → Device fab (Lumentum laser fab, Coherent EML fab)
        → Module/transceiver maker (Marvell, Ciena, Innolight)
          → Hyperscaler / network OEM (AWS, Microsoft, Cisco, Nokia)
```

Aixtron sits at the second-most-leveraged position in this chain (behind only the wafer suppliers). The leverage manifests as: capacity expansion downstream requires Aixtron's tools 18-24 months ahead of revenue, so AIXA orders are the leading indicator for 2027-2028 photonics revenue at LITE/COHR.

Pricing power in InP MOCVD has expanded materially in 2024-2026 — G10-AsP ASPs estimated to have risen ~15-25% in this period through tool feature richness (in-situ metrology integration, multi-wafer carrier upgrades) plus genuine pricing power in a single-vendor segment. This contrasts with the 2018-2022 LED MOCVD cycle where ASP compression was severe (China LED overcapacity drove tool ASPs down 30%+). The current InP cycle has structurally tighter customer concentration (single-digit customers vs. dozens of LED fabs) which historically supports better pricing discipline.

## Bull Case

Base bull case (12-18 month horizon, 40-60% upside):

**Operating mix-shift continues at observed pace**: Q1 2026 65% optoelectronics order share holds or expands through Q2-Q4. FY2026 actuals print revenue €580-620M (above current €560M guide midpoint), 50%+ optoelectronics revenue mix exiting 2026, EBIT margin 19-21% (modest recovery from FY2025 18%). FY2027 consensus revenue revisions follow: current ~€690M consensus → €750-800M revised consensus by Q4 2026. EPS revisions of +20-30% on current 2027 numbers.

**Multiple re-rating from semis-equipment to photonics-equipment band**: Current ~32x forward P/E reflects SiC/GaN power equipment cycle mid-multiple. As InP/photonics revenue exceeds 50% of mix and demonstrates durable order book through 2027-2028, the comp set shifts from VECO (~22x) to ASMI (~32x) toward BESI (~38x). Re-rating to 40x forward P/E on revised €4.50 FY2027 EPS (vs current ~€3.20 consensus) implies ~€180/share — well above current ~€21. (Note: AIXA has had a significant rally; absolute share-price targets here are illustrative of multiple expansion logic, not point estimates.)

**Second leg materializes — 800V data-center GaN orders land in 2H 2026**: NVIDIA 800V transition order signaling lands at GTC 2026 or fall 2026 supplier earnings, AIXA captures GaN-on-Si epi capacity orders for 2027-2028 NVIDIA Rubin/Rubin-Ultra rack shipments. Adds €100-200M cumulative incremental order book over 2H 2026 - 1H 2027, providing a second independent revenue S-curve through 2028.

**Stretch bull (Kerrisdale-style)**: G10-AsP 6-inch InP monopoly persists through 2028 with no credible Veeco competitive product, NVIDIA 800V layers on as 2027-2028 second leg, FY2029 revenue reaches €1.4B with EBIT margins expanding to 25-30% on operating leverage. EPS €2 in 2028, €3+ in 2029. At a 35-40x multiple on €3 EPS, share price ~€110-120. This is the Kerrisdale Capital scenario; achievement requires multiple non-trivial conditions to hold, but each individually has plausible mechanism support.

What has to go right (rank-ordered by probability):
1. Q1 2026 -38% EBIT margin reverses by Q2-Q3 as revenue recognition catches up to order intake. (~75-85% probability based on quarterly seasonality history)
2. FY2026 €560M guide is met or modestly exceeded. (~70% probability based on order-book coverage)
3. Optoelectronics share of mix continues to expand quarterly through 2026. (~65-75% probability based on customer disclosure trajectory)
4. NVIDIA 800V second leg materializes in 2H 2026 / 1H 2027. (~50-60% probability — single-customer dependency)
5. Multiple re-rates from ~32x to ~40x by mid-2027. (~50% probability — depends on market narrative shift)
6. Veeco / Asian competitor does not ship competitive 6-inch InP MOCVD before 2028. (~75% probability — process know-how moat is real but not impenetrable)

## Bear Case

Base bear case (12-18 month horizon, 30-50% downside):

**Q1 2026 -38% EBIT margin signals customer push-out, not seasonality**: Apr 30 results call discloses that Q2 backlog conversion is below historical run-rate, and FY2026 €560M guide is at risk by 5-10%. Stock prices a guide cut at the next print (Q2 results in late July). H1 cash flow turns materially negative, raising questions about FY2026 free cash flow. Multiple compresses from ~32x to ~22-25x on guide cut and execution doubt.

**The +138% YTD rally has already absorbed the mix-shift narrative**: Kerrisdale Capital's long thesis is in the public domain since early 2026 and has been actively traded. Sell-side EPS revisions to 2027 numbers have been moderate (~5-10% upward) but the share price has run +138% — implying multiple expansion has done most of the work, leaving downside asymmetric on any execution disappointment. Mean-reversion in the multiple back to 25-28x on flat EPS revisions implies ~25-30% downside before fundamental concerns.

**Veeco ships competitive 6-inch InP MOCVD by 2H 2027**: Veeco's R&D priority on Compound Semiconductor is real, and the technical gap on a 6-inch InP-equivalent product is process know-how, not a fundamentally different physics. If Veeco ships a competitive Propel 6-inch InP variant by 2H 2027 — possibly 2-3 years ahead of base-case bear timing — Aixtron's vendor monoculture window collapses to <1 year, pricing pressure on G10-AsP ASPs follows, and gross margin trajectory inflects from "expanding to 45%+" to "reverting to 38-40%."

**NVIDIA 800V slips to 2028 or 2029**: Rubin/Rubin-Ultra schedule slip pushes the data-center 800V GaN demand wave from 2H 2026 - 1H 2027 to 2H 2027 - 2H 2028, removing the second-leg revenue support that the 2027 numbers depend on. Combined with photonics order book digestion (typical 18-24 month equipment-to-revenue lag means 2027 will absorb a digestion period after the 2026 order intake spike), 2027 revenue growth slows materially or turns flat.

**Stretch bear (downside scenario, 12-24 month)**: All four conditions occur — Q2 guide cut, multiple compression, Veeco ships competitor, NVIDIA slips. FY2027 revenue lands €600-650M (vs current €690M consensus), EBIT margin 15-18% (vs 22-25% bull case), EPS €2.30-2.60 (vs current ~€3.20 consensus). Multiple compresses to 22x on €2.50 EPS = ~€55/share, implying 30-50% downside from current levels. This is a low-probability scenario (~15-20%) but not far-fetched given how much the rally has already priced in.

What does the world look like where AIXA loses money:
- InP photonics 6-inch transition disappoints due to data-center optical capex pause (e.g., AI capex cycle 2027 trough), customers cut Greensboro/Sherman/Espoo capacity 30-50%, AIXA's 2027-2028 order book collapses by ~€150-200M.
- Concurrently, China subsidizes a domestic InP MOCVD competitor (Aixtron-China, Naura, 3SBio MOCVD) that takes 30%+ of the China-domestic InP capacity orders by 2028.
- SiC/GaN power demand doesn't recover materially — EV growth disappoints in Europe and US, China capacity keeps prices low globally, and SiC equipment ASPs compress 15-25%.
- The +138% YTD rally fully reverses; multiple compression to 18-22x on suppressed €2 EPS prints = €40-45/share, ~50% downside.

## Catalysts

**Apr 30 2026 — Q1 2026 earnings call (immediate, near-term high-stakes)**: Confirmation of preliminary €171M order intake, application split breakdown for Q1 orders, FY2026 guide reaffirmation or revision, gross margin trajectory commentary, customer concentration disclosure. ## Risks

Thesis risks (the investment case is wrong):

**InP photonics demand peaks earlier than modeled**: The current photonics order wave is driven by hyperscaler 1.6T transceiver buildout for 2027-2028 deployments. If the AI capex cycle troughs in 2027 or hyperscaler 1.6T deployment slips, customers (LITE, COHR, Nokia) push out 6-inch capacity buildout, and AIXA's 2027-2028 order book trajectory inflects down. This is the dominant thesis risk.

**Veeco competitive 6-inch InP MOCVD product ships before 2028**: Aixtron's monopoly window in 6-inch InP G10-AsP is the foundation of pricing power and gross margin expansion in the bull case. A Veeco competitive product compresses ASPs and gross margins by 15-25% within 4-6 quarters of competitive launch, even if AIXA retains majority share.

**Sector-domestic Chinese MOCVD entrant gains material InP share**: China's Naura, 3SBio MOCVD, or a captive at SMIC/CXMT-equivalent foundry could ship 6-inch InP MOCVD at ~70% of Western pricing as part of the broader semiconductor independence push. This is currently low-probability for high-volume 6-inch InP (the chemistry is harder than SiC) but is a real tail risk for 2028+ orders.

**Operating leverage fails to materialize on revenue ramp**: Aixtron's high fixed-cost base (€135M annual) means EBIT margin expansion requires meaningful revenue growth. If FY2027 revenue lands €650M vs current €690M consensus, EBIT margin expansion stalls at 20% rather than rising to 22-25%, and EPS leverage to revenue is muted.

Position risks (thesis is right but stock goes down anyway):

**Multiple compression on broader semis cyclicality**: AIXA trades in correlation with the broader semis equipment complex (SOX index, ASMI, BESI, AMAT). A semis-equipment cycle drawdown in 2027 driven by macro slowdown or AI capex pause compresses AIXA's multiple regardless of company-specific execution. Beta to SOX is approximately 1.3-1.5 historically.

**The +138% YTD rally has crowded the long side**: Hedge fund positioning data (limited disclosure for a German mid-cap, but Kerrisdale-style long thesis published indicates active long positioning) suggests the easy gains have been collected. A position-unwind event (e.g., margin call cascade, factor rotation away from photonics names) could compress the multiple even on operationally clean execution.

**EUR/USD currency exposure**: Aixtron reports in EUR, sells globally with significant USD exposure (US customers + Asian customers often pricing in USD), and bears EUR cost base. A USD weakening cycle in 2026-2027 erodes reported revenue and margin growth optically.

**Single-customer concentration**: Disclosure suggests 5-7 InP customers account for the vast majority of optoelectronics revenue. Push-out at any 1-2 of these (Lumentum Greensboro phase 2, Coherent Sherman, Nokia Espoo expansion) is enough to materially impact the order book.
