# TSM — Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company

## Summary

TSMC is a functional monopoly on leading-edge compute priced as a cyclical foundry. 92% share of advanced nodes (≤7nm), ~58.8% gross margin in Q1 2026, and 20% N2 price premium over N3 confirm the pricing-power regime has arrived — yet consensus still models TSMC on capex cyclicality rather than monopoly rents. The non-consensus angle is CoWoS and COUPE as separable revenue lines (advanced-packaging + photonics TAM expansion) plus A16 backside-power-rail exclusivity to NVDA Feynman (2027) that deepens customer lock-in beyond the wafer contract. Medium conviction, not high: Taiwan tail risk is real, AAPL+NVDA ≈ 40% revenue concentration is unprecedented for a $1T+ company, and the Arizona $165B expansion dilutes margins 2-3pts by 2030. The investment case is durability-of-monopoly over 5 years, not a 2026 earnings beat.

## Key Non-consensus Insights

### 1. CoWoS is a $10B+ separable revenue annuity, not a rounding error in wafer revenue

Consensus models CoWoS capacity inside TSMC's foundry revenue bucket. It should be modeled as a separate $10B+ annuity with 20% price hikes accepted by NVDA for 2026 allocation. CoWoS capacity scaled 35K → 75K wafers/month (2024→2026) and is targeted at 130K wpm by 2027 — a 3.7x expansion in 3 years. NVDA pre-booked >50% of 2026 CoWoS capacity at the +20% pricing. AVGO, AMD, and custom ASIC customers fight for the remainder. Advanced packaging revenue was ~8% of TSMC total in 2024, on track to exceed 15% by 2027. The duopoly is CoWoS-S (silicon interposer, HBM-adjacent logic) and CoWoS-L (large interposer, 8-stack HBM) — both unmatched at scale. Intel Foveros and Samsung I-Cube are >2 generations behind in capacity and yield.

### 2. Silicon shield is quantified, not theoretical — $165B US co-investment is the new tail-risk discount

The "silicon shield" argument (Taiwan's strategic value deters Chinese action) was theoretical through 2024. It is now backed by concrete US co-investment: TSMC's $165B Arizona commitment (6 fabs + 2 advanced-packaging + 1 R&D center, up from $65B pre-2025) and the March 2025 Trump $100B additional pledge are not just political theater — they make Taiwan loss a direct US Treasury balance-sheet hit, not just a diplomatic issue. The US has effectively bought co-insurance on Taiwan's survival by bonding TSMC to American soil. This is why TSM trades at 22x forward earnings despite >$1T market cap and 40% Asian-fab-concentration risk that would deserve a 15x multiple in a vacuum.

### 3. A16 exclusivity to NVDA Feynman creates infrastructural co-dependence, not a supplier-customer relationship

A16 (2nd-gen 2nm class with Super Power Rail backside power delivery) goes into exclusive volume production for NVDA's Feynman architecture in late 2026 / 2027. Backside power delivery is a structural node advance that reduces IR drop and improves density 6-8% with 15-20% performance-per-watt gain. Apple took 1st-gen N2 (M5 shipped Q4 2025). NVDA takes A16 exclusively at launch. This is not a typical foundry-customer dynamic — NVDA's entire 2027+ roadmap is *architecturally dependent* on TSMC A16 timing. Pricing asymmetry: TSMC captures ~40% of NVDA's BOM cost via wafer + CoWoS + advanced test, versus 15-18% for a typical high-end foundry relationship. This asymmetry is what justifies modeling TSMC FCF growth closer to NVDA unit growth than to WFE capex cycles.

### 4. COUPE silicon photonics is category expansion — TSMC is becoming the photonics foundry, not just logic

TSMC's COUPE (Compact Universal Photonic Engine) entered risk production with AMD in Feb 2026, 9 months ahead of roadmap. The $280M Taiwan silicon-photonics R&D center (announced Oct 2025) signals TSMC views optical interconnect as a second logic business, not an accessory. Current market: optical interconnect is ~$14B split between InPhi (acquired by MRVL), LITE EMLs, and Chinese optical module makers. TSMC's move to integrate SiPh on-wafer removes the packaging/alignment step and captures 40-60% of interconnect BOM value that today flows to optical-module vendors. If COUPE ramps with AMD MI400 (2027) and NVDA Spectrum-X upgrades (2027-2028), TSMC adds a $5-8B revenue line by 2029 that isn't in consensus models. The competitive moat for photonics foundry is the same moat as logic foundry — process know-how, customer partnerships, and yield learning curve — and nobody else has 10 years of photonics R&D at volume scale.

### 5. Pricing power is rising, not topped — N2 @ +20% premium says monopoly rents are expanding

Consensus assumes gross margin is already near ceiling at 58-59%. The N2 price premium (20% above N3, itself already a 15-18% premium to N5) says the opposite: TSMC is still harvesting pricing power as customers have nowhere else to go. N2 volume mix in 2026: ~10% of revenue. Scaling to 35-40% by 2028 alone adds 300-400bps to blended gross margin via mix shift — before any additional price hikes. 2028 gross margin of 62-64% is plausible if N2/A16/A14 mix accelerates and US fabs achieve cost parity (stretch goal: $200B+ in additional tooling + TSMC Arizona learning rate). Consensus models a declining margin path into Arizona dilution (2-3pts drag by 2030). The offset — monopoly pricing at node transitions — is not in the sell-side model.

## Outstanding Questions

### 1. What fraction of 2027 CoWoS is reservable vs. de facto locked?

If >60% of 2027 CoWoS is already allocated (NVDA + AAPL + AVGO + AMD), the marginal ASIC customer has a choice between paying TSMC's asking price or not getting silicon. That's monopoly pricing. If only 40% is pre-locked, there's still competitive pressure from Samsung I-Cube. Answer comes from the July 2026 capex guide and any NVDA quarterly disclosure of packaging allocation.

### 2. Is the Arizona yield gap closing or widening through 2026?

TSMC Arizona Fab 21 started N4 production in Q1 2025 with reported yield parity to Taiwan by Q3 2025 — faster than Intel Ohio or Samsung Texas. If Arizona N2 (Fab 22 target: 2028) achieves yield parity within 2 quarters of ramp, the margin dilution thesis is wrong. If it takes >4 quarters, the 2-3pt margin drag is real. Answer: Q2 2027 earnings when Arizona N4 mix crosses 15%.

### 3. How much of the $42B 2026 capex is CoWoS/advanced-packaging vs. logic?

Historically capex split has been 70% logic / 10% packaging / 20% mature + SPE. 2026 guidance implies packaging is now 18-22% of capex. If true, TSMC is treating CoWoS as a strategic category rather than a side business — which validates Insight #1 but also raises the downside risk of packaging oversupply if AI accelerator demand softens in 2027. Answer: Q4 2026 earnings capex breakout.

### 4. Does Intel 18A ramp with any credible leading-edge customer in 2026-2027?

Intel Foundry's Crescent Island (18A Panther Lake launch pad) needs an external customer to prove the "foundry" pivot. Microsoft has publicly tested 18A. If any major (MSFT ASIC, MediaTek, AMD mobile) commits to 18A production, TSMC loses some optionality at the low-volume edge of leading-edge. If Intel 18A misses Q4 2026 HVM (high-volume manufacturing) or yields below 60%, the Intel threat collapses. Answer: Intel Q3 2026 / Q4 2026 results and any customer disclosure.

### 5. What is the real Apple revenue contribution trajectory after M5 ramp?

Apple has historically been 25-26% of TSMC revenue, but iPhone unit growth is decelerating and M-series Mac/iPad units are small. If Apple share drops below 22% in 2026-2027, TSMC needs the AI accelerator customers (NVDA/AMD/AVGO) to fully absorb the slack. If they do — and they will at the current trajectory — the customer mix shifts from "consumer cyclical" to "enterprise AI capex cyclical." That's not obviously less risky — see AI Bubble Risk linkage. Answer: Apple 2026 holiday and Q1 2027 unit volumes.

### 6. Does Huawei Ascend + SMIC progress reduce TSMC China TAM faster than expected?

Huawei's Ascend 950PR (Q1 2026 ship, in-house HBM, ByteDance $5.6B order) is being fabricated at SMIC, not TSMC — TSMC is blocked from Chinese AI customers anyway. But if Huawei+SMIC capture Chinese domestic AI capex at scale, total TSMC-addressable AI capex shrinks by the ~$40-50B Chinese bucket. That's already in the base case but could accelerate. Answer: 2027 China AI capex breakdown in hyperscaler earnings.

### 7. Is COUPE photonics timing credible or optimistic?

TSMC claimed COUPE in risk production Feb 2026 with AMD. Risk production ≠ HVM. If AMD's MI400 slips to late 2027 or 2028 (plausible given TSMC's own A16 timing for AMD-adjacent products), COUPE revenue contribution shifts right by a year. A $5B revenue delay at 50%+ gross margin = $2.5B EBITDA shift. Material for a $1T+ company. Answer: AMD AI product roadmap updates through 2026.

### 8. At what valuation multiple does customer concentration risk start mattering?

TSM trades at 22x NTM earnings. AAPL+NVDA = ~40% of revenue, possibly 45% by 2027. If NVDA sees earnings compression (competitor erosion, AI capex slowdown, any of the NVDA bear case scenarios), TSMC feels it directly. This has never been tested at these concentration levels for a $1T+ foundry. Comparable: Broadcom at AVGO+AAPL concentration, but AVGO is diversified. Answer: pressure-test in the next NVDA conviction-change event.

## Business Model & Product Description

**Pure-play contract foundry — makes chips designed by others.** Opposite of Intel's historical IDM model. TSMC revenue = wafer volume × ASP × yield, where ASP is a function of node vintage (N2 > N3 > N5 > N7 > N16/28/mature). Margins compound via learning-rate: each successive wafer at a given node costs less to produce than the previous one, but ASP stays sticky because customers can't re-qualify designs on competing nodes without 18-24 months of engineering.

**Analogy**: TSMC is to advanced logic what TSMC alone is — there is no second. The closest analogue in manufacturing is ASML for EUV lithography or Airbus/Boeing for widebody aircraft. Unlike ASML, TSMC owns the entire process integration (hundreds of steps) and is not swappable. A customer cannot take a TSMC-optimized design to Samsung or Intel in <18 months.

### Key products

**Logic nodes** (the core business):
- **N5/N4 family** — 2020-2024 workhorse, still 36% of revenue; Apple A17/A18, NVDA Hopper/Ada, AMD Zen 4/5
- **N3 family** (N3E, N3P, N3X) — 2023+ ramp, 22%; Apple M5/A18 Pro, NVDA Blackwell, AMD Zen 5/Turin
- **N2** — late 2025 HVM; Apple M-series 2026, AMD EPYC 2026, exclusive early slots for AI accelerators
- **N2P** — 2026 refresh of N2, adds backside power delivery preview
- **A16** — late 2026, full Super Power Rail (backside power); exclusive NVDA Feynman launch node
- **A14, A14P** — 2028/2029, next-gen with new transistor architecture (CFET rumored)

**Advanced packaging**:
- **CoWoS-S**: silicon interposer, used for HBM-adjacent GPU logic (NVDA H100, H200, B200)
- **CoWoS-L**: large-interposer for 8-stack HBM and multi-chiplet (NVDA B300, AMD MI325X, future Rubin)
- **SoIC-X**: hybrid bonding, 3D die stacking; Apple M5, AMD 3D V-Cache, NVDA Rubin Ultra
- **InFO**: integrated fan-out, lower-cost packaging for phones/edge AI

**Photonics/optical**:
- **COUPE**: Compact Universal Photonic Engine — co-packaged silicon photonics for optical interconnect; AMD risk production Feb 2026

## Industry Context

### Competitive landscape

| Foundry | Advanced node capability | 2025 leading-edge share | Strategic position |

| GlobalFoundries | Mature only (>14nm) | 0% (no advanced) | Mature-node, specialty focus |

### Pricing power trajectory

Historical: TSMC ASP rose 6-8% per annum 2015-2020 (node mix + modest price hikes). 2021-2023 post-pandemic 20% one-time price hike across nodes. 2024-2025: further 3-6% hikes on advanced nodes. 2026: N2 launches at +20% premium to N3. Forward: sell-side models 3-5% annual pricing through 2028; the insight is this is too conservative given monopoly and customer lock-in at leading edge.

### Value chain position

TSMC sits at the narrowest chokepoint in the entire AI stack. ASML supplies EUV to everyone, but only TSMC converts EUV into leading-edge logic wafers at volume. The chain:
```
Chip designer (NVDA/AVGO/AAPL) → TSMC logic wafer → TSMC CoWoS packaging → 
SK Hynix/Samsung HBM (on TSMC interposer) → BESI hybrid bonding tools → 
Hyperscaler installation (GOOG/META/MSFT/AMZN) → Enterprise AI deployment
```
At every step TSMC either produces (wafer, packaging) or enables (hybrid bonding, photonics). The only step TSMC doesn't dominate is HBM manufacturing — SK Hynix leads at ~55% HBM share.

### Market share & pricing power shifts

2015: TSMC 50% leading-edge, Intel 45%, Samsung 5% — three-way race.
2020: TSMC 78%, Samsung 18%, Intel 4% — Intel fails 10nm, Samsung 3nm GAA delayed.
2025: TSMC 92%, Samsung 5%, Intel 2%, SMIC 1% — TSMC is a de facto monopoly.
2028 projected: TSMC 88-92% maintained (Intel 18A recovers slightly if Microsoft commits), Samsung 4-6%, Intel 4-6%, SMIC 2-4% (Chinese domestic only).

## Bull Case

**Target: $380-420 TSM (≈25x 2028 EPS, +40-55% upside from ~$270)**

The bull case is the monopoly-rents thesis fully realized over 2026-2028:

1. **Pricing power compounds**: N2 at +20% premium sets precedent; A16 and A14 launch at +15-20% premium stack-up. Blended gross margin reaches 63-65% by 2028 as N2/A16 mix exceeds 35%.

2. **CoWoS becomes a $15B+ line by 2027**: 130K wpm at $100K+ ASP per wafer-equivalent. 55-60% gross margin. Separately reportable line item drives multiple re-rating.

3. **COUPE photonics adds $5-8B by 2028**: TSMC becomes the optical-interconnect foundry; $40B+ TAM across datacenter, automotive LiDAR, quantum.

4. **NVDA Feynman + Rubin + AMD MI400 volume explodes**: TSMC captures 40%+ of $250B+ AI accelerator BOM across wafer + packaging + test.

5. **Apple sustains share**: M-series Mac/iPad volumes accelerate on-device AI workloads; Apple share stays at 25%.

6. **Arizona achieves yield parity by Q4 2027**: margin dilution from US fabs caps at 150bps rather than 300bps. US revenue share (>25% by 2028) de-risks Taiwan tail.

7. **Taiwan risk discount narrows**: US $165B co-investment + Trump administration explicit tariff exemption + Pentagon AI silicon sourcing from TSMC Arizona creates a de facto security guarantee. Discount narrows from ~15% to 5-8%.

**2028 EPS estimate in bull case: $16-17 per ADR share (vs $12 consensus).** At 25x, that's $400+.

## Bear Case

**Target: $170-200 TSM (≈14x depressed EPS, -25-35% downside)**

Bear case is a combination of customer concentration shock + Arizona margin drag + geopolitical re-rating:

1. **AI capex compresses in 2027**: NVDA earnings disappoint (hyperscaler ROI shortfall; see [[AI Bubble Risk and Semiconductor Valuations]]). TSMC HPC revenue grows only 5-10% in 2027 vs +50% 2026. AI-leveraged platforms (AVGO, AMD, MRVL) follow.

2. **Apple share erodes to <20%**: iPhone saturation + on-device AI workload staying on M-series only. AAPL revenue contribution falls 25% → 18% by 2028.

3. **Intel 18A lands a credible second customer** (MSFT ASIC, MediaTek, or AMD mobile): TSMC leading-edge share slips to 86-87%, pricing discipline cracks at the margin.

4. **Arizona yield parity takes 6+ quarters longer than Taiwan**: blended gross margin drags 300-400bps by 2028. Capex efficiency falls. US fab ROIC becomes unattractive.

5. **Taiwan geopolitical escalation**: not invasion (tail), but Chinese blockade rehearsal, semiconductor embargo threat, or Trump administration renewed 100% tariff rhetoric. Stock re-rates to 15x from 22x on discount-rate expansion alone = -30% multiple compression.

6. **CoWoS overbuild**: if TSMC targets 130K wpm by 2027 and AI accelerator demand cools, packaging utilization drops from 100%+ to 75%. Underutilization on packaging has the same margin math as foundry underutilization — every point of utilization loss is ~40bps of gross margin.

**2028 EPS in bear case: $8-9 per ADR share.** At 14x, that's $110-126 — but high-quality monopoly franchises tend to find floor at 16-18x. Realistic floor: $170-200.

## Risks

### Thesis risks (the investment case is wrong)

1. **AI capex cycle peak is Q4 2026** — not 2028 or later. Most important single risk. If hyperscaler capex growth stalls at +$400B annualized and decelerates, TSMC HPC revenue misses by 20-30% in 2027-2028. Linked to [[AI Bubble Risk and Semiconductor Valuations]].

2. **Customer concentration shock** — AAPL+NVDA at 40% of revenue is unprecedented for a $1T+ manufacturer. A single NVDA negative surprise translates to immediate TSMC downgrade cycle. This is a thesis risk because it invalidates the "diversified monopoly" framing.

3. **Intel Foundry 18A ramp succeeds with external customers** — breaks the "no second" logic-leader narrative. Low-probability (Intel has failed for 10 years) but non-zero and catastrophic for multiple if it happens.

4. **Arizona structural margin drag is 300bps+ permanent** — if US fab cost disadvantage is a permanent 10-15% premium to Taiwan (energy, labor, supply chain), Arizona becomes a strategic cost center rather than a revenue-enhancing diversification.

### Position risks (thesis is right but stock drops)

5. **Taiwan geopolitical event** — Chinese air/sea blockade rehearsal, cable cuts, "quarantine" operations short of invasion. Stock drops 20-30% on any such headline even without invasion.

6. **Valuation multiple compression** — if 10-year Treasury rises to 5%+, TSM 22x multiple compresses to 17-18x on discount-rate expansion alone. -20% even with earnings on track.

7. **TWD appreciation** — TSMC reports in NTD but is dollar-revenue-heavy. If TWD strengthens vs USD (historically happens in Taiwan election years or US rate cuts), margin headwind of 50-100bps with no change in underlying business.

8. **Export control expansion** — Trump administration 2025 additional restrictions on TSMC shipments to any Chinese AI customer (not just blacklisted ones). Small direct impact but signals political risk.

9. **HBM supply constraint bottleneck** — SK Hynix/Samsung HBM shortage caps CoWoS utilization below TSMC's own packaging capacity. Linked to [[Research/2026-04-10 - Hybrid Bonding and BESI Revenue Impact.md]].
