#### Indian Defence Contractors: Publicly Listed Stocks
India has a robust defence sector with multiple publicly listed companies, reflecting its emphasis on self-reliance and indigenization under initiatives like "Aatmanirbhar Bharat." These companies are listed on the Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE) and/or the National Stock Exchange (NSE), and their stocks are actively traded by investors. The research identified the following key publicly listed Indian defence contractors:

- **Bharat Electronics Limited (BEL)**: A major manufacturer of defence electronics, including communication devices, electronic warfare systems, and radars. It is a Navratna Public Sector Undertaking (PSU) and listed on both BSE and NSE ([Groww - Best Defence Stocks in India 2025](https://groww.in/blog/best-defence-stocks-in-india)).
- **Hindustan Aeronautics Limited (HAL)**: Specializes in aerospace and defence, manufacturing aircraft, helicopters, and avionics. It is another prominent PSU listed on BSE and NSE ([Equitymaster - List of Defence Stocks](https://www.equitymaster.com/stockquotes/defence/list-of-defence-sector)).
- **Bharat Dynamics Limited (BDL)**: Focuses on guided missiles and allied defence equipment, listed on BSE and NSE.
- **Mazagon Dock Shipbuilders Limited (MDL)**: Builds warships and submarines for the Indian Navy, publicly traded on BSE and NSE.
- **Cochin Shipyard Limited**: Engaged in building warships and offshore platforms, listed on BSE and NSE.
- **Garden Reach Shipbuilders and Engineers Limited (GRSE)**: Manufactures warships for the Indian Navy, listed on BSE and NSE.
- **Bharat Earth Movers Limited (BEML)**: Produces heavy earth-moving equipment and is involved in defence projects, listed on both exchanges with the Government of India holding a 54.03% stake ([Defence Public Sector Undertakings | DDP](https://www.ddpmod.gov.in/our-organizations/defence-public-sector-undertakings)).
- **Mishra Dhatu Nigam Limited (MIDHANI)**: Specializes in superalloys and titanium alloys for defence and aerospace, listed on BSE and NSE.

- **Zen Technologies Limited**: Provides defence training and simulation systems, listed on BSE and NSE.
- **Data Patterns (India) Limited**: Offers electronic manufacturing services, including for defence, listed on BSE and NSE.
- **MTAR Technologies Limited**: Engaged in precision engineering for aerospace and defence, listed on BSE and NSE.
- **Paras Defence and Space Technologies Limited**: Manufactures electro-optical systems and unmanned aerial systems, listed on BSE and NSE.
- **Dynamatic Technologies Limited**: Involved in aerospace and defence manufacturing, listed on BSE and NSE.

**Survey Note: Detailed Analysis of Longest Recorded Time Without Sleep**

This note provides a comprehensive analysis of the longest recorded time a human has stayed awake, based on extensive research conducted as of May 11, 2025. The focus is on identifying the most reliable and documented cases, with a detailed breakdown of findings and historical context.

### Record Overview
The longest officially recognized time a human has stayed awake is 453 hours and 40 minutes, which is 18 days, 21 hours, and 40 minutes, achieved by Robert McDonald in 1986. This record was acknowledged by Guinness World Records before they ceased monitoring such attempts in 1997 due to the inherent dangers of sleep deprivation. The evidence leans toward this being the current record, with no known subsequent attempts breaking it, as confirmed by multiple sources.

### Historical Context and Notable Cases
Several individuals have attempted to stay awake for extended periods, with varying levels of documentation and medical supervision. Below is a table summarizing key cases, including duration, year, and noted effects:

Randy Gardner's case, staying awake for 264 hours in 1964, is one of the most well-documented, with medical monitoring by Stanford sleep researcher Dr. William C. Dement and Lt. Cmdr. John J. Ross. His experiment supported later studies on "microsleeps," which are momentary lapses into sleep lasting a few seconds. However, his record was surpassed multiple times, with Robert McDonald's attempt being the last recognized by Guinness.

### Challenges and Controversies
The accuracy of these records is complicated by the possibility of microsleeps, where individuals might briefly fall asleep without realizing it, potentially lowering the true duration of wakefulness. Guinness World Records stopped monitoring in 1997 due to these dangers and the risk of fatal familial insomnia, a rare genetic disorder causing death after months without sleep, though not relevant to voluntary attempts.

Other claims, such as Tony Wright's alleged 266 hours, lack the same level of scrutiny and are not recognized. The India Today article mentions McDonald's 19 days, aligning with the 453 hours and 40 minutes, reinforcing his record.

### Medical and Scientific Perspective
Medical sources, such as Healthline and Medical News Today, often cite Randy Gardner's 264 hours as the longest recorded, possibly due to its well-documented nature. However, these sources do not contradict McDonald's record, and the lack of recent medical journal articles directly addressing McDonald's case suggests his record remains the benchmark in recognized contexts. The effects of sleep deprivation, including hallucinations, cognitive impairment, and mood changes, are well-documented, supporting Guinness's decision to halt monitoring.

### Conclusion
Based on the research, Robert McDonald's 18 days, 21 hours, and 40 minutes in 1986 is the longest recorded time a human has stayed awake, recognized by Guinness World Records. The evidence leans toward no subsequent records due to safety concerns, with Randy Gardner's case being notable but shorter. This analysis highlights the resilience and risks of extreme sleep deprivation, emphasizing the importance of rest for human health.

In Christian tradition, it’s associated with the Roman soldier who pierced Jesus’ side with the Holy Lance, though the name’s historical use predates this, appearing in Roman contexts like Gaius Cassius Longinus, a conspirator against Julius Caesar.

**Q:** What does 如来 mean

---

如来 (Rúlái) is a Chinese term from Buddhism, meaning "Thus Come One" or "He Who Has Thus Come." It translates the Sanskrit *Tathāgata*, a title for the Buddha, signifying one who has attained enlightenment and comes as truth itself. It emphasizes the Buddha's arrival in accordance with reality or truth.

---

**Q:** what does 如 mean

---

如 (rú) in Chinese means "like," "as," "similar to," or "if." It is used to draw comparisons, indicate manner, or express conditionality. In Buddhist contexts, as in 如来 (Rúlái), it contributes to the meaning "thus" or "as it is," reflecting the Sanskrit *tathā* (true, thus). Its usage varies by context, often denoting resemblance or accordance.

---

**Q:** Does it not mean come?

---

如 (rú) itself does not mean "come." It means "like," "as," "similar to," or "if" in Chinese, used for comparison or condition. In 如来 (Rúlái, Tathāgata), the "come" aspect derives from 来 (lái), meaning "come," while 如 contributes "thus" or "as." Together, they form "Thus Come One," but 如 alone doesn’t carry the meaning of "come."
