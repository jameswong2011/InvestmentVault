# ServiceNow's 2025 paradox: strong AI traction, weak stock

ServiceNow (NOW) has suffered a **27-31% year-to-date decline in 2025** despite exceptional AI execution, with Now Assist products on pace to exceed **$500 million ACV** this year and targeting **$1 billion by 2026**. The disconnect stems from three compounding catalysts: a January guidance miss that spooked growth investors, an aggressive **$12+ billion M&A spending spree** that raised execution concerns, and a high-profile December downgrade warning that AI may cannibalize traditional SaaS seat licenses. Institutional ownership remains robust at 87%, with more buyers than sellers in recent quarters and short interest declining—suggesting institutional investors see the selloff as overdone while retail sentiment capitulates.

## KeyBanc's "Death of SaaS" thesis rattled investors

Despite the KeyBanc downgrade, analyst consensus remains **overwhelmingly positive** with 28 buy ratings versus just 2 holds and 1 sell. The average price target of **$221-228 post-split** implies roughly **50% upside** from current levels. RBC Capital trimmed targets but maintained Outperform, while BTIG initiated coverage with a Buy rating and $200 target in December.

The December downgrade crystallized growing fears about AI's double-edged impact on enterprise software. Analyst Jackson Ader argued that generative AI productivity gains could reduce the number of software seats enterprises need, creating "seat count pressure" that threatens ServiceNow's hybrid pricing model. His most provocative claim: ServiceNow "may cede ground to Microsoft in 2026" as the AI orchestration battle intensifies.

ServiceNow has **significantly underperformed** the broader software sector, with the IGV ETF (which holds NOW as its 4th largest position at 7% weight) returning **+13.8% YTD** compared to NOW's -30% decline. Peers like Salesforce and Microsoft have fared better, though the entire enterprise software sector experienced weakness in 2025 as investors favored hardware and semiconductor plays benefiting from AI infrastructure buildout.
