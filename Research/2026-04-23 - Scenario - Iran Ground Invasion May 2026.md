# Scenario — Iran Ceasefire Collapse & US Ground Invasion (May 2026)

## Scenario Definition

**Triggering event**: April 21 ceasefire expires without resolution; Iran-US nuclear negotiations fail on enriched uranium removal; US initiates ground invasion operations in May 2026. This escalates from the current dual-blockade regime (Iran IRGC closure + US counter-blockade on Iranian ports, per [[Macro/Iran War Macroeconomic Scenario]]) to active kinetic conflict on Iranian soil.

**Quantitative parameters (6-month horizon, May-Oct 2026)**:
- **Brent oil**: spikes to $120-150/bbl on invasion confirmation; sustained $100+/bbl for duration (lower bound assumes SPR release + Saudi spare capacity via pipeline bypass partially offsets; upper bound assumes full Strait of Hormuz closure + mining)
- **Strait of Hormuz**: insurance premia already at 5%+ of hull value ($5M+ per laden transit); 20-40% throughput disruption sustained; full closure = $180+ oil tail
- **10Y UST**: -30 to -60bp on flight-to-safety week one, then +50-100bp as oil-driven CPI re-accelerates; net ambiguous by month 3
- **USD**: DXY +4-6% safe-haven bid; INR -5 to -10% (India oil-import shock); EM FX -5 to -10% broadly
- **VIX**: 25 → 40+ on invasion week; sustained 22-30 for 3 months
- **Equity risk premium**: +150-200bp; multiples compress 10-15% broad market, 20%+ on rate-sensitive/long-duration
- **Defense spending**: supplemental Congressional appropriation $50-100B likely; DOD procurement pull-forward

**Primary transmission channels**:
1. Oil price → airlines, chemicals, logistics cost, EM import bills, E&P producers (winners)
2. Strait of Hormuz shipping risk → tanker rates explode (VLCC/MR), insurance premia sustained
3. Risk-off / VIX → high-multiple tech compression, volatility, credit spreads widen
4. Rates → ambiguous (duration benefits briefly, then hurts as inflation reaccelerates)
5. USD strength → EM equities hurt; US exporters hurt at margin
6. **India-specific**: unique exposure — 85% oil import dependence, strategic Iran trade corridor, rupee pressure, ~30-day SPR vs China's ~300 days
7. Defense procurement → supplemental budget winners, AI-native defense primes accelerate
8. Supply chain / Middle East logistics → semiconductor shipping, Red Sea routes, fertilizer chain (34% urea, 20% ammonia/phosphate through Hormuz)
9. LNG geopolitical premium → European gas repricing on Ras Laffan permanence; US LNG export pull
10. Cyber retaliation → Iran-linked APTs (water utilities, banking, critical infrastructure) — F500 cybersecurity spending accelerates

**Probability estimate**: Tail event, ~5-10% over 6 months. Asymmetric payoff (portfolio-wide drawdown potential) justifies the preparation even at low probability.

## Second-Order Effects

### Supply chain cascades

**Hormuz closure → LNG supply gap persists.** Cheniere captures structural replacement volume. Secondary effect: European industrial gas users curtail (bullish US LNG) while Asian buyers with 3-week strategic reserves (Japan, Korea) scramble — cascades through product tankers (STNG) which benefit from both product tanker rerouting AND LNG carrier redeployments.

**Ras Laffan + Iran fertilizer offline** (34% of global urea, 20% ammonia/phosphate through Hormuz) cascades to agricultural inputs. Fertilizer +50% hurts DE (John Deere) customer farm economics short-term. Helps commodity prices medium-term (corn, soy reprice higher). Net: farmer capex delays worsen despite commodity uplift — reinforces DE's trough thesis rather than helping it.

**Iran cyber retaliation** against Western infrastructure (precedent: 2023 water utility attacks, 2012 banking system attacks, 2017 petrochemical plant attacks) forces emergency budget reallocation at every hyperscaler, every bank, every utility. Direct windfall to CRWD (threat intel + kernel-level EDR) and PANW (platform + CyberArk PAM depth for credential-based APT defense). Secondary benefits to NET (Cloudflare DDoS + security) and PLTR (Gotham/Apollo threat analysis).

**Defense supply-chain pull-forward** — munitions procurement, ISR capabilities, AV reconnaissance, submarine production — cascades from kinetic conflict directly to PLTR via Maven ($1.275B ceiling), Alpamayo AV stack (Mercedes CLA 2026 already shipping, Ukraine-style AV reconnaissance use cases accelerate), ShipOS ($448M Navy — submarine production compression), TITAN Army deployment. Second-order benefits: AVGO (custom silicon for defense AI workloads), NVDA (sovereign AI $30B+ grows faster; DGX SuperPOD procurement by allied governments accelerates).

### Demand substitution

**Consumer discretionary destruction hits five verticals simultaneously**: OPEN (housing freeze via mortgage rates), MTN (travel discretionary + sentiment), TTWO (GTA VI pricing sensitivity), UBER (delivery discretionary), HIMS (wellness subscription discretionary). These were thought diversified across industries but correlate down under gasoline + recession.

**Subscription resilience structurally advantaged**: NFLX (1.8% churn vs streaming industry 5.5%), SPOT (1.3% churn), DUOL ($13/mo low bar), GAW (behavioral lock-in, hobby recession-resistant) each weather downturn through sticky unit economics. Relative winners within consumer.

**AI capex timing**: hyperscalers unlikely to cancel committed orders (Rubin, CoWoS, AI fabric), more likely to extend procurement timelines by 1-2 quarters. BESI / LITE / AVGO / SEMICAP contracted backlog durable. Tail risk is 2027 pause, not 2026 collapse. The distinction is critical: contracted revenue vs speculative bookings.

**Crude vs product cracks divergence favors Jamnagar (Reliance)**: $35-42/bbl diesel crack windfall partially offsets 85% crude import burden within the RELIANCE consolidated entity. O2C segment wins while Jio (consumer spending compression) and Retail (inflation hit) lose — net effect still major negative because O2C is ~30% of EBITDA vs consumer ~54%.

## Portfolio Assessment

**Net portfolio impact**: Moderately negative near-term with strong directional bifurcation. Estimated first-30-day drawdown -15 to -25% before Major Positive beneficiary re-rating offsets. Full 6-month scenario: -5 to -15% net depending on beneficiary capture (LNG/STNG/CCJ/PLTR/CRWD/PANW each could appreciate 30-50%+ in the scenario).

**Worst-case cluster**: OPEN + MTN + TTWO + UBER simultaneously hit as consumer discretionary cyclicals. Combined drawdown 30-40% in these names compounds with RELIANCE + EDEL India cluster at 25-35% drawdown — aggregate 5-8% of portfolio at risk of 30%+ drawdown.

**Concentration risk exposed**:
1. India (RELIANCE + EDEL) — thought diversified, actually highly correlated
2. Consumer discretionary (OPEN + MTN + TTWO + HIMS + DUOL + UBER + SHOP) — single factor
3. AI infrastructure (11 names) — single factor masked by breadth
4. Cybersecurity + Defense (CRWD + PANW + PLTR) — rare positive-alpha cluster (beneficial concentration)

**Natural hedges already held**:
- LNG + STNG + CCJ long vs consumer/India short — crisis amplification balances
- CRWD + PANW long vs AI-capex-sensitive short — cyber accelerates while capex pauses
- PLTR long vs high-multiple consumer tech short — defense pull-forward vs discretionary compression

**Hedge gaps (not currently held)**:
- **Defense primes** (LMT, RTX, NOC, HII, KTOS): the purest kinetic conflict beneficiaries; PLTR is defense-adjacent but not direct. Even 2-3% allocation would materially reduce portfolio tail exposure
- **Gold/precious metals** (GLD, GDX, Newmont): wartime monetary hedge; Iran macro note explicitly cites gold's contrarian entry at the $4,400 level with central bank demand floor
- **Upstream crude producers** (OXY, XOM, CVX): vault has gas (LNG) and logistics (STNG) but not upstream crude — a gap
- **VIX 35/50 call spreads**: tail hedge explicitly recommended in [[Macro/Iran War Macroeconomic Scenario]] at 10-15x payoff asymmetry
- **Indian rupee short** (JEPG puts, INDA puts): hedge against EDEL + RELIANCE India concentration

**Opportunities created**:

- **Fertilizer producers**: Mosaic (MOS), Nutrien (NTR) — direct beneficiary of Ras Laffan urea disruption

- **European LNG regas**: trading on EU gas repricing if US producers capacity-constrained
