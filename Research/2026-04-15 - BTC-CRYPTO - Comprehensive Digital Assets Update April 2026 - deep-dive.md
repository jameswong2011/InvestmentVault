## Regulatory Developments

### GENIUS Act (Payment Stablecoins)
- **Status**: Signed into law July 18, 2025
- **Senate vote**: 68-30 (bipartisan); House: passed July 17, 2025
- **Key provisions**:
  - Payment stablecoins must be backed 1:1 by USD or low-risk assets
  - Only permitted issuers may issue for US persons
  - Payment stablecoins explicitly not securities under securities law
  - Subject to Bank Secrecy Act (AML compliance)
  - Section 4(a)(11): Prohibits yield payments on payment stablecoins
- **Implementation**: Treasury final rules by July 2026; FDIC comment period extended to May 18, 2026
- **Key tension**: Circle-Coinbase revenue sharing arrangement potentially violates yield prohibition; regulatory scrutiny ongoing

### CLARITY Act (Digital Asset Market Structure)
- **Status**: Passed House (294-134, July 2025); Senate negotiations ongoing (April 2026)
- **Key provisions**:
  - CFTC "exclusive jurisdiction" over digital commodity spot markets
  - SEC retains jurisdiction over investment contract assets
  - Creates clear commodity vs security taxonomy for digital assets
- **Obstacles**: Stablecoin yield restrictions primary sticking point (since Jan 2026)
- **White House**: Crypto adviser Witt says "other hurdles being cleared" (April 13, 2026)
- **Treasury Secretary Bessent**: Declared Clarity Act a "national security priority" (April 9, 2026)
- **Risk**: Senator Lummis warning — must pass before Nov 2026 midterms or wait until 2030
- **Impact if passed**: Most significant crypto regulatory event since GENIUS Act; removes SEC "regulation by enforcement"
