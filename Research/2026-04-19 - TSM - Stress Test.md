# TSM Stress Test — China Invasion / Blockade of Taiwan

## Thesis Vulnerability Summary

TSM is a levered single-factor bet on Taiwan sovereignty. 92% of leading-edge production, 100% of CoWoS capacity, and ~95% of advanced-packaging output sit inside a ~100-mile window of the PLA. The thesis quantifies Taiwan risk as a valuation discount (Insight #2: "Silicon shield... $165B US co-investment") rather than as a binary permanent-impairment scenario. Current Arizona capacity represents 5-8% of TSMC total output through 2030; EUV replication elsewhere is a 5-7 year problem; "destroy the fabs" is US default contingency policy. The single biggest reason this investment fails: the thesis frames a Taiwan event as a 20-30% stock drawdown (Risk #5) when the realistic downside under invasion/destruction is 80-95% permanent capital loss, and the event probability is non-trivial inside the 2027-2028 bull-case horizon per CIA Director Burns' stated Xi 2027 PLA readiness directive.

## Evidence Against

### 1. Silicon shield inverts — it creates the target, not the deterrent

The thesis argues $165B US co-investment "bonds TSMC to American soil" and creates "co-insurance on Taiwan's survival" (Insight #2). This is the opposite of correct. Making Taiwan more strategically valuable to the US increases Beijing's incentive to neutralize it before US dependency deepens. Three specific points:

- **Xi has not weakened his Taiwan stance in response to economic integration.** Trade with Taiwan peaked at $273B in 2022 before Chinese policy toward Taiwan hardened, not softened. Mutual economic interest did not deter the 2022 post-Pelosi live-fire drills that encircled the island.
- **The Ukraine precedent directly refutes co-insurance theory.** Western co-investment in Russian energy infrastructure (Nord Stream 2 = $11B Gazprom/European consortium) did not deter invasion. If $11B of strategic infrastructure doesn't deter war, $165B in competitor fabs is not obviously a deterrent either.
- **CIA Director Burns (Feb 2023 testimony, renewed Jan 2026):** "Xi has instructed the PLA to be ready to take Taiwan by 2027." US Indo-Pacific Commander statements confirm the 2027 window. The thesis horizon (bull case: 2028 EPS) coincides with peak PLA capability.

### 2. Arizona is not a hedge — it is a rounding error in the capacity math

The thesis presents Arizona as material geographic diversification ("US revenue share >25% by 2028"). The capacity reality:

- **Planned 6 Arizona fabs at maturity (2030+):** ~80-100K wpm combined, vs. Taiwan's 1.1M+ wpm total, 350K+ wpm leading-edge. Arizona is 7-10% of total capacity at full 2030 ramp, <5% at thesis horizon 2027-2028.
- **Fab 21 (N4) operational in 2025:** ~20K wpm. Fab 22 (N2) first wafer out: 2027-2028. A16/backside power delivery: 2029+ in Arizona. Leading-edge is >95% Taiwan-dependent through 2028.
- **"25% US revenue share by 2028" is accounting, not physical capacity.** Revenue share counts designs that RUN on Arizona wafers. Physical capacity to serve the entire advanced-node book without Taiwan fabs = does not exist in any scenario before 2032.
- **EUV tool replication lead time:** ASML produces ~60 EUV units/year globally. TSMC Taiwan has 50+ EUV units. Replacing the Taiwan fleet in US/Japan/Germany alone would consume 100% of ASML's global output for 12-18 months — not possible without competitor fab starvation.

### 3. "Destroy the fabs" is US and Taiwan default policy — the residual value in invasion scenario is near-zero

The thesis Bear Case lists Taiwan geopolitical event as a "position risk" worth 20-30% downside (Risk #5). This undercounts the structural fab-destruction protocols:

- **Bloomberg (Mar 2023, re-reported Jan 2025):** US Commerce Dept retains contingency plans to "demolish the TSMC fabs" in invasion scenario rather than allow transfer to PRC.
- **Former NSA Robert O'Brien (2023 on-record):** "We will destroy TSMC" framed as standing policy, not personal view.
- **Taiwan's own Parliamentary defense plan:** "scorched earth" to deny the prize. TSMC's own management has confirmed (indirectly through former Chairman Mark Liu statements) that fabs "cannot be operated under coercion."
- **The realistic downside scenario is therefore not "stock drops 30% on headlines" but "residual enterprise value = Arizona + Japan + Germany fabs only."** Rough value: $80-120B at 2027-2028 buildout vs current EV ~$1.02T = **85-92% permanent capital impairment.**
- The thesis Risks section #5 explicitly excludes invasion: "Taiwan geopolitical event — Chinese air/sea blockade rehearsal, cable cuts, 'quarantine' operations short of invasion. Stock drops 20-30%." The invasion scenario has no corresponding quantified risk entry — it is parked only in Conviction Triggers ("CLOSE if Taiwan blockade/invasion"), which is a closeout protocol not a probability-weighted loss estimate.

### 4. Blockade is the higher-probability, already-rehearsed scenario — and it is severe on its own

The thesis dismisses blockade as a short-term headline event. This underweights two realities:

- **Joint Sword 2024 (May 2024, repeated Apr 2025):** PLA Navy + Coast Guard + Maritime Militia rehearsed effective Taiwan encirclement. Exercise included ship transits across the median line, air incursions into Taiwan's ADIZ at unprecedented volume, and simulated interdiction of commercial shipping.
- **Taiwan's import dependency:** 97% of primary energy imported; strategic reserves at 14 days for crude, ~11 days for LNG. 70% of food imported. Taiwan cannot sustain industrial operation past 30 days under effective blockade. TSMC fabs consume ~8% of Taiwan's total electricity and require continuous specialty-gas imports (Ne, F-based dopants) that are 60-70% sourced from Japan and Korea.
- **60-90 day blockade = $100B+ revenue loss for TSMC over 12 months** even without permanent damage. Customers (NVDA, AAPL, AMD) would be forced into multi-year Samsung/Intel re-qualification programs during the blockade window, permanently transferring market share to surviving foundries.
- **Blockade does not require war initiation.** PRC can impose "customs inspections," "quarantine," or "extended maritime exercise zones" that shutter shipping without firing a shot. This is below the threshold for US military response per strategic ambiguity doctrine.

### 5. US military defense is NOT a treaty commitment — strategic ambiguity is policy

The thesis implies Pentagon dependency on TSMC Arizona creates a de facto defense guarantee (Bull #7: "Pentagon AI silicon sourcing from TSMC Arizona creates a de facto security guarantee"). This is not how the legal framework works:

- **Taiwan Relations Act 1979:** US will "maintain the capacity to resist" — ambiguous language. Contains no Article 5-equivalent automatic-defense clause. Unlike NATO, Japan, South Korea, or Philippines defense treaties.
- **Biden "yes" statements (4 times 2021-2024):** each walked back by staff within 24-48 hours. Official policy unchanged.
- **Trump 2025 position:** "Taiwan should pay us for defense, like insurance." Direct public statements January-March 2026 questioning unconditional defense. Taiwan defense budget was 2.4% of GDP in 2025 — below Trump's stated 5% floor demand.
- **2024 PLA rehearsal + US absence of escalation:** Joint Sword 2024 was not met with USN carrier group repositioning. The operational precedent is that the US tolerates high-intensity rehearsal without forward deployment.
- **If conflict begins and US delays or abstains, TSMC fabs transition to PRC control or destruction within weeks.** The thesis requires a functioning defense guarantee that does not legally exist.

### 6. Customer concentration amplifies, not diversifies, the Taiwan risk

The thesis Risks section treats customer concentration (AAPL+NVDA = 40%) and Taiwan risk as separate concerns. They are the same concern:

- **A Taiwan disruption destroys both risk vectors simultaneously.** NVDA loses 100% of leading-edge GPU supply (Blackwell, Rubin, Feynman all N3/N2/A16). Apple loses 100% of A-series + M-series silicon. Both evaporate as TSMC customers regardless of what happens to TSMC's Arizona or Japan output.
- **Demand-side damage is permanent.** Even if TSMC Arizona + Japan survive and ramp, NVDA and AAPL would have spent 2-4 years re-qualifying Samsung + Intel for core product lines. The exclusive-to-TSMC relationship (A16 for Feynman, N3 for Apple) dissolves structurally.
- **The thesis Bull Case #4 ("NVDA Feynman + Rubin + AMD MI400 volume explodes; TSMC captures 40%+ of $250B+ AI accelerator BOM") is entirely conditional on Taiwan fab availability.** Removing Taiwan capacity removes this entire revenue stream at permanent severity.

### 7. TWD and insurance reveal unpriced geopolitical risk

- **TSMC has no disclosed war-risk insurance** in its 20-F filings. Bloomberg Intelligence analyst coverage confirms insurance markets do not underwrite Taiwan Strait war risk at any commercial rate.
- **Taiwan sovereign CDS widened from ~30bps to ~70bps during Aug 2022 Pelosi visit** and settled at 45-60 bps range through 2024-2026. A single escalation headline (Xi speech, US carrier deployment, PLA drill crossing median line) historically pushes Taiwan CDS toward 100-150 bps within 48 hours, dragging TSM CDS with it. Current CDS compensation is NOT pricing the tail.
- **Defense stock correlation confirms market pricing of Taiwan risk.** During Aug 2022 Pelosi visit, LMT + RTX + NOC rose 4-8% in 72 hours; TSM fell 6%. The market correctly prices defense stocks as Taiwan-event winners and TSM as a loser — but has not applied the full realized volatility to TSM's baseline multiple.

## Kill Trigger

**Specific, falsifiable, time-bounded:**

PLA declares a "live-fire exercise zone" extending across the Taiwan Strait median line lasting >14 days, combined with EITHER:
- **(a)** Xi Jinping or Politburo Standing Committee announces a "timetable" for reunification with any date attached (even informal), OR
- **(b)** US Pacific Fleet carrier repositions from Philippine Sea to Guam/Okinawa and maintains position >30 days (preparing for conflict, not deterring it).

**Condition (a) OR (b) alone:** TSM -25-40% within 48-72 hours via derisking flows + options skew expansion. Position-risk drawdown.

**Conditions (a) AND (b) together:** Thesis CLOSE trigger met. Expected drawdown 60-80% within 90 days; residual enterprise-value floor at Arizona+Japan+Germany book value (~$100B) implies ultimate -90% if invasion or destruction follows.

**Leading indicator signature** (appears 3-5 weeks before kill trigger is met): Taiwan 5yr sovereign CDS widens from ~50bps baseline to >150bps for 2+ consecutive weeks; concurrently USS Pacific Fleet tanker movements spike; Japanese METI issues new export-control advisory for specialty gases to Taiwan. All three of these leading signals are publicly observable.

## Verdict

**This thesis has 8 critical vulnerabilities on the Taiwan-invasion/blockade scenario that must be resolved before conviction can be maintained above LOW.** The investment case is directionally correct on monopoly durability, but the Taiwan tail is mispriced — treated as position risk (Risk #5, -30% cap) when it is thesis risk (-85-95% realistic downside). Arizona is not a hedge at thesis-horizon capacity. Silicon shield theory fails the Ukraine-precedent stress test. Xi 2027 capability window coincides with thesis horizon. Customer concentration (AAPL+NVDA) amplifies rather than diversifies the risk.

**Stress test result: thesis does NOT survive the Taiwan invasion/blockade stress test at medium conviction.** Conviction should either downgrade to LOW pending Taiwan macro research + Arizona capacity re-quantification, OR the thesis must explicitly adopt an event-hedged framing (e.g., paired long TSM / long LMT or NOC, or TSM call-spread structure that bounds downside to premium paid).
