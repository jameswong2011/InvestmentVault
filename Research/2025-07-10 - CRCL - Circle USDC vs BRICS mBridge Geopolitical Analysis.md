# Circle/USDC vs BRICS mBridge: Geopolitical Investment Analysis

## Summary

This research note captures a Grok deep-dive into the geopolitical investment dynamics surrounding Circle (CRCL) and USDC as a US-aligned stablecoin bulwark against the BRICS+ mBridge initiative. The analysis frames stablecoins as a strategic financial instrument in the emerging contest between US dollar hegemony and BRICS de-dollarisation efforts.

## Key Data Points

### Circle / USDC Metrics (as of mid-2025)
- **USDC circulation**: $62B, backed by US Treasury reserves
- **USDC all-time volume**: $26T cumulative
- **USDC growth in 2025**: +78% year-over-year
- **Circle stock (CRCL)**: ~$207/share post-IPO, +300-500% from IPO price
- **IPO valuation target**: $5B
- **Revenue model**: Primarily interest on USDC reserves (invested in Treasuries), revenue-sharing with partners (e.g. Coinbase), API/minting/payment infrastructure fees
- **Projected 2025 revenue**: ~$1.89B (driven by ~$55B USDC circulation at ~5% yield + platform fees)
- **Valuation concern**: 152x EBITDA, rising distribution costs (62%)
- **USDC integrated on**: 23 blockchains
- **Key partnerships**: Coinbase (revenue-sharing), OKX (liquidity), Shopify, Stripe

### BRICS mBridge
- **Status**: MVP (Minimum Viable Product) stage
- **Participants**: China, UAE, and other BRICS-aligned central banks
- **Architecture**: Blockchain-based CBDC cross-border settlement platform
- **Scope**: Wholesale CBDC platform for peer-to-peer payments between central banks
- **Connected**: ~13 CBDCs
- **BIS role**: Debates over termination/handover; project being handed to participating central banks
- **Key pilot**: Renminbi-denominated transactions
- **Related initiative**: BRICS BCBPI (payments infrastructure), "BRICS Bridge" platform

## Geopolitical Framing

### USDC as Strategic US Asset
- ARK Invest has called stablecoins the "most important US strategic asset" in the digital era
- US-aligned nations increasingly view USDC as a financial ally for maintaining dollar-denominated settlement rails
- Stablecoins entrench USD supremacy by extending dollar reach into emerging markets and DeFi ecosystems
- GENIUS Act (July 2025) created federal framework mandating 1:1 reserves, legitimising private-sector stablecoins as CBDC alternatives
- Circle's regulatory compliance positioning (transparency, audits) makes it the "digital dollar" model for US-aligned systems

### BRICS De-Dollarisation Dynamics
- BRICS+ nations pushing de-dollarisation via local currency settlement and mBridge
- mBridge enables peer-to-peer CBDC cross-border settlement, bypassing SWIFT and USD intermediation
- Petroyuan initiatives remain part of the broader de-dollarisation push
- BRICS gold-backed stablecoin proposals discussed but not implemented
- Key limitation: BRICS local currency push has "stalled" — internal coordination challenges persist
- Paradox: BRICS nations themselves use stablecoins like USDT extensively for crypto trading

### Investment Dynamic
- **Bull case**: USDC becomes default USD alternative to mBridge; Circle captures RWA tokenisation, CBDC interoperability fees, DeFi yield optimisation, global remittance dominance, AI-driven risk management products; market share reaches 40%+ → stock to $250+
- **Bear case**: Rate cuts compress reserve interest income; bank stablecoin competition (PYUSD, RLUSD, USDTB) fragments market; rising distribution costs (62%) squeeze margins; stagnant USDC supply at $62B
- **Grok assessment**: Overvalued short-term at ~$207; "not a good short-term buy, wait for dip"; long-term thesis depends on structural expansion of USDC utility

## 10-Year Revenue Model Projection (2035)

Assuming USDC becomes the stable default USD alternative to mBridge:
1. **Reserve interest income** (base) — scaled with circulation growth
2. **Tokenisation of real-world assets** — fees on RWA settlement
3. **CBDC interoperability fees** — bridging USDC with central bank digital currencies
4. **DeFi yield optimisation services** — institutional access to on-chain yield
5. **Global remittance dominance** — displacing legacy corridors (Western Union, etc.)
6. **AI-driven risk management products** — compliance and fraud detection services
