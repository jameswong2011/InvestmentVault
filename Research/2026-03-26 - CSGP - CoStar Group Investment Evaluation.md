**CoStar Group (NASDAQ: CSGP) is a high-quality compounder with a dominant data-and-marketplace moat in commercial real estate (CRE) and apartments, now leveraging that cash engine to build a third leg in residential for-sale via Homes.com while adding 3D/spatial AI differentiation through the 2025 Matterport acquisition.**

The core business remains exceptionally strong (high-90% renewals, 47%+ segment margins in CRE), generating steady free cash flow to fund disciplined residential scaling and share repurchases. 2025 results: $3.25B revenue (+19% YoY), Adjusted EBITDA $442M (+83% YoY to 14% margin), record $308M net new subscription bookings. 2026 guidance: ~$3.8B revenue (+18%), $740–800M Adjusted EBITDA (20% margin, +83% YoY, highest in company history). Medium-term (to 2030): ~15% revenue CAGR, Adjusted EBITDA to $2.3B at 35% margin as Homes.com turns profitable and operating leverage kicks in.

The stock has pulled back sharply (recently ~$42–50 range amid activist pressure and Homes.com spend) and trades at an attractive ~5–6x forward sales with rapid EBITDA/margin expansion ahead—potentially a compelling long-term entry for investors comfortable with 4–5 years of residential investment tapering. Risks include residential execution timing, broader real-estate cycles, and integration/competition, but management’s proven playbook (e.g., Apartments.com) and data moat provide a wide margin of safety.

### Homes.com: Rapid Traffic/Sub Growth but Early Monetization; Investment Now Tapering Sharply
Homes.com has been the fastest-growing major U.S. residential portal. In 2025 it delivered >108 million average monthly unique visitors (leading the CoStar residential network that exceeded 139 million combined), 2.1 billion views, and SEO traffic up 143% YoY. Agent subscribers reached ~31,000 (337% increase since Q1 2024; +18,000 in 2025 alone), with 76% on annual contracts and ~$100M annual recurring revenue (ARR) run-rate—still small vs. Zillow’s scale but the fastest subscriber ramp of any major portal.

It promotes ~216k for-sale listings (~9% U.S. share) and wins 60% more listings per member than non-subscribers. Monetization uses a “your-listing, your-lead” direct-to-agent model (unlike Zillow’s referral-heavy approach) via subscriptions, premium/Boost listings, builder partnerships, and in-depth advertising. Homes AI (launched late 2025) adds conversational search powered by proprietary data + Matterport 3D—unreplicable by competitors—and is being rolled across the portfolio for better conversion.

**Success vs. portals (Zillow, Realtor.com, Redfin):** Strong on traffic and subscriber momentum; it is now positioned as the #2 or fastest-growing network and is eroding share from entrenched players. However, revenue remains modest relative to the $850M net investment/loss in 2025 (cumulative ~$3B invested), drawing activist criticism (e.g., Third Point, D.E. Shaw) calling it a “fiasco.” CoStar counters that this mirrors the successful Apartments.com playbook and that brand awareness is now high enough to shift from heavy top-of-funnel spend to conversion/monetization.

**Monetization potential left:** Very high. Traffic scale is already massive; the remaining unlock is higher conversion via AI, deeper ad products, and cross-selling into the full CoStar ecosystem (commercial + rentals). Management targets revenue exceeding expenses exiting 2029 and positive Adjusted EBITDA in 2030, with Homes.com becoming a major EBITDA contributor. Long-term TAM for residential portals is enormous; CoStar’s direct model and proprietary data/AI could support Zillow-like economics at lower customer-acquisition cost over time.

**Additional investment required:** Sharply lower and predictable. Net investment drops >$300M in 2026 (to ~$550M) via marketing cuts (~$130M), personnel/ops efficiencies (~$85M), and revenue growth (~$20M), then declines $100M+ annually through 2030. The “heavy lifting” (national brand launch, content build) is complete; focus shifts to scaling.

### Matterport: 3D Digital Twins; Early but Accelerating Penetration, Strong Moat Addition
Matterport’s product is a spatial data platform that captures buildings/spaces as dimensionally accurate **3D digital twins** (virtual tours + precise measurements, floor plans, etc.) using AI, computer vision, and capture cameras/services. It turns physical spaces into searchable, analyzable data for real estate, construction, and facilities management.

**Penetration in CoStar portfolio:** Pre-acquisition (Feb 2025 close), CoStar already hosted ~300k Matterport digital twins across its products (field research for inspections, CoStar/LoopNet listings, Homes.com tours). Post-acquisition: $147M revenue contribution in 2025 (9 months; cameras, capture services, subscriptions), with duplicative public-company costs eliminated (~$120M savings). It is now embedded in the Commercial segment and expanding across residential (Homes.com immersive tours) and all property types. Adoption is accelerating but still early-stage relative to total listings (number of twins hosted is a pricing factor in subscriptions; no exact % disclosed, but integration is “ongoing” with full financial/controls inclusion targeted for 2026).

**Timeline to full integration:** Largely complete on the cost/revenue side (synergies realized in 2025); deeper AI/spatial-data fusion across the entire ecosystem (e.g., enhanced analytics, Homes AI) is expected to ramp through 2026–2027. No hard “full integration” date, but contribution is already positive and management views it as core to digitizing all real-estate segments.

**Moat provided:** Significant and durable. CoStar’s existing property database + Matterport’s 3D/spatial dataset creates a proprietary, hard-to-replicate asset (immersive tours drive 10x leads and far higher engagement time vs. 2D photos). It raises switching costs for brokers/agents, improves listing quality/visibility, and powers unique AI insights competitors cannot match at scale. This extends CoStar’s data moat from “information” to “experiential/spatial” real estate.

### CoStar’s Moat and Position in Commercial & Apartment Real Estate
**CRE (CoStar + LoopNet):** Near-monopoly status in U.S./Canada data and analytics. CoStar maintains the most comprehensive proprietary database (hundreds of fields on every property, largest in-house research team, rigorous quality controls). LoopNet is the most-trafficked CRE marketplace. High switching costs, 89–90%+ renewal rates, and 47%+ margins reflect this. Competitors (Crexi in marketplaces—ongoing antitrust litigation; Altus Group/Moody’s in analytics) lack comparable depth/scale. CoStar is not literally a monopoly but operates with monopoly-like economics and network effects.

**Apartments (Apartments.com):** Clear category leader in multifamily rentals. It generates the highest revenue in the space (part of the $1.46B residential segment in 2025, up 20% YoY) and outperforms Zillow’s rental business on scale and monetization. Strong network effects and direct lead generation for property managers.

Overall competitive position remains elite. The Matterport/AI layer and Homes.com buildout extend the moat into residential and global markets without diluting the core CRE fortress. Economic sensitivity exists (CRE cycles), but diversified revenue (subscriptions 93% of total) and data moat provide resilience.

**Investment summary:** CoStar is a rare combination of a high-margin data monopoly funding disciplined disruption in residential, plus emerging 3D/AI differentiation. With investment in Homes.com now peaking and tapering, margin expansion accelerating, and buybacks underway, the risk/reward favors long-term investors at current valuations. Near-term volatility from activists or housing softness is possible, but the 2030 targets (35% EBITDA margin, Homes.com profitable) imply substantial upside if execution holds. This is a “buy the dip” opportunity in a secular winner.
