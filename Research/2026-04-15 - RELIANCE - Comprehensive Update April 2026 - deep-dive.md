## 1. Jio Platforms IPO Update

### DRHP Filing & Regulatory Timeline

- **DRHP Status**: Reliance began work on the Draft Red Herring Prospectus in March 2026. Filing was expected by late March/early April 2026. The delay was caused by SEBI's rule reducing minimum public float from 5% to 2.5% for companies valued above Rs 5 trillion, which required a Finance Ministry gazette notification (issued March 13, 2026).
- **SEBI Review**: Standard 30-75 day review period post-filing. Earliest price band announcement: May-June 2026. IPO open date likely Q2 CY2026 (Q1 FY2027).
- **IPO Structure**: ~2.5% stake dilution, structured mainly as an Offer for Sale (OFS) by existing investors. Expected to raise **$4-4.5 billion**.
- **Filing includes**: Financial statements through December 2025 (Q3 FY26).

## 3. New Energy Division

### Battery Gigafactory

- **40 GWh LFP (lithium iron phosphate) factory** at Jamnagar: Production to begin **H2 CY2026**
- Modular design — scalable to **100 GWh** per annum
- Initial products: Utility-scale BESS solutions, C&I battery packs, residential and telecoms packs
- Located within the **Dhirubhai Ambani Green Energy Giga Complex** (5,000 acres)

Source: [Manufacturing Today](https://www.manufacturingtodayindia.com/reliance-to-begin-battery-gigafactory-operations-in-2026), [ESS News](https://www.ess-news.com/2025/09/01/reliance-industries-to-launch-40-gwh-battery-gigafactory-in-2026/)

### Faradion Sodium-Ion Battery Hub

- **30 GWh sodium-ion battery facility** at Jamnagar (via Faradion, acquired for $135M)
- Sodium-ion cells are **~30% cheaper** than lithium-ion with comparable energy density (~160 Wh/kg)
- Advantages: No lithium/cobalt dependency, sustainable materials, better low-temperature performance
- Reliance expanded its stake in Faradion in late 2024

### Electrolyzer Gigafactory

- **3 GW annual capacity** electrolyzer factory
- Scheduled to start production by **end of CY2026**
- Enables cost-competitive green hydrogen production at global scale

### Solar Manufacturing

- **200 MW HJT (heterojunction technology) solar modules**: First production line commissioned **April 2025**
- Scaling to **10 GW annual capacity** in coming quarters, then to **20 GW**
- Will be the **largest and most integrated single-site solar manufacturing complex globally**
- HJT technology offers higher efficiency (24%+) vs conventional PERC cells

Source: [Mercom India](https://www.mercomindia.com/reliance-industries-rolls-out-first-200-mw-hjt-modules-at-jamnagar)

### Samsung C&T Green Ammonia Offtake Deal

- **$3 billion**, **15-year** binding supply and purchase agreement signed **March 16, 2026**
- One of the **largest long-term green ammonia offtake deals globally**
- Supply begins **H2 FY2029** from the Jamnagar complex
- Produced using renewable power from mega-scale solar + storage in Kutch, feeding integrated green hydrogen and ammonia production at Jamnagar
- Target: **3 million tonnes per annum** of renewable hydrogen equivalent by 2032

> [!tip] Non-Consensus Insight
> The Samsung C&T deal is the first **commercially binding** validation of Reliance's green energy buildout. Until now, the Rs 75,000 crore new energy capex was entirely pre-revenue and viewed skeptically by the market. A $3B, 15-year offtake from a blue-chip Korean conglomerate dramatically de-risks the revenue side. The market has not yet priced in the optionality of similar deals with other Asian/European buyers. If Reliance secures 2-3 more such agreements, the New Energy segment alone could be worth $15-25B on a DCF basis.

Sources: [PV Magazine](https://www.pv-magazine.com/2026/03/20/reliance-industries-samsung-ct-sign-3-billion-green-ammonia-supply-deal/), [Solar Quarter](https://solarquarter.com/2026/03/17/reliance-industries-signs-3-billion-green-ammonia-deal-with-samsung-ct-for-15-year-supply/), [ESG Today](https://www.esgtoday.com/reliance-signs-3-billion-green-ammonia-supply-deal-with-samsung-ct/)

### Integrated Green Energy Ecosystem

The Dhirubhai Ambani Green Energy Giga Complex at Jamnagar houses:
1. Solar module gigafactory (10 GW, scaling to 20 GW)
2. Battery storage gigafactory (40 GWh LFP, scaling to 100 GWh)
3. Sodium-ion battery hub (30 GWh via Faradion)
4. Electrolyzer gigafactory (3 GW)
5. Fuel cell manufacturing
6. Power electronics manufacturing

Marine and land infrastructure at Jamnagar and Kandla connect with solar and hydrogen production sites at Kutch, facilitating large-scale export.

## 4. O2C (Oil-to-Chemicals) Segment

### Q3 FY26 Performance

| Metric | Value | Change |
|--------|-------|--------|
| O2C EBITDA | Rs 16,507 crore | +14.6% YoY |
| Diesel crack spreads | $35-42/bbl | Up from ~$20 |

### Drivers of Current Margin Strength

- **Geopolitical premium**: Middle East supply disruptions (Iran conflict) sharply increased refining and petrochemical spreads
- Diesel cracks surged to $35-42/bbl from ~$20 — this is the primary driver of O2C EBITDA outperformance
- Jefferies: If high spreads continue, RIL FY27 EBITDA could rise ~2% incremental
- Motilal Oswal: 8.5% potential increase if disruptions last through H1 FY27

### India Export Duty Impact

- Government levied duties of Rs 21.5/litre on diesel, Rs 29.5/litre on ATF
- More recently: Diesel export duty **more than doubled** to Rs 55.5/litre; ATF increased to Rs 42/litre
- **~50% of Reliance's refining capacity is protected** from the tax (SEZ-based Jamnagar units), significantly reducing overall impact
- Windfall tax reimposition remains a perpetual overhang

Sources: [Business Standard](https://www.business-standard.com/markets/news/ril-share-price-fall-export-duty-petrol-diesel-impact-126032700393_1.html), [Business League](https://www.businessleague.in/diesel-export-duty-hike-india-2026-govt-doubles-levies-amid-global-war/)

### Chinese Overcapacity Dynamics

- China's PTA capacity **doubled to 92 million tonnes** since 2019; PET capacity doubled over three years to 22 million tonnes
- Polyester chain margins compressed to **$432/MT** (from $475/MT YoY)
- Chinese authorities now stepping up to curb overcapacity — set **2029 deadline** to shut down outdated petrochemical plants
- India's own petrochemical capacity ramp-up faces a market where oversupply is structural
- Recovery timeline: post-2026 capacity build-up slowdown may lead to demand-led recovery, but "satisfactory" profitability unlikely before **early 2030s**

> [!warning] Non-Consensus Insight
> The O2C segment is experiencing a rare **bifurcation**: fuel/refining margins are at multi-year highs due to Iran-driven supply disruption, while petrochemical margins remain structurally depressed by Chinese overcapacity. The market is pricing the elevated fuel cracks as temporary (correct) but may be underestimating how long they persist — the insurance mechanism has killed Hormuz transit more effectively than any navy, and post-ceasefire normalization could take months. Conversely, the petchem recovery is likely to take much longer than consensus expects given China's 2029 shutdown deadline.

Sources: [OilPrice](https://oilprice.com/Energy/Crude-Oil/Chinas-Overcapacity-Forces-Painful-Petrochemicals-Reset.html), [WoodMac](https://www.woodmac.com/news/opinion/petrochemicals-in-peril-oversupply-crisis-and-energy-transition-threaten-industry-survival/)

### US Trade Deal Impact

- Feb 2, 2026: US-India bilateral trade deal — US reduced tariffs on Indian goods from 50% to 18%
- India committed to zero tariffs on US goods, **halting Russian oil purchases**, and shifting to US sourcing
- >90% of Indian oil products imported by US came from Reliance's Jamnagar refinery
- Russian oil waiver **expired April 11, 2026** — India now scrambling for alternative supplies
- Average Indian crude basket: surged from **$69/bbl (Feb 2026) to $113/bbl (March 2026)**

Sources: [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/14/us-hormuz-blockade-hits-india-just-as-russian-oil-purchase-waiver-expires-deepening-energy-worries.html), [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/newsletters/2026-01-20/trump-tariffs-impact-on-indian-exporters-reliance-s-oil-business-challenges)

## 8. Macro Context

### Iran Conflict Impact on India Energy

- India imports **>85% of crude** — ~5.5 million bpd, world's third-largest importer
- Lost ~3 million bpd of crude that previously transited Strait of Hormuz
- Indian crude basket: **$69/bbl (Feb 2026) to $113/bbl (March 2026)** — 64% surge
- Strategic reserves: ~160 million barrels (**~30 days** buffer vs China's 300 days)
- **60% of India's LPG demand** fulfilled by Gulf imports; domestic prices jumped Rs 60/cylinder
- **91% of LPG** comes from Gulf states
- Russian oil waiver expired **April 11, 2026** — compounding supply squeeze
- Finance Ministry warned 7.0-7.4% growth forecast faces "considerable downside risk"

Sources: [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/14/us-hormuz-blockade-hits-india-just-as-russian-oil-purchase-waiver-expires-deepening-energy-worries.html), [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/06/india-iran-oil-imports-strait-hormuz-us-tensions.html), [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/3/27/india-slashes-petrol-diesel-import-taxes-as-fuel-prices-remain-high)

> [!danger] Critical Risk
> The simultaneous loss of Russian crude (waiver expiry) and Hormuz-transiting Gulf crude (Iran conflict) creates a **dual supply shock** unprecedented for India. Reliance's Jamnagar refinery — the world's largest — depends on crude imports. While elevated crack spreads boost refining margins short-term, sustained crude above $100/bbl compresses the Indian consumer economy (fuel inflation, input costs) and could trigger demand destruction that ultimately hurts Reliance's consumer businesses (Retail, Jio subscriber additions among price-sensitive segments).

## Key Non-Consensus Insights Summary

1. **Stub valuation anomaly**: At $130-170B for Jio alone, the market implicitly values Retail + O2C + New Energy at near-zero. This is mathematically untenable — Retail alone had Rs 97,600 crore quarterly revenue and New Energy just signed a $3B Samsung C&T deal.

2. **JioMart's profitability advantage**: Being contribution-margin positive at 1.6M daily orders while competitors burn cash is a structural advantage the market treats as irrelevant. If quick commerce follows the playbook of e-commerce (winner takes most), JioMart's profitable unit economics become decisive once VC funding dries up.

3. **O2C bifurcation**: Fuel cracks at multi-year highs ($35-42/bbl diesel) while petchem margins are structurally depressed. The market is pricing both as temporary, but the Iran-driven fuel premium may persist much longer than consensus expects due to insurance-driven Hormuz closure.

4. **Samsung C&T deal as proof of concept**: The first commercially binding validation of Rs 75,000 crore new energy capex. Market has not priced in the pipeline of similar deals that could follow.

5. **Dual crude supply shock**: Loss of both Russian (waiver expiry April 11) and Gulf crude (Iran conflict) is unprecedented. Reliance is both the largest beneficiary (crack spreads) and the largest victim (input cost inflation) of this dynamic.

6. **ARPU upside optionality**: At Rs 213.7, Jio ARPU remains ~28% below management's Rs 300 target. Every Rs 10 ARPU increase adds approximately Rs 6,000+ crore annual revenue on 515M subscribers.

7. **Akash Ambani elevation**: Pre-IPO MD appointment is designed to resolve succession ambiguity for institutional investors. The real test comes post-IPO when he must operate under public market scrutiny.
