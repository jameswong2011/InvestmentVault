## 7) Key sensitivities to monitor (these change the estimate the most)

1) **Power sourcing mix for data centers**  
IEA expects renewables to meet ~half of incremental data-center electricity, with gas/coal still large contributors. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn9view1Ņłü  
If the realized mix tilts more toward **gas** (or nuclear), silver demand rises less; if it tilts more toward **solar PV**, silver demand rises more.

2) **PV silver per watt trajectory**  
Metals Focus/Silver Institute emphasize ongoing **thrifting** in PV silver loadings. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn19view0Ņłéturn19view1Ņłü  
If that trend stalls (or reverses for technical reasons), the PV channel becomes much larger.

3) **Data-center density (AI GPU clusters)**  
Higher density means fewer facilities but more specialized, high-performance electrical/thermal materials per MW; the Silver Institute notes these environments rely on silverŌĆÖs electrical and thermal properties. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn22view0Ņłü  

4) **Recycling growth**  
Recycling was **193.9 Moz in 2024**. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn13view0Ņłéturn19view0Ņłü  
Meaningful increases in electronics and industrial scrap recovery could offset incremental demand, but scaling recovery takes time and depends on scrap flows and refining capacity.
