# John Deere (DE) — Comprehensive Investment Evaluation

## Key Takeaways

- **Cyclical trough with structural improvements**: FY2025 revenue $45.7B (-17.8%), net income $5.03B (-28%). But trough margins (12.6% operating) are 450bps better than comparable 2016 downturn — reflecting permanent structural enhancements from precision ag, manufacturing efficiency, and product mix.
- **Technology moat deepening**: See & Spray (5M+ acres, 50% herbicide reduction, 90%+ take rate on new combines), autonomous tillage (200K+ acres, commercial production spring 2026), Operations Center (500M+ acres globally). $2.5B R&D annually (~4.5% of sales), 2-3x competitor absolute spending.
- **Cyclical recovery catalysts accumulating**: 2025-2026 identified as cycle trough. Equipment age demographics creating pent-up demand, $40B+ government agricultural programs, interest rate normalization improving financing affordability. Historical 2-3 year recovery phases with earnings exceeding prior peaks.
- **Valuation compressed**: ~$619 share price, trailing P/E ~33x (elevated on trough earnings), forward P/E 20-25x, EV/EBITDA ~15-16x. Market cap $167.4B, EV ~$225B. Base case ~9% annualized return (2026-2028), bull case $850+ (~15%).
- **Recurring revenue target**: 10% of sales from recurring revenue by 2030 (~$6B) via software subscriptions, data services, performance-based contracts.

## Market Share Position

- **NA Large Tractors (>200hp)**: ~55% (vs CNH ~25%, Kubota <5%)
- **NA Combines**: ~50% (vs CNH ~30%)
- **Revenue scale**: $55.6B vs CNH $22.1B vs Kubota $20.1B — 2.5x nearest competitor
- **India**: Growing (26% FY2025 growth) — emerging market expansion reducing NA concentration

## Key Risks

1. **Extended agricultural downturn**: NA large ag demand expected to decline 15-20% in 2026 before recovery
2. **Technology adoption pace**: Autonomous tillage and precision ag adoption slower than projected
3. **Competitor technology narrowing**: CNH/Raven approaching point-solution parity (closing 2025-2027), but platform integration gap stable/widening
4. **Emerging market price competition**: Mahindra, TAFE, Chinese OEMs in India/China/SE Asia
5. **Interest rate sensitivity**: Financing operations ($62.8B debt, 40-50% relates to financial services) sensitive to rate environment
6. **Commodity cycle risk**: Farm income compression directly impacts equipment purchase decisions
