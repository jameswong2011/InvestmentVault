### **$BNGO — Bionano Genomics**

**Technology advantage:** OGM fills a critical gap between karyotyping/FISH (low resolution) and next-gen sequencing (struggles with large structural variants). Installed base reached 371 systems by year-end 2024. They received a Category I CPT code for OGM in hematological malignancies, important for reimbursement. However, the company is struggling financially — full-year 2024 revenue is expected around $30.5-30.7 million, a 15% decrease from 2023.

### **$ISRG — Intuitive Surgical**

**Business model:** Razor-and-blade economics. System sales (~$2M per unit) generate upfront revenue, but the recurring revenue from instruments/accessories (~$1,400+ per procedure, single-use) and service contracts drives profitability. In 2024, approximately 2.68 million procedures were performed on da Vinci systems globally, with 17% year-over-year growth. The installed base reached nearly 10,000 systems.

### **$CLGN — CollPlant Biotechnologies**

**Summary observation:** Your list spans the healthcare innovation spectrum well — from established profitable leaders (ISRG, UTHR) to growth-stage AI platforms (TEM, RXRX, TMDX) to high-risk early-stage plays (RBOT, BNGO, CLGN, LTRN, RENX). The smaller names carry substantial execution and financing risk but represent potentially transformative technologies if they achieve clinical/commercial validation.
