## Peer comparison reveals premium positioning

Notably, **NVIDIA offers superior growth-adjusted value** with a PEG ratio of just 0.68 compared to Broadcom's 1.44, despite NVIDIA's larger absolute market cap. For investors seeking pure AI exposure, NVIDIA's lower forward P/E (23x) and PEG make it mathematically cheaper per unit of growth.

## Valuation reflects AI growth premium versus history

The **PEG ratio of 1.44** suggests reasonable valuation relative to expected 39% EPS growth in FY2026. Analyst consensus projects EPS expanding from $6.81 in FY2025 to $9.45 in FY2026 and $12.44 in FY2027, implying the stock's forward multiple compresses each year if the stock price holds steady.

The trailing P/E of ~90x appears alarming but is inflated by VMware acquisition accounting; the **non-GAAP P/E is closer to 40-45x**. More meaningfully, the **forward P/E of 42x based on FY2025 estimates moderates to approximately 30x on FY2027 projected earnings** if consensus growth materializes.

Current multiples require context around both GAAP accounting distortions and AI-driven growth expectations.

## Investment thesis hinges on AI trajectory

Bear case considerations center on customer concentration risk (reliance on 3-4 hyperscalers), VMware customer churn from aggressive pricing (150-300% price increases), competition from NVIDIA and AMD in custom silicon, and multiple compression if AI spending decelerates.

The bull case for Broadcom requires continued AI infrastructure spending expansion. Goldman Sachs projects Broadcom's AI revenue reaching **$45 billion by FY2026 and $77 billion by FY2027**, representing 128% and 70% year-over-year growth respectively. At those levels, AI would represent over 60% of semiconductor revenue, transforming Broadcom into a fundamentally different company than it was three years ago.
