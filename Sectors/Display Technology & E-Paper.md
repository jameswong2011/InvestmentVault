# Display Technology & E-Paper

## Active Theses
- [[Theses/EINK - E Ink Holdings]] — E Ink Holdings (monopolistic e-paper supplier / ESL adoption wave / ultra-low-power display niche)

## Key industry questions
- Is Chinese OLED capacity (BOE + Visionox + CSOT, ~76% of global OLED capacity by 2025) replaying the LCD displacement playbook against Samsung Display/LG Display — or do repeated BOE yield failures (98% order loss on iPhone 17 Pro) prove that premium OLED is structurally different from commoditized LCD, and does state-subsidized Chinese OLED capacity (RMB ~170B across four mega-fabs) turn the premium tier into another margin-destruction cycle by 2028? Within this backdrop, e-paper sits as a niche, high-margin vector (E Ink monopoly) whose TCO advantage vs LCD signage is being revalued as AI-driven power costs inflate, while MicroLED transitions from lab to commercialization in 2025-2026 via Sanan/JBD/Aledia/Samsung, with AR glasses (Meta Orion, Android XR, iPhone Fold, MacBook OLED) as the next major demand inflection.

## Industry history

### CRT era (1897 – late 1990s)
Cathode Ray Tube (CRT) technology, invented by Karl Ferdinand Braun in 1897, was commercialized as color TV in the early 1950s by RCA (shadow-mask design). Sony broke the RCA stronghold with the **Trinitron aperture-grille CRT** introduced April 1968 (12-inch KV-1210), which passed ~75% of electron beam energy to phosphor versus ~15% for shadow-mask (Spectrum IEEE). Sony sold 280 million Trinitron units over the following decades, establishing Japanese consumer electronics as a world-class category and funding Sony's broader platform expansion. By the late 1990s LCD had begun displacing CRTs in monitors, with TV displacement following 2005-2010.

### Plasma era and its rapid death (1997 – 2014)
Plasma Display Panels (PDPs) were mass-marketed by Fujitsu (1997), Panasonic, Pioneer, and Samsung. Pioneer's **Kuro** line (2007) achieved near-infinite contrast and is still regarded as a reference black level by enthusiasts — but Pioneer exited plasma in **2009**, selling its IP to Panasonic. **Panasonic terminated plasma production in December 2013**, ending sales March 2014; LG and Samsung exited plasma later in 2014. The death was driven by (i) inability to match LCD cost curve after Chinese Gen-8.5 investment, (ii) difficulty scaling to 4K, (iii) energy-efficiency regulations in EU/US that penalized plasma's ~300W-class draw, (iv) inability to hit thin form factors demanded by wall-mounting.

### LCD rise (1970s – 2020s), Korean then Chinese dominance
Passive-matrix LCD commercialized in calculators (Sharp, 1973) and watches in the 1970s. Active-matrix TFT-LCD emerged in the 1990s. Korea became the volume leader via **Samsung** (first Gen-7 fab, 2005) and **LG Display** (then LG.Philips LCD, formed 1999). Chinese displacement began with **BOE Technology** (founded 1993 as an employee spin-off of Beijing Electron Tube Factory), which received an estimated **$3.9 billion in state subsidies 2010-2021** (ITIF) plus USD 536M in 2023 alone (Global Trade Alert). By 2025, **China holds ~69% of global LCD capacity**, Taiwan ~19%, Korea ~4%, Japan <4% — with 32" LCD TV panels trading at ~$75 at cycle trough vs peak $230+ (TrendForce). **Sharp exited large-format LCD at its Sakai Gen-10 fab in August 2024**; the Sakai site is being converted to an AI data center by SoftBank for ~JPY 100B (Strata-gee). **LG Display sold its final LCD TV fab (Guangzhou P10.5) to TCL CSOT in September 2024 for ~CNY 10.8B ($1.5B)** with the remaining 20% stake transferred January 2026 for a total CNY 13.4B ($1.8B) (TrendForce).

### OLED emergence (1987 – present)
**Ching Wan Tang and Steven Van Slyke at Eastman Kodak built the first practical OLED device in 1987**, using a two-layer organic small-molecule structure. Kodak licensed the technology broadly; Pioneer commercialized small-molecule OLED in 1997, followed by TDK (2001) and Samsung-NEC Mobile Display / SNMD (2002). **Samsung launched the first commercial AMOLED display in 2003**, pivoting to mobile dominance by 2007. **LG Display acquired Kodak's OLED IP in April 2009**, focusing on white-OLED (WOLED) with color filter for large-panel TV; **LG launched the first WOLED TVs in 2013**. Samsung Display now dominates small-to-medium OLED (smartphones) with **48% revenue share** in 2025 vs 21% for LG Display and 14% for BOE (UBI Research/CounterPoint). **QD-OLED**, Samsung Display's hybrid (blue OLED + quantum dot color conversion layer), entered mass production in 2022; by March 2026 cumulative shipments exceeded **5 million monitor panels** and Samsung Display holds ~75% of the monitor OLED market (Korea Times).

### MicroLED origins (2000s research – 2025 commercial era)
Self-emissive inorganic microLED research dates to late 1990s/early 2000s (Hongxing Jiang, Texas Tech). **Sony launched CLEDIS (Crystal LED) in 2016** at 1.3m-class commercial sizes; Samsung unveiled **The Wall** (modular 146-inch MicroLED) at CES 2018, now selling at ~$150,000+ unit prices. **2025 marked the first meaningful commercial production ramp** with three high-volume fabs: ENNOSTAR (Taiwan), HC SemiTek (Yangzhou), and Sanan Optoelectronics (Xiamen/Hubei). Apple's microLED Apple Watch project was **cancelled in March 2024** — Ams-Osram took a $650-900M impairment, Kulicke & Soffa took $110-130M (TrendForce).

### E-paper (1970s – present)
Xerox PARC's **Gyricon** (late 1970s, Nick Sheridon) originated rotating-bead electrophoretic technology. **E Ink Corporation was founded at MIT Media Lab in 1997** by Joseph Jacobson. Sony launched the first commercial e-reader using E Ink (Librie, 2004); Amazon followed with Kindle (2007). **E Ink acquired SiPix in 2012**, consolidating microcup electrophoretic IP. Competitor technologies failed at commercialization: **Qualcomm Mirasol** (IMOD MEMS, acquired Iridigm 2004 for $170M, wrote off $300M and discontinued 2013); **Liquavista** (electrowetting, acquired by Samsung 2011, then Amazon 2013, shuttered 2018); ChLCD (IRIS Optronics); electrochromic (RDI). E Ink's current monopoly and technology transitions (OED, Spectra 6, Kaleido, Gallery) are covered in [[Theses/EINK - E Ink Holdings]].

## Competitive dynamics

### Market share by technology (2025)
- **Global display capacity by country (2025, Omdia/Counterpoint)**: China 69% LCD and 76% OLED; South Korea ~8-9%; Taiwan ~19% (mainly LCD); Japan <4%.
- **OLED revenue share (2025)**: Samsung Display 48%, LG Display 21%, BOE 14%, Tianma 6%, others (Visionox, CSOT, Everdisplay) ~11% (UBI Research).
- **OLED TV global (2024)**: LG Electronics 52.4% unit / 47.2% revenue; Samsung Electronics 27.3% unit / 35.4% revenue. In Q1 2025 Samsung surpassed LG in North America OLED TV for the first time (Samsung 45.2% vs LG 42% unit share).
- **Monitor OLED (2025)**: Samsung Display's QD-OLED ~75% share; 2025 QD-OLED monitor shipments ~2.5M units; cumulative shipments exceeded 5M by March 2026.
- **Apple smartphone OLED allocation (iPhone 17, 2025, ~89M panels)**: Samsung Display 57M (64%), LG Display 30M (34%), BOE 1.3M (1.4%) — BOE share collapsed from prior plans due to Mianyang B11 yield failures in late 2025.
- **Automotive OLED (2025)**: Samsung Display >70% share (cost/scale advantage in rigid OLED), BOE ~13%; Tianma ranks #1 globally in automotive display units (20% share) and instrument clusters (27%) by unit.
- **LCD panel share (2024, by area)**: BOE #1, CSOT #2 (post-Guangzhou deal), Innolux, AUO, HKC, Sharp, JDI; top 4 Chinese players command >70% of LCD capacity.

### Pricing power trajectory
- **LCD**: structurally weak. 32" panel troughed ~$75 in late 2025 (TrendForce); 55" and 65" also bottomed in Q4 2025. Korean exit (LG sold Guangzhou, Sharp closed Sakai) removed 2 Gen-10.5 lines but Chinese consolidation (BOE + CSOT now ~50%+ combined) has restored some discipline — panel makers running 80-90% utilization to avoid flooding the market. Prices stabilized Dec 2025 and are expected to rise in early 2026 on memory-chip cost pass-through (TrendForce).
- **OLED mobile**: Samsung Display maintains premium pricing; Samsung Display operating margin ~14% in 2025 on KRW 29.9T revenue (~$23B), KRW 4.2T operating profit (~$3.2B). BOE operates at ~3% OLED operating margin. Samsung Display shipped 125M smartphone OLEDs to Apple in 2025 (78M for iPhone 17); LG Display shipped 75.1M. BOE's attempts to close the quality gap stalled repeatedly: late-2025 Mianyang B11 fab process defects led Apple to cancel iPhone 17 Pro orders and shift volumes back to Samsung Display.
- **OLED TV**: LG Display near-monopoly on WOLED (ceased LCD TV panel production 2022, sold last LCD fab 2024). Samsung Display QD-OLED is the quality leader in sub-85" premium. LG Display 2025 full-year OLED revenue mix hit **61%** (first profitable year since 2020), revenue KRW 25.8T / $18B, operating profit KRW 517B / $360M.
- **QD-OLED monitors**: peak brightness 1,300 nits, 360Hz refresh (2026 V-Stripe), 21:9 ultra-wide. Supplied to 7 monitor OEMs including ASUS, MSI.

### New entrant threats
- **Visionox**: 8.6-Gen V5 IT OLED line in Hefei, $7.6B-$7.7B total investment, government-funded ~80% of capex; Hefei municipal government owns 31.9% after $412M infusion. ViP (Visionox intelligent Pixelization) is maskless OLED patterning targeting cost and yield advantage vs FMM.
- **TCL CSOT**: T8 Gen-8.6 **inkjet-printed OLED** line in Guangzhou, ~CNY 29.5B ($4B) total investment — world's first 8.6-gen IJP OLED. Printed OLED theoretically reduces material waste ~90% vs FMM deposition.
- **Jade Bird Display (JBD)**: MicroLED microdisplay (near-eye AR/VR); raised $140M Series B2 led by Hundun + CITIC Jingshi; backers include Alibaba, Samsung, BYD, Geely. Hummingbird Mini II 0.15cc single-green panels used in Alibaba's Quark AI glasses; also Rokid, Xiaomi, TCL, Vuzix, reportedly Meta Orion.
- **Sanan Optoelectronics**: achieved 22% external quantum efficiency on GaN-on-Si red chips for MicroLED — industry-leading.
- **Aledia (France)**: 3D GaN nanowire microLED on 200mm/300mm Si wafers; total funding ~$600M (~€360M disclosed); opened $200M production line 2024; FlexiNOVA epiwafer platform launched May 2025.

### Strategic pivots
- **LG Display**: exited LCD TV panels entirely. OLED revenue mix rose from 32% (2020) → 55% (2024) → **61% (2025)**. First profit in 4 years. $1.4B OLED capex planned for 2026.
- **Samsung Display**: exited LCD (closed L8 Gen-8 fab 2022) and fully OLED. A6 Gen-8.6 IT OLED line in Asan repurposed from old L8 LCD fab, ~$3.1B investment ($4.1T KRW per Chairman Lee). Mass production begins May 2026, targeting Apple MacBook Pro OLED (exclusive 3-year supply agreement).
- **Sharp**: withdrew from LCD TV panel business; Kameyama No. 2 LCD fab sale to Foxconn collapsed in 2026 after Foxconn reassessed; Sakai converted to AI data center (SoftBank).
- **JDI**: halting OLED at Mobara fab by March 2026; no longer supplies Apple. Pursuing ~$13B US display fab with Japanese/US government backing (concept stage).

### Why JDI/Sharp keep failing
High capital intensity (an 8.6-Gen OLED line is $4-9B fab cost) collides with Chinese state-subsidized entrants covering 50-70% of capex via land/capital grants, zero-interest loans, and R&D subsidies (ITIF). Japanese capital markets will not fund loss-making volume LCD because return-on-capital is deeply negative vs BOE/CSOT's subsidized cost base. BOE alone received ~$3.9B in subsidies 2010-2021; its six largest investors are state-owned entities from Beijing, Chongqing, and Hefei (23.8% combined stake as of Q3 2020). JDI is now dependent on Ichigo Trust ($920M bailout, 2020) after three INCJ bailouts.

## Product level analysis

### LCD sub-types

| Type | Full name | Contrast | Viewing angle | Response | Color | Use case |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **TN** | Twisted Nematic | ~800-1,000:1 | 90-120° | 1-5 ms | 60-70% sRGB | Cheap monitors, e-sports |
| **VA** | Vertical Alignment | 3,000:1+ (static) | ~160° | 2-3 ms | 90-95% sRGB | TVs (HDR), monitors |
| **IPS** | In-Plane Switching | 800-1,200:1 | 178° | 4-8 ms | 95-100% sRGB, P3, AdobeRGB | Professional monitors, MacBooks, tablets |
| **PLS** | Plane-to-Line Switching (Samsung's IPS variant) | ~1,000:1 | 178° | 4-8 ms | 95-100% sRGB | Samsung budget alternatives to IPS |
| **Oxide TFT (IGZO/LTPO)** | Indium-Gallium-Zinc-Oxide, LTPO (low-temp polycrystalline oxide) | — | — | Variable refresh | — | Apple ProMotion, wearables |

### OLED sub-types
- **AMOLED (Active Matrix OLED)** — Samsung Display's mobile dominance. RGB subpixels deposited via Fine Metal Mask (FMM); each pixel is self-emissive.
- **WOLED (White OLED)** — LG Display TV. Stacked blue/yellow OLED with color filter. Simpler to mass produce large panels; slightly lower peak brightness + color volume than QD-OLED.
- **QD-OLED** — Samsung Display. Blue OLED drives quantum-dot color-conversion layer (R/G). Higher color volume and peak brightness than WOLED (1,500+ nits on Gen-4 panels); ~75% monitor OLED share.
- **Tandem OLED** — 2-stack OLED for longer lifetime / higher brightness; used in iPad Pro M4/M5 (Samsung SDC + LG Display) and 2026 MacBook Pro (Samsung A6 Gen-8.6 exclusive).
- **Foldable OLED** — UTG (Ultra-Thin Glass, Samsung's preferred covering) vs CPI (Colorless Polyimide). Samsung Display claims 20% crease reduction via UTG refinement in Galaxy Z Fold 8 generation. Apple's foldable iPhone (Q3 2026 launch expected) uses Samsung Display 7.5" CoE (Color-filter-on-Encapsulation) panel exclusively for 3 years.
- **Blue phosphorescent OLED** — LG Display mass-produced first blue PHOLED in May 2025 after 8 months of joint work with Universal Display; represents the "final dream OLED" with substantially improved power efficiency.

### MiniLED vs MicroLED
- **MiniLED**: LCD backlight enhancement with ~1,000-10,000 local dimming zones using 100-200μm LEDs. Still LCD panel at front; color still via color filter; blooming/halo possible on dark backgrounds; but >2,000 nits peak brightness and lower cost than OLED. Samsung Neo QLED, Apple Pro Display XDR, iPad Pro 12.9" (M2 era).
- **MicroLED**: true self-emissive inorganic GaN/InGaN LEDs <50μm. No color filter (R/G/B grown or transferred directly). Theoretical brightness >10,000 nits; no burn-in; modular (tileable). Barrier: mass transfer yield (billions of micro chips per 4K panel). Commercial deployments: Samsung The Wall (146"+), Sony CLEDIS (146"-219"), LG Magnit.

### QLED vs QD-OLED (pricing/marketing clarification)
- **QLED** = Samsung marketing for LCD + quantum dot color enhancement film (QDEF). Still LCD technology; not self-emissive. Brightness and longevity benefits but pixel-level contrast limited by backlight.
- **Neo QLED** = Samsung MiniLED + QDEF.
- **QD-OLED** = blue OLED + QD color conversion. Self-emissive. Different product despite similar marketing.

### E-paper / reflective display technologies
- **Electrophoretic (E Ink)** — charged pigment particles in microcapsules. Detailed competitive analysis in [[Theses/EINK - E Ink Holdings]].
- **Cholesteric LCD (ChLCD)** — IRIS Optronics (AUO subsidiary); reflective; supports color. Niche adoption.
- **MEMS (Qualcomm Mirasol IMOD)** — discontinued 2013 after $300M write-off.
- **Electrowetting (Liquavista)** — discontinued by Amazon 2018.
- **Electrochromic** — industrial/automotive mirrors; not a mainstream reading display.

### Flexible/foldable cover materials
- **UTG** (Ultra-Thin Glass, ~30μm, Corning/SCHOTT and Samsung-affiliated): harder surface, better crease reduction; Samsung Display preferred. Apple reportedly adopting UTG for iPhone Fold.
- **CPI** (Colorless Polyimide film, Kolon/SKC): softer, lower-cost; used in some Chinese foldables.

### AR/VR microdisplays
- **MicroOLED (Si-OLED)** — Sony (Vision Pro 1 supplier, 4K), BOE, SeeYa (2.5K, Bigscreen Beyond), Samsung Display (acquired eMagin 2023 for $218M; RGB Direct Emitter Patterning = 54.6% of microOLED revenue 2025). Samsung Display D-Week 2025 prototype: 1.4", 5,000 PPI, 5K×5K, 15,000 nits peak, 120Hz.
- **LCoS (Liquid Crystal on Silicon)** — Himax (HIMX) with AUO; Front-lit LCoS for AR glasses shown at CES 2026; ultra-slim, ultra-low-power. Also Google (Raxium acquisition 2022 pivot from LCoS to microLED).
- **MicroLED microdisplays** — JBD (leader), Aledia, Playnitride; used in Meta Orion prototype (~$10,000/unit BOM, not productized), Rokid, Xiaomi, Alibaba Quark AI glasses.

### Automotive displays
- Large format P2P (Pillar-to-Pillar) single-panel: LG Display 51" P2P OLED (Sony Honda Mobility Afeela), 40" with switchable privacy.
- LTPS TFT LCD: 45% automotive share 2025 (Omdia); OLED 9% growing to premium applications.
- Content per vehicle: ~$80 (2020) → ~$200+ (2030) with premium projected >$500 in luxury multi-display cockpits (Omdia).

## Macro shifts

### 1. Chinese state-capitalism overhang across tiers
LCD commoditization was the first wave; OLED is now being replicated with RMB ~170B public-private spending across four 8.6-gen fabs, and MicroLED is the third wave in preparation. The question is whether each tier behaves identically. Samsung Display / LG Display argue **quality gap is structural** (BOE iPhone 17 Pro yield failure in late 2025 is supporting evidence); Chinese proponents argue gap narrows with every generation (BOE now supplies >50% of iPhone 17e budget volumes, Visionox + Tianma absorbed Samsung's displaced Galaxy allocation when BOE was kicked out Nov 2024).

### 2. AI data center power costs reshape e-paper TCO
Energy cost inflation (AI-driven industrial demand + policy) improves e-paper signage economics vs LCD. E Ink's Fraimic signage partner claims ~97% lifecycle energy reduction vs LCD. ESL total shipments hit ~500M units 2025 (+25% YoY); Walmart's rollout is the marquee driver. Full product depth and Spectra 6 / Gallery 3 competitive dynamics in [[Theses/EINK - E Ink Holdings]].

### 3. Apple OLED transition spreading to Mac
- iPad Pro adopted OLED 2024 (Samsung + LG tandem supply).
- **MacBook Pro OLED Q4 2026** (Samsung A6 Gen-8.6 exclusive, with M6 Pro/Max 2nm chips, potentially touchscreen Dynamic Island).
- **iPad mini 8** OLED expected late 2026.
- **iMac OLED** in development, timing fluid.
- Each Apple product transition to OLED adds 10-40M panel demand; structurally grows Samsung Display / LG Display revenue while bypassing BOE until yields qualify.

### 4. Foldable inflection via iPhone Fold
- Samsung foldable shipments ~10M/yr plateau.
- **Apple iPhone Fold launch September 2026** expected with **Samsung Display exclusive 3-year panel supply** (7.5" CoE foldable). Volume targets 8-11M in 2026, potentially 20M, triple by 2027.
- LG Display not qualified for foldable; BOE foldable quality below Apple spec.

### 5. VR/AR microOLED supply constraints
- Vision Pro Gen 2 (2026) rumor supply: BOE + SeeYa as alternative to Sony.
- Samsung Galaxy XR launching with Sony + Samsung Display (eMagin-derived) dual supply.
- Meta Orion research prototype at ~$10K BOM — not productized; consumer Meta AR glasses expected post-2027.
- Google Android XR glasses using Raxium-derived microLED; 2026 launch monocular first.

### 6. Automotive displays exploding per-vehicle content
- $80 (2020) → $200+ (2030) avg; ultra-premium multi-display cockpits >$500.
- LTPS TFT LCD at 45% of 2025 automotive; OLED 9% and rising.
- LG Display leading P2P OLED 51"; Tianma leads unit volume (20% auto share).
- Samsung Display >70% rigid automotive OLED.

### 7. Tariff and geopolitics
- **Section 301 (25%) remains in force** on Chinese electronics after Supreme Court Feb 2026 struck IEEPA-based tariffs; effective duty ~35% on Chinese smartphones, laptops, and components.
- **USTR initiated Section 301 investigations March 11 2026 on 16 economies including Taiwan** for "structural excess capacity" — comments due April 15 2026. Taiwan IEEPA 15% rate (user input) has not been independently confirmed in public sources; tariff environment remains fluid.
- **DoD pressure on BOE**: Sept 2024 House Select Committee on CCP pressed DoD to classify BOE as Chinese military company; Apple reviewing continued use.
- **Dual-sourcing away from China** emerging in signage/server/industrial; Taiwanese and Korean panels relatively more attractive when they avoid highest 301 lines.

### 8. Raw materials and supply chain
- **Iridium** — key PHOLED dopant; Universal Display manages supply. Limited global mine production concentrated in South Africa + Russia.
- **Glass substrates** — **Corning (GLW) holds >60% large-area Gen-8+ substrate share**; vs AGC and NEG. Corning Gen-10.5 fab investment shared with customers via LTAs. Corning 2025 core sales $16.41B (+13% YoY).
- **Polarizers** — Nitto Denko + Sumitomo Chemical + LG Chem (Shanjin Optoelectronics) control ~71% of market (2025 market ~$6.87B). BenQ Materials, Samsung SDI, CMMT also relevant.
- **Driver ICs** — Novatek (TV/monitor + OLED TDDI), Samsung LSI (vertical), MediaTek, LX Semicon, Himax. Top 5 control ~65% revenue. Market size ~$8.91B 2025 projected; 9.24% CAGR to $13.86B by 2030.
- **Deposition equipment** — **Canon Tokki has near-monopoly on OLED deposition** for Gen-6/8.6; Ulvac (Samsung projects), Sunic System (Korean alternative used by BOE/Visionox for some lines).
- **QD materials** — Shoei Chemical (post-Nanosys, 2023) dominant; 100% of Nanosys QD production now at Shoei; new Itoshima Japan plant under construction.

## Investor heuristics

### Consensus views
1. LCD is a commoditized Chinese-dominated business with minimal profitability; Korean/Japanese exits vindicate the thesis.
2. OLED is a Samsung/LG duopoly slowly being eroded by BOE/Visionox/CSOT with Chinese state backing; eventually Chinese OLED catches up and crushes margins like LCD did.
3. MicroLED is a decade away from consumer TV mass-market.
4. E-paper is a niche for ESL and e-readers; not a display-industry volume driver.
5. Samsung Display and LG Display derive premium margin from Apple exclusivity and foldable/tandem technical moats; Apple is the gating dependency for display vendor economics.
6. Display is a cyclical capital-intensive commodity; valuations should reflect low-teens multiples on trough earnings.

### Where consensus could be wrong
- **BOE OLED displacement of Samsung is not linear.** Late-2025 B11 Mianyang yield failure → 98% loss of iPhone 17 Pro allocation is not a blip; it's the 2nd major yield failure for BOE on Apple in 18 months. Premium OLED may be structurally higher-barrier than LCD because (a) tandem architectures, (b) LTPO backplane complexity, (c) CoE integration, and (d) FMM/ViP/IJP process nuances compound. Chinese state capital can fund capex but not yield learning.
- **E-paper disruptive at TCO-level vs LCD signage as AI power costs inflate.** The Fraimic/Samsung bio-resin energy argument is becoming investable, not just aspirational — ESL shipments +25% YoY 2025 reaching ~500M units, with retail signage (Walmart driver) as the emerging vector.
- **MicroLED cost curve may compress faster than consensus.** 2025 was the first commercial production year (ENNOSTAR, HC SemiTek, Sanan). Sanan at 22% EQE on red GaN-on-Si is parity-level. JBD microdisplay unit pricing in AR glasses is dropping fast due to Alibaba + Xiaomi volume. Ironically, MicroLED consumer TV is still 5-10 years out, but microLED microdisplay for AR is already commercial.
- **Foldable OLED may be a step change, not a niche.** Samsung Display's 3-year Apple iPhone Fold exclusive could reset the segment. At 8-11M in 2026 and triple by 2027, foldable ASP premium of 2-3x supports Samsung Display's margin moat.
- **Korean display makers may have permanently lost LCD but built a structural OLED + MicroLED moat**, not just a window. LG Display's 61% OLED mix (first profitable year in 4 years) suggests the transition was economically justified despite the traumatic exit. Samsung Display's tandem OLED + eMagin microOLED + MicroLED IP stack is not easily replicable by Chinese subsidy.
- **Chinese state support is shifting from LCD → OLED → MicroLED**, with different strategic implications. LCD subsidies are in run-off; OLED is peak subsidy 2024-2027 (Visionox $7.6B with 80% government equity; BOE Chengdu $8.7B; TCL CSOT Guangzhou $4.0B); MicroLED subsidies are ramping via Sanan, JBD, local provinces. Investors who model China's state-capitalism as monolithic will miss tier-specific profitability.
