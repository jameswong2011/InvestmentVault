## Key Dynamics
- **India's alternatives market growing at ~30% CAGR; Edelweiss owns the leading GP platform.** EAAA AUM at ₹68,175 crore with 33% YoY fee-paying AUM growth. CDPQ committed $1.3B to stressed assets partnership. Pre-IPO placement (March 2026) implied ₹8,500 crore valuation for EAAA. Near-monopoly in Bharat Bond passives (~50% share of debt passive funds).
- **The "incubate, scale, monetize" playbook is validated by four capital events.** Nuvama (+215% post-listing), WestBridge MF (57x PAT), Nido/Carlyle (₹2,100 crore), and EAAA pre-IPO placement. The market applies a holding-company complexity discount rather than pricing this as a repeatable platform factory.
- **EAAA IPO DRHP re-filed with SEBI (January 2026) after March 2025 rejection.** Approval pending as of April 2026. Timeline has slipped from initial April 2026 target; realistic launch likely H2 CY2026.
- **Nido Home Finance sale to Carlyle (₹2,100 crore) is the near-term deleveraging catalyst.** 99.4% shareholder approval secured; expected completion by July 2026. Corporate net debt at ₹6,520 crore, targeting near-zero in ~3 years.
- **Competitive dynamics shifting.** 360 ONE's AIF AUM approaching ₹51,000 crore through acquisitive strategy (UBS India, Batlivala & Karani). Distribution-led vs. EAAA's GP manufacturing model — different moats, but the gap is narrowing.
- **US insurance broking is an adjacent opportunity worth monitoring.** Research on the US insurance broking market highlights structural consolidation trends and fee-based revenue models relevant to India's financial services evolution.

## Cross-Sector: Stablecoin Regulation Reshaping Financial Services

**The GENIUS Act (July 2025) and advancing CLARITY Act are creating a parallel, regulated payments infrastructure that directly competes with correspondent banking, cross-border FX, and traditional remittance corridors.** Circle's CPN (launched April 8, 2026) enlists Santander, Deutsche Bank, SocGen, and Standard Chartered as founding partners — enabling banks to settle cross-border payments in USDC/EURC without holding crypto. This represents the first time Tier 1 global banks are actively participating in stablecoin settlement infrastructure. The cross-border payments TAM is $238-397B in revenue on $208T in volume. B2B stablecoin payments surged from <$100M/month (early 2023) to $6B/month (mid-2025) — a 60x increase. Stripe/Bridge received OCC conditional approval for a national trust bank charter, signaling stablecoin issuance moving into the regulated banking perimeter. The implications for traditional financial services: (1) correspondent banking fees are under direct attack from instant stablecoin settlement; (2) FX intermediaries face disintermediation as USDC→EURC conversion replaces multi-bank currency chains; (3) remittance companies (Western Union, MoneyGram) face 1-2% stablecoin costs vs 6-8% traditional; (4) banks choosing to *integrate* stablecoins (via CPN) may gain volume that offsets lost intermediary fees. The GENIUS Act yield prohibition protects bank deposit franchises from direct stablecoin competition on rates, but not from competition on payments efficiency. *(See: [[Theses/CRCL - Circle Internet Group]], [[Macro/Stablecoin Regulation as Geopolitical Infrastructure]], [[Research/2025-07-15 - Visa Mastercard Stablecoin Competition]])*
