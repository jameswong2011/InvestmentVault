## Key Dynamics
- **Cheniere's toll-road model is the most misunderstood business model in energy — and the April 2026 ceasefire doesn't change the structural story.** The market prices Cheniere as a commodity company, but ~95% of cash flows come from take-or-pay SPAs with fixed liquefaction fees ($2.25-3.50/MMBtu). Management's stated sensitivity: <$50M EBITDA per $1/MMBtu change on <1 mtpa open capacity. The ceasefire reduced JKM from mid-$20s to high-$17s, compressing spot upside, but the contracted base ($120B+ through 2050) is unaffected. Meanwhile, Ras Laffan's 12.8 mtpa damage requires 2-4 year gas turbine lead times for repair — the supply gap persists regardless of Hormuz reopening. Cheniere trades at 7.5-8.5x trailing EV/EBITDA vs a 5-year avg of ~9.8x despite a strengthening franchise. *(Updated Apr 15, 2026)*
- **The insurance mechanism — not navies — now controls commercial maritime energy flows, creating permanent normalization lags after geopolitical events.** War risk premiums surged from 0.125% to 1-3% of hull value during the Hormuz crisis, with Lloyd's redesignating the entire Persian Gulf as a conflict zone. Even post-ceasefire, Iran is charging $1M+ tolls per transit and insurers demand sustained proof of safety before repricing. For LNG (no pipeline bypass, unlike crude's 3.5-5.5 mb/d alternatives), this means commercial flow normalization takes months, not days — systematically underpriced by equity investors anchoring to crude oil signals. This dynamic advantages Cheniere (Atlantic Basin, zero Hormuz exposure) and disadvantages Qatar/Middle Eastern suppliers permanently. *(Updated Apr 15, 2026)*
- **The US LNG competitive landscape is crowded but Cheniere's execution credibility moat is widening with every competitor stumble.** Venture Global won the Repsol arbitration but TotalEnergies publicly refuses new contracts; Golden Pass's contractor went bankrupt; Tellurian sold for $900M after burning $1B. Cheniere completed CCL Stage 3 Trains 1-5 on/ahead of schedule, reached FID on Trains 8&9, and is advancing SPL Expansion (~20 mtpa) toward a 2027 FID. In an industry where offtaker trust determines FID, execution record compounds. *(Updated Apr 15, 2026)*
- **The analyst coverage structure creates a structural mispricing of Reliance — energy desks undervalue the technology platform.** Jio Platforms (515.3M subscribers, 51.8% EBITDA margins, $130-180B standalone valuation, Meta JV for enterprise AI, 485 patents in 5G/6G) is valued using conglomerate-discount frameworks rather than platform-premium frameworks. Consumer businesses now generate 54% of consolidated EBITDA. At ₹1,220, the stock prices maximum pessimism while 33/36 analysts rate Buy with +23-41% implied upside — the widest gap between consensus and price in Reliance's recent history. Jio IPO targeting Q1 FY2027 (by July 2026), raising $4-6B at $130-180B valuation, 17 banks appointed (Morgan Stanley, Goldman Sachs). *(Updated Apr 15, 2026)*
- **Reliance's O2C segment is experiencing a rare bifurcation — fuel cracks at multi-year highs while petchem margins are structurally depressed.** Diesel cracks surged to $35-42/bbl on Iran-Hormuz disruption, driving O2C EBITDA to ₹16,507 crore (+14.6% YoY). But China's PTA capacity doubled to 92M tonnes since 2019, compressing polyester chain margins to $432/MT with recovery not expected before early 2030s. Indian export duties more than doubled (diesel ₹55.5/litre), though ~50% of Jamnagar capacity is SEZ-protected. The insurance mechanism driving Hormuz closure creates normalization lags of months — fuel cracks may persist longer than consensus expects. India faces a dual crude supply shock: Russian waiver expired April 11 + Hormuz disruption; Indian crude basket surged $69→$113/bbl. *(Updated Apr 15, 2026)*
- **Samsung C&T's $3B/15-year green ammonia offtake is the first commercial validation of Reliance's ₹75,000 crore new energy buildout.** The Dhirubhai Ambani Green Energy Giga Complex at Jamnagar (5,000 acres, 4x Tesla Gigafactory) integrates solar manufacturing (200 MW HJT commissioned, scaling to 10-20 GW), battery storage (40 GWh LFP, H2 CY2026 commissioning), sodium-ion batteries (30 GWh via Faradion, 30% cheaper than Li-ion), and 3 GW electrolyzers. This vertically integrated solar-to-ammonia architecture is globally unique. Each additional comparable offtake deal adds $5-8B to new energy segment valuation (currently priced at near-zero). *(Updated Apr 15, 2026)*
- **Product tankers — not crude tankers — capture the tighter barrel in the Hormuz crisis, and STNG is the infrastructure play the market mislabels as a commodity trade.** Diesel cracks have hit $44-50/bbl (5-year avg ~$28/bbl), jet fuel cracks peaked near $100/bbl, and Singapore gasoil surged 57% vs Brent at 55-60% — refined products are moving more than crude because no pipeline bypass exists for products at Hormuz (unlike crude's 9-10M bbl/day bypass capacity). STNG's 90-vessel fleet (34 LR2, 42 MR, 14 Handymax) has generated Q1 2026 TCEs of $32-51k/day, with Q2 LR2 bookings at $101k/day (16% fixed). The balance sheet transformation from $3.1B net debt to $334M net cash, $11,000/day cash breakeven, and ~$860M in cumulative buybacks makes this a fortress balance sheet earning crisis-level rates. The dual blockade (Iran IRGC closure + US counter-blockade post-April 12 talk collapse) and July normalisation timeline even under full reopening provides rate support through Q2/Q3 minimum. Hafnia-TORM consolidation (13.97% stake, Dec 2025) signals industry repricing that benefits STNG as either consolidator or premium target. Key risk: ceasefire expires April 21 and diplomatic resolution could collapse rates within weeks. *(Updated Apr 15, 2026)*

Oil services stocks activate. Every prior spike has ended in recession within 12-18 months.

## Thermal Coal & LNG Substitution

*The Hormuz closure has removed ~20% of global LNG supply, triggering the most severe gas-to-coal switching since the 1970s.*

### Core Thesis

The permanent destruction of 12.5 MTPA of Qatari LNG capacity provides a multi-year structural floor under coal prices regardless of ceasefire outcome. Gas-to-coal switching of **40-90 million tonnes** is underway across Asia and Europe. No coal passes through the Strait of Hormuz — making it the most accessible alternative to disrupted Middle East gas. 2022 precedent: Newcastle coal surged from ~$47/t to an all-time high of $443/t; coal-TTF gas correlation reached 0.92.

### Four Price Scenarios (Newcastle thermal coal, 6-month horizon)

| Scenario | Probability | Newcastle Price | Key Assumptions |
|----------|------------|----------------|-----------------|
| **A: Prolonged disruption 6+ months** | 45% | $185-245/t | Hormuz closed through Q2; Qatar LNG offline; full switching materializes |
| **B: Partial reopening 3-4 months** | 30% | $150-185/t | Ceasefire by May-June; Qatar restarts 84%; insurance premium persists |
| **C: Quick resolution 1-3 months** | 15% | $125-140/t (peak $150-165) | Diplomatic breakthrough; 12.5 MTPA still permanently destroyed |
| **D: Escalation** | 10% | $250-350/t | Attacks on coal export infra or global recession triggers |
| **Probability-weighted target** | — | **~$198/t** | **35-40% upside from ~$140-146/t; ~5:1 risk/reward** |

### Demand: Japan and South Korea Are Primary Switching Markets

- **Japan**: 47 GW coal fleet can offset 70% of gas-fired generation; potential 15-30 MT/yr additional imports
- **South Korea**: 61 coal units can offset >100% of gas generation; 10-18 MT/yr additional
- **Europe**: Constrained by retirements (11 GW in 2024) and ~€87/t carbon prices; 20-40 MT/yr potential (Poland, Germany, Italy)
- **SE Asia**: Bangladesh, Pakistan, Thailand, Vietnam, Philippines add 13-23 MT/yr (infrastructure-limited)

### Supply: Infrastructure Caps Upside

- **Indonesia**: Cut production from 836 MT → 600 MT target; new 1-5% export tax; DMO may rise to 30%+
- **Australia**: Latent capacity ~55% higher, but Hunter Valley rail/port bottlenecks are binding
- **South Africa**: Richards Bay 91 MT/yr capacity, Transnet rail delivers only ~71 MT

### Five Key Risks

1. **Global recession**: IEA estimates 12 mb/d supply disruption; Brent $150-200 → demand destruction could offset substitution gains. 2022 counter-evidence: coal producers posted records despite recession fears.
2. **China domestic ramp**: Record 4.83B tonnes in 2025; 1,200+ GW at only 51% utilization; seaborne imports fell ~14%. But Japan/Korea/SE Asia — not China — are the primary switching markets.
3. **Indonesia export policy**: Production cuts + export tax are price-supportive but create physical delivery risk.
4. **Nuclear restarts competing**: Japan's Kashiwazaki-Kariwa restarting; over 12+ months some coal demand redirects to nuclear.
5. **ESG structural illiquidity**: 45+ insurers with coal exclusion → persistent discount, but also outsized price impact from any incremental buying.
