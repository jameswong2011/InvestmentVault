## Gold: Paradox in the Iran Crisis

Gold's performance has defied safe-haven orthodoxy during the Iran war. After hitting $5,595/oz in January 2026, gold fell ~20% including a $2T flash crash on March 23. The transmission mechanism: oil-driven inflation → hawkish Fed holding at 3.5-3.75% → stronger dollar → pressure on non-yielding assets → margin calls → forced liquidation. This mirrors the early stages of 1970s oil shocks, when gold initially sold off before ultimately rallying dramatically.

- **Central bank floor**: ~755-850 tonnes expected purchases in 2026
- **JP Morgan target**: $6,300/oz by year-end
- **Deutsche Bank target**: $6,000/oz
- **Historical pattern**: Gold works when inflation outpaces rate hikes (1979) but fails under aggressive tightening (2022, gold -0.8%)

## Silver: Structural Supply Deficit

Silver's dual industrial/monetary nature creates unique dynamics: safe-haven buying versus demand destruction fears for its 59% industrial use (solar PV alone = 29% of industrial demand).

- **7th consecutive year of supply deficit** (-148.9 Moz in 2024)
- **70% produced as byproduct** — structurally constrained mine supply
- **AI/data center catalyst**: Electricity demand from data centers is a stealth silver driver via solar PV consumption

## Key Considerations

- **Physical vs. Miners**: Physical-backed ETFs track spot directly with minimal tracking error. Miners offer operational leverage (amplified gains/losses) but introduce company-specific risks.
- **Tax treatment**: Physical gold/silver ETFs are taxed as **collectibles** (28% max rate on long-term gains vs 20% for equities). Mining ETFs are taxed as regular equities.
- **Leveraged products** (2x/3x): Trading instruments only — daily rebalancing causes decay; not buy-and-hold.
- **Best institutional choices**: GLDM/IAU (gold, lowest cost) and SLV (silver, most liquid).
