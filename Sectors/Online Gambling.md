# Online Gambling

## Active Theses
- [[Theses/KAMBI - Kambi Group]] — Kambi Group (B2B sportsbook platform / modular pivot / Tzeract AI trading moat / FDJ "boomerang" vertical integration thesis)

## Key industry questions
- **Does vertical integration dominate B2B?** DraftKings/SBTech set the "own the stack" narrative in 2020-21, yet FDJ UNITED's 2025 return to Kambi via Odds Feed+ signals a modular hybrid model — which layer is the durable moat (turnkey vs. components vs. data)?
- **Is the FanDuel/DraftKings US duopoly (~78% GGR) defensible?** Kalshi/Polymarket prediction markets at $22B / $15B valuations, CFTC federal preemption, and Gen Z "experience economy" shifts are stealing the 2026 narrative — cannibalisation vs. adjacent TAM.
- **How much tax escalation do operators absorb before structural margin impairment?** UK RGD 21%→40% (April 2026), GBD 15%→25% (April 2027), Illinois per-bet $0.25-$0.50 fee, Colombia deposit tax, Netherlands deposit limits — 90% pass-through assumption vs. demand destruction.
- **Is iGaming (+27% YoY at Flutter) the growth engine now, not sports betting (+10%)?** Only 8 US states legal iGaming vs. 38 sports betting, yet 3-5× the revenue per state — regulatory expansion asymmetry.
- **Does AI-driven odds trading build a liquidity flywheel moat or commoditise pricing?** Kambi Tzeract prices 50% of network bets on €17B+ liquidity; Sportradar VAIX processes 60M daily transactions — network effects vs. model convergence.

## Industry history

**Pre-internet foundations (1960s-1993).** Licensed physical bookmaking consolidated in the UK (Ladbrokes 1886 / William Hill 1934 / Coral 1926), Italy (Totocalcio), and Nevada (post-1949). Antigua & Barbuda and Gibraltar positioned as offshore gaming hubs for tax reasons. US sports betting restricted to Nevada under PASPA (1992). Lotteries and racing remained national champions in most European markets.

**Offshore birth of iGaming (1994-2000).** Antigua & Barbuda's Free Trade & Processing Zone Act (1994) issued the first offshore online gaming licences. Microgaming launched the first functional online casino software (1994). Intertops took the first online sports bet on 17 January 1996 (Tottenham v. Hereford, $50 stake). Online gambling websites grew from 15 (1996) to 200 (1997). bet365 founded 2000 in Stoke-on-Trent by the Coates family — built in-house platform, scaled globally from private cash flow (still private, £4B FY25 revenue).

**Poker boom and regulatory whiplash (2001-2011).** PokerStars (2001), Full Tilt (2004), Absolute Poker drove the televised poker boom. PartyGaming IPO (2005, London) was UK's largest IPO in five years, valuing it at £5B — briefly a FTSE 100 constituent. Congress passed the Unlawful Internet Gambling Enforcement Act (UIGEA) on 13 October 2006 as a port-security bill rider — payment processors banned from handling online gambling transactions, causing PartyGaming and 888 to exit US overnight (PartyGaming stock fell ~60% on the news). PokerStars and Full Tilt stayed, using workaround payment structures. DOJ unsealed indictments against PokerStars / Full Tilt / Absolute on 15 April 2011 ("Black Friday") — Full Tilt revealed as $300M Ponzi-style shortfall; founders charged with bank fraud and money laundering. PokerStars eventually settled, acquired Full Tilt's assets. US online poker retreated to single-state-siloed markets (NJ/NV/DE 2013, PA 2019, MI 2021).

**DFS grey zone and Paddy Power scale-up (2009-2017).** FanDuel founded 2009, DraftKings founded 2012 — used UIGEA "fantasy sports" skill-game carve-out to aggregate millions of US users. State-by-state DFS legalisation accelerated post-2016. Paddy Power merged with Betfair (February 2016) to form Paddy Power Betfair plc — combined market cap ~£7B.

**PASPA strike-down and US gold rush (2018-2020).** Murphy v. NCAA (14 May 2018, 6-3 decision) struck down PASPA on Tenth Amendment anti-commandeering grounds. First legal post-PASPA bet taken in Delaware and New Jersey within weeks. Paddy Power Betfair acquired FanDuel for $158M (May 2018). GVC Holdings acquired Ladbrokes Coral for £4B (March 2018), formed BetMGM 50/50 JV with MGM Resorts (July 2018). Paddy Power Betfair rebranded as Flutter (March 2019); announced $6.95B Stars Group merger (October 2019, completed May 2020) — bringing PokerStars and Sky Betting & Gaming under one roof and creating the world's largest online gambling company. Eldorado + Caesars $8.5B merger completed July 2020. DraftKings completed SPAC listing and SBTech acquisition (April 2020) at a $3.3B combined valuation — the vertical integration watershed.

**Vertical integration wave and consolidation (2020-2023).** DraftKings migrated off Kambi to SBTech (mid-to-late 2021). Caesars acquired William Hill for £2.9B (April 2021), integrated the US operations, sold non-US assets to 888 for £1.95B (July 2022). GVC rebranded to Entain (December 2020); MGM made $11B takeover bid for Entain (January 2021, rejected). Fanatics launched sportsbook, acquired PointsBet US for $225M (August 2023). Penn Entertainment acquired Barstool (2020) for ~$551M, sold it back to Dave Portnoy for $1 (August 2023), signed $2B 10-year ESPN Bet licensing deal (August 2023).

**Modular pivot and regulatory escalation (2024-2026).** DraftKings acquired Jackpocket (lottery courier) for $750M (February 2024, closed May 2024). 888 rebranded as Evoke (March 2024). FDJ UNITED acquired Kindred for €2.45B (Q4 2024), returned to Kambi for Odds Feed+ (November 2025 expansion across Unibet/32Red/Parions) — validating the "modular boomerang" thesis. Brazil regulated market launched 1 January 2025 (66+ licensed operators, 12% GGR tax + player winnings tax). Penn and ESPN mutually terminated early (December 2025); DraftKings signed as ESPN's exclusive sportsbook (1 December 2025). UK Budget 2025 raised Remote Gaming Duty from 21% to 40% (April 2026) and General Betting Duty from 15% to 25% (April 2027) — industry-wide £810M/year initial fiscal impact, up to £1.16B by 2030/31. Kalshi ($22B valuation) and Polymarket ($15B) emerged as prediction-market-based sports-betting competitors with CFTC federal preemption. Multiple states (Indiana, Maine, California, Connecticut) banned sweepstakes casinos in 2025-2026. BetMGM reached first full-year adjusted EBITDA profitability ($220M, FY25) — industry maturation inflection.

**Structure: US duopoly, European oligopoly, fragmented emerging markets.** Global online GGR reached ~$117.5B (2025), projected to ~$186.6B by 2029 (~11% CAGR). Market structure varies dramatically by geography.

**Build vs. buy has settled into hybrid modular.** The 2020-2021 DraftKings vertical integration narrative catalysed market expectations that all scaled operators would own their full stack. Five years later, the evidence is mixed: DraftKings/SBTech and Flutter/Sky Bet/Global Platform validated in-housing for the top two, but mid-tier operators (Hard Rock Digital, Rush Street, Bally's, OLG, ATG) retain Kambi or white-label providers, and even vertically integrated operators (FDJ UNITED, Hard Rock, Superbet, Coolbet, ComeOn) buy best-in-class components (Odds Feed+, Tzeract trading) from specialists. Parallels to Stripe/Adyen in payments and AWS in compute — operators buy the layers they can't differentiate on.

**Pricing power directional shifts:**
- **Operators → Platform providers (weakening for B2B platforms):** Revenue-share rates compressed from 20-25% (2015) to 10-12% (2025) as scaled operators consolidated leverage.
- **Platform providers → Operators (strengthening via modular):** Odds Feed+ priced on subscription/fixed-fee basis, de-linking from operator GGR volatility and capturing margin from operators' trading-margin expansion (10.0%→10.8% Kambi network FY25).
- **Operators → Data providers (mixed):** League exclusivity (NFL/Genius, MLB/Sportradar) enables price escalation; NBA's non-exclusive Sportradar+Genius dual-feed caps upside.
- **Operators → Consumers (strengthening in parlays):** Same-game-parlay dominance drove operator hold from 6-7% (2021) to 9-11% (2025). In NJ, parlays = 32% of handle but 65% of revenue. SGPs now ~35-40% of GGR, up from <20% in 2021.
- **Operators → Advertisers (early stage):** FanDuel Sports Network rebrand from Bally's (Oct 2024), ESPN-DraftKings integration, digital retargeting at scale — embedding sportsbook inventory into media rights.

**Market-share movement rationales:**
- **FanDuel's lead over DraftKings** was not inevitable — FanDuel's parlay product ("Same Game Parlay" branding, launched ahead of DraftKings in 2019) and NY retention post-51% tax rate proved superior cohort economics. DraftKings narrowed the gap 2023-2025 via SBTech integration maturing and Jackpocket lottery cross-sell.
- **BetMGM losing share to DK/FD** stemmed from later technology maturity (Entain's platform migration from Playtech to OpenBet to in-house took longer); turnaround in 2025 driven by iGaming (where MGM's Vegas-to-mobile cross-sell was always stronger) rather than sports betting.
- **PointsBet/Barstool/ESPN Bet failures** demonstrated that media brand + sportsbook stack does not beat duopoly scale economics. Penn lost ~$1.5B on Barstool + ESPN Bet combined (2020-2025).
- **Prediction markets encroaching** on the bottom of the funnel — Kalshi's $25M fee revenue in four days during March Madness 2026 and DKNG stock -32% YTD reflect direct attention/wallet diversion.

## Product level analysis

**Sports betting platforms (turnkey)** provide end-to-end sportsbook infrastructure: odds compilation, trading, risk management, player wallet, regulatory compliance, UI. Analogous to "full Shopify."
- **Kambi Turnkey Sportsbook** (B2B, ~40+ partners, 50+ markets) — odds from live liquidity pool of €17B+; Bet Builder (native SGP, 22% pre-match bets at 2022 WC, 31% at Euro 2024, 57% at 2024 Nations League finals, ~50% at Super Bowl LIX).
- **DraftKings Sportsbook** (in-house SBTech platform post-2021) — 34% US share; iOS/Android apps + desktop; integrated iGaming (Golden Nugget); Same-Game Parlay Plus (multi-sport cross-parlay).
- **FanDuel Sportsbook** (Flutter Global Platform + SBG tech stack) — 44% US share, 39.6% handle; proprietary trading via FanDuel Trading Group (FTG); integrated Daily Fantasy Sports flywheel (legacy UIGEA-exempt user base that converted to sportsbook post-PASPA).
- **bet365 Platform** (wholly proprietary, Stoke-built) — live streaming, in-play betting leadership (pioneered cash-out and in-play as default betting mode); ~70% of volume in-play.
- **SBTech/DraftKings** and **OpenBet (Endeavor/IMG)** are the remaining enterprise-grade turnkey platforms after Kambi.

**Odds feed / trading services (modular)** decouple pricing from the full stack — operators with proprietary sportsbooks buy odds via API.
- **Kambi Odds Feed+** — launched 2024; single API; pre-match, live, player props, micro-markets; odds *traded on Kambi's live liquidity* (sharper vs. algorithmic). Customers: Hard Rock Digital (8 US states), FDJ UNITED, Superbet, Coolbet, ComeOn.
- **Sportradar Alpha Odds / Managed Trading Services (MTS)** — VAIX acquisition (2022) provides AI learning across 60M daily transactions and 60+ sportsbooks; Alpha Odds reported 10% profit increase avg for 60 clients (2023); 900+ operators in 120+ countries.
- **Genius Sports** — official data (NFL exclusive, NCAA, EPL); BetVision (interactive in-stream betting); expanding into trading and betting content. LiveRight odds compilation service.
- **Stats Perform, Betgenius, Huddle** — mid-tier data/odds providers.

**AI trading engines (strategic moat)** — automated pricing, repricing, risk management at scale.
- **Kambi Tzeract** — incubated in-house, named division late 2025; prices ~50% of network bets; 2026 World Cup first fully AI-traded major tournament; network-effect scales with liquidity.
- **Sportradar VAIX** — acquired 2022; AI learning across 60+ sportsbooks, 60M daily transactions; underpins MTS product.
- **Bet365 proprietary trading** — opaque; reportedly employs 2,000+ traders globally; in-play is the flagship product (>$300B/year matched).
- **FanDuel Trading Group** — internal trading desk; Flutter disclosed 2025 that FTG prices ~70% of US sports bets.

**Same-Game Parlay / Bet Builder products (margin driver)** — multi-leg parlays within a single game.
- **Kambi Bet Builder** — native integration into turnkey; 22-57% penetration at major tournaments; 88% of Super Bowl LIX parlays contained a prop.
- **FanDuel Same Game Parlay** — first-to-market branding (2019); ~45-50% of pre-match revenue.
- **DraftKings Same-Game Parlay Plus** — cross-sport/multi-game aggregation.
- **BetMGM Edit My Bet** — post-submission parlay modification (cash out individual legs).
- Aggregate parlay hold ~20% vs. ~5% straight bet hold, a 4× margin uplift that explains the industry-wide move from 6-7% hold (2021) to 9-11% (2025).

**iGaming / online casino platforms**
- **Evolution AB (EVO.ST)** — dominant live casino supplier (~45-60% global share by various estimates); live dealer streaming of Blackjack, Roulette, Baccarat plus proprietary game shows (Crazy Time, Monopoly Live, Lightning Roulette, Deal or No Deal). Acquired NetEnt (slots), Ezugi (emerging markets), Red Tiger, Big Time Gaming (Megaways), Nolimit City. 30+ studios globally; 110+ game releases in 2025.
- **Playtech (PTEC.L)** — #2 live casino (~15-30%); full-stack (slots, live, platform); operator relationships with William Hill, BGO.
- **Pragmatic Play** — classic table + live (~25% live share); Megaways licensing; emerging markets focus.
- **Light & Wonder, IGT, Scientific Games (SGHC spinoff), Aristocrat** — slots-heavy RGS (remote game server) providers; content licensed to operator casino apps.

**Lottery courier / iLottery**
- **Jackpocket (DraftKings)** — $750M acquisition closed May 2024; digital lottery ticket courier in 18 states; regulatory arbitrage under state lottery authority (not sports-betting licences).
- **theLotter, Lotto.com** — smaller courier competitors; state-licensed models.
- **IGT, Intralot** — traditional B2G lottery technology providers; Bally's considering Intralot bundle (2025-2026).

**Prediction markets / event contracts (new vertical)**
- **Kalshi** — CFTC-designated Contract Market (DCM); $1-1.5B revenue run-rate; $22B valuation (Coatue led); fee model on binary contract volume. Sports accounted for 85%+ of volume during March Madness 2026; $25M fee revenue in four-day window.
- **Polymarket** — crypto-native (blockchain settlement in USDC); $15B valuation ($400M raise in progress); opened US market under CFTC framework early 2026.
- **DraftKings Predictions** (via Railbird Technologies acquisition, Oct 2025) — federally regulated event-contracts app launched Dec 2025.
- **Fanatics Markets** — standalone event-contract app via Paragon Global Markets (CFTC introducing broker); combo builder launched April 2026.
- **Robinhood, Crypto.com** — embedded event contracts within existing trading apps.

**Player Account Management (PAM)** — regulatory compliance substrate covering KYC, AML, responsible gambling, payment orchestration, limit setting, self-exclusion.
- In-house (Flutter, DraftKings, bet365) for scaled operators.
- **OMEGA Systems** (acquired by Kambi 2025) — unlocks casino-centric Nevada/regulated markets.
- **GiG, EveryMatrix, Pronet Gaming** — mid-tier B2B PAM suppliers.

**Crypto / GambleFi**
- Crypto casinos ~17% of global iGaming bets (up from near-zero in 2020); **Stake.com** (private, >$3B rev est) the dominant crypto-native operator.
- Stablecoin (USDT/USDC) payments rising — eliminate FX volatility, fast settlement, unbanked addressability.
- GambleFi protocols (Rollbit, Decentral Games) use liquidity-pool / token-stake economics.

**Strategic patterns:**
1. **Scale + brand consolidation in US (2020-2023)** — Eldorado/Caesars/William Hill, Penn/Barstool, Fanatics/PointsBet — driven by state-by-state licence aggregation post-PASPA.
2. **Cross-Atlantic platform roll-ups (2016-2020)** — Flutter, Entain, 888 — targeted geography-diversification and liquidity pooling.
3. **Content supplier consolidation (2019-2024)** — Evolution rolled up slots/live-casino suppliers to create category oligopoly.
4. **Vertical integration (2020-2021)** — DraftKings/SBTech set template; Flutter/SBG Global Platform, Caesars/William Hill tech followed; BetMGM migrated to Entain in-house.
5. **Modular re-entry (2024-2026)** — FDJ UNITED/Kambi boomerang, DraftKings/Jackpocket, BetMGM's OpenBet-to-in-house migration completing — operators buying components rather than rebuilding from scratch.
6. **Prediction-market positioning (2025-2026)** — DraftKings/Railbird, Fanatics/Paragon — operators racing to own CFTC-regulated event-contract distribution ahead of Kalshi/Polymarket scaling.

**New entrants and disruption:**

- **Kalshi (founded 2019, Y Combinator / Sequoia / Coatue)** — CFTC-regulated prediction market exchange; $22B valuation April 2026; sports contracts launched late 2025 exploiting CFTC federal preemption to offer "sports event contracts" in states without sports-betting licences. Existential distribution threat: bypasses state-by-state licensing frameworks that are the US sportsbooks' competitive moat.
- **Polymarket (founded 2020, Peter Thiel / 1confirmation)** — crypto-native (Polygon blockchain); $15B valuation April 2026; opened US via Chapter 40 CFTC designation January 2026.
- **Stake.com (private, Costa Rica-based)** — crypto casino/sportsbook; estimated $3B+ revenue; unregulated in most of the world but dominant in the crypto-first demographic; Stake.us sweepstakes version under US state-level crackdown.
- **VGW (Chumba, LuckyLand)** — sweepstakes casino model ("no purchase necessary" sweeps coin); estimated $2B+ revenue but Indiana (July 2026), Maine, California, Connecticut bans signalling regulatory closure; class-action litigation >100 cases filed 2025.
- **Robinhood, Crypto.com event contracts** — embedded prediction market functionality within existing trading apps; distribution leverage but no sports-betting infrastructure depth.
- **theScore Bet (Penn/Rush Street)** — regional challenger brand; Penn migrated ESPN Bet users post-termination.
- **Betsson, Kaizen Gaming (Betano), Superbet** — European mid-cap operators scaling into LatAm (Brazil launch Jan 2025); Kaizen at €3B+ revenue and accelerating.
- **African challengers (Betway via Super Group, SportyBet, Premier Bet)** — emerging-markets growth vector outside US/EU regulatory burden.

**Net pricing-power effect for incumbents:** Prediction markets and sweepstakes casinos apply pressure on the bottom 30% of the TAM (casual / entertainment-driven users); regulated operators retain the sharp bettors via higher limits, parlay depth, and better VIP treatment. Pass-through pricing on UK/IL tax increases suggests operators still hold some pricing power — but demand destruction estimates of 15-20% point to asymmetric incidence on the lower-value cohort.

## Macro shifts

**Regulatory tax escalation (2024-2027).** The defining macro headwind. The UK, Illinois, Colombia, Netherlands, and incoming Brazilian tax changes collectively remove €1.5-2B of annualised EBITDA from the industry over 2026-2028:

| Jurisdiction | Change | Effective | Annualised industry cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| UK | Remote Gaming Duty 21% → 40% | April 2026 | £810M Yr1, £1.16B by 2030/31 |
| UK | General Betting Duty 15% → 25% (ex-racing) | April 2027 | Estimated £250-350M |
| Illinois | $0.25/$0.50 per-bet tax (first 20M / thereafter) | July 2025 | DKNG +$79M tax burden (5.4% of EBITDA); FanDuel +$86M (2% of EBITDA); 15% volume drop |
| Illinois | Progressive tax to 40% (prior year) | 2024 | Applies to largest sportsbooks |
| Colombia | Deposit tax introduced | 2025 | €3.6M Kambi-attributable, industry ~€100M |
| Netherlands | Deposit limits + €10k/month caps | 2024-2025 | Flutter/Kindred materially impacted |
| Brazil | 12% GGR + 15% on winnings >BRL 2,824 | Jan 2025 | 36% total effective rate |

Operator response: pass-through to consumers (FanDuel/DraftKings $0.50 Illinois surcharge); exit marginal markets (bet365 exited China grey market; Super Group exited US iGaming 2024); M&A acceleration (Bally's/Evoke bid, Brazil consolidation wave). Pass-through estimated at ~90% but with ~15-20% demand destruction.

**Responsible gambling regulatory expansion.** UK Gambling Commission rolling out frictionless financial risk assessments (affordability checks) — "light touch" at moderate spend, "enhanced" at higher losses; credit cards banned for gambling; reverse withdrawals banned; new online slot rules. UK Premier League front-of-shirt gambling sponsorship ban from 2026/27 season; "whistle-to-whistle" TV advertising ban sustained. Australia and Netherlands implementing deposit limit regimes. US: state-level responsible gambling requirements, NFL prop-bet-restriction memo (Nov 2025), NBA gambling policy revisions, Senate Blumenthal inquiry into league-operator partnerships (May 2026 deadline).

**AI and automation as structural driver, not risk.** Transformer-based pricing engines (Kambi Tzeract, Sportradar Alpha Odds + VAIX, Flutter FTG, bet365 proprietary) automate trading workflows, faster repricing, higher bet acceptance, longer market uptime. Operator trading margin expansion (Kambi network 10.0%→10.8% FY25) directly attributable. But pricing competition between platform providers compresses take rates — Kambi revenue-share 20-25% (2015) to 10-12% (2025). AI-driven responsible-gambling detection (session length, bet frequency, deposit patterns) replacing reactive models — regulatory tailwind for scaled operators with deeper ML capabilities.

**Prediction markets federal preemption as paradigm shift.** CFTC "swaps" designation (Q1 2026) places Kalshi/Polymarket under exclusive federal jurisdiction, bypassing state-by-state gambling licensing regimes. Senate bills (Blumenthal, Schiff, Curtis) targeting event-contract sports restrictions. Ohio federal court (March 2026) ruled Kalshi amounts to gambling — contradicting CFTC framework; Nevada/Arizona issued temporary bans. Supreme Court hearing anticipated late 2026. Outcome is binary: if federal preemption holds, prediction markets become the de facto national sportsbook bypassing the state tax regimes that generate $10B+ in annual gaming tax revenue. If state gambling regulators prevail, prediction markets revert to niche political/cultural contracts.

**Demographic inflection — Gen Z over-indexes on experience.** Millennials remain dominant digital-bettor cohort (42% participation vs Gen X 28%), but Gen Z displays lower traditional sports-betting participation (36.5% vs Millennials 40%) with stronger preference for prediction-market-style event contracts, micro-betting, crypto casinos, and social/community-native gambling. 17% of Gen Z bettors place daily bets; credit card delinquencies in sports-betting states materially elevated (NY Fed study, March 2026). Implications: traditional sportsbooks must build experience-driven product (live streaming, real-time micro-markets, social wagering, gamification) to retain wallet share — or concede the next cohort to Kalshi/Polymarket/Stake.

**Payment-rail disruption.** Stablecoin (USDT/USDC) adoption rising in grey-market and crypto-native operations — faster settlement, transparent on-chain audit, borderless liquidity. Regulatory response fragmented: Brazil, EU MiCA frameworks accommodating; US sanctions and AML controls tightening. Stablecoins projected to represent majority of transactional volume on crypto gambling platforms by 2026. Traditional operators (Flutter, DraftKings) remain ACH/card-rail dependent; opening for crypto-native or crypto-enabled operators to capture cross-border flow.

**Geographic expansion: LatAm > US > APAC asymmetry.** Brazil (Jan 2025), Mexico (pending), Argentina province-by-province, Chile (2026 pending) represent the largest regulated TAM expansion wave. US state-by-state iGaming legalisation slow (8 states live; MA/IL/NY/PA active bills in 2026). APAC remains largely restricted: Philippines ended POGO regime (2024); India judicial-driven fragmentation (state-level, skill-vs-chance distinction); Japan Anti-Gambling Act amended Sept 2025 to criminalise website advertising. China/Korea/Indonesia zero-tolerance. Grey-market TAM in Asia estimated $50B+ but increasingly enforcement-closed.

## Investor heuristics

**Consensus (priced-in) narratives:**
1. **"Vertical integration is the only viable model"** — anchored on DraftKings/SBTech execution; discounts modular component value.
2. **"FanDuel + DraftKings are structural duopoly"** — 78% GGR share extrapolated to all state-expansions; implies near-zero value for tier-2 operators and B2B providers.
3. **"Prediction markets will eat regulated sports betting"** — DKNG -35% YTD, FLUT -35% 1-yr, Kalshi/Polymarket at $22B/$15B; consensus believes federal CFTC preemption holds and offshores TAM.
4. **"UK 40% RGD = permanent margin impairment"** — Entain £100M FY26 hit, bet365 profit decline, Rank £40M OP hit interpreted as structural rather than transitional.
5. **"iGaming is a mature $8B US market, not a growth vertical"** — despite 30% YoY growth (Q3 2025), the limited state count (8 legal) anchors expectations to linear regulatory expansion.
6. **"B2B is dead post-DraftKings"** — Kambi at 0.5x revenue, GAN acquired for $0.90/share, Sportradar trades at half post-IPO levels.

**Where consensus is likely wrong (non-consensus angles):**

- **The duopoly is less durable than headline share suggests.** FanDuel 44% / DraftKings 34% of GGR but only ~72% of handle in mature states like NJ — and the 28% tail includes profitable niche operators (Rush Street, Hard Rock, Caesars digital) that consensus treats as zeroes. Kalshi/Polymarket disruption cuts both ways: if federal preemption holds, the duopoly's state-licence moat depreciates; if states win, DKNG Predictions and FanDuel's future event-contract products get squeezed.
- **iGaming is the growth vertical, not sports betting.** Flutter FY25: iGaming +27% vs. sports betting +10%; BetMGM FY25: iGaming $1.83B (+24%) vs. online sports $903M (+63% — but off lower base); AGA Q3 2025 US iGaming +30% YoY. Only 8 US states legal iGaming vs. 38 sports betting — 5× the per-capita revenue in legal states. Next legalisation wave (Illinois, Massachusetts, New York, Pennsylvania expansions) could add $5-10B US iGaming GGR by 2028. Evolution AB (Stockholm, ~$20B market cap) and Playtech underappreciated relative to this trajectory.
- **Modular B2B is growing, not declining.** FDJ UNITED boomerang, Hard Rock Digital Odds Feed+ (8 US states), Superbet/Coolbet/ComeOn signings, 15 new Kambi partnerships Q4 2025-Q1 2026 — the tier-2/3 operator TAM is expanding, not contracting. Scale operators buy components (odds, trading, PAM, content) even when owning the full stack. Revenue-share compression is real but offset by modular volume growth and fixed-fee/subscription pricing.
- **AI-driven trading liquidity flywheel is genuinely moat-creating.** Kambi Tzeract prices on €17B+ pooled liquidity from 45+ operators — a dataset that standalone operators (outside Flutter/bet365) cannot replicate. Sportradar VAIX on 60M daily transactions. Operators buying algorithmically-modelled odds from smaller providers face structural pricing-accuracy disadvantage. This is less "commodity" than consensus assumes — closer to market-making on Nasdaq liquidity vs. building your own exchange.
- **UK 40% RGD consolidates to scale players, not impairs industry.** Entain / Flutter / bet365 can absorb 90% pass-through and convert UK share from mid-tier operators (Evoke, Rank, Star Sports) who lack marketing and liquidity scale to maintain margins at the new tax level. Past tax-regime changes (Italy 2018-2020, France 2020-2023, Germany 2021) all consolidated share to top-3 operators. Bally's/Intralot bid for Evoke (£225M Dec 2025) is the first visible acquisition inflection.
- **The "BetMGM turnaround" is bigger than consensus credits.** FY25 $220M EBITDA vs. -$244M FY24 = $464M YoY swing; FY26 guide $300-350M; FY27 management target $500M. Entain's 50% stake materially de-risked — yet ENT stock trades at -15% YTD (April 2026). BetMGM profitability was supposed to be 2027-2028 base case; arriving 2025 compresses Entain bear narrative.
- **Prediction-market competitive threat is distribution, not product.** Kalshi unit economics lag sportsbooks (no parlay margin; thin fee on binary volume; regulatory overhang). Critical question: can Kalshi monetise sports-event-contract volume at parlay-hold-equivalent margins? If not, the existential threat to DraftKings/Flutter is overstated. More likely: regulated sportsbooks and prediction markets converge — DraftKings Predictions + Fanatics Markets already hedging.
- **Sweepstakes and crypto casino TAM loss is a tailwind for regulated operators.** California / Indiana / Maine bans on VGW and Stake.us remove $1-2B of US annual wagering volume from grey-market alternatives — direct conversion opportunity for FanDuel/DraftKings/BetMGM iGaming in those states. Not consensus-priced.
